Difficulty

Its charged Psychic moves can quickly knock out unprepared Pokémon, so coordination and move choice are critical. In larger groups, raw DPS dominates, but dodging certain high-damage attacks can save your team for the long fight.

Best Moveset

  • Fast: Shadow Claw, Psycho Cut, Metal Claw
  • Charged: Dark Pulse, Iron Head, Future Sight,

Dangerous Moves: Outrage is extremely dangerous. Wild Charge hits very hard.

Necrozma is more dangerous when it uses Electric-type charged moves like Wild Charge or Fusion Bolt, especially against Water- or Flying-type counters.

CP Ranges

Understanding CP ranges helps trainers evaluate the strength of Necrozma when caught from raids.

  • Perfect (L20): 2,217-2,307 CP
  • Perfect (L20): 2,018-2,104 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,522-2,630 CP
  • Boosted by: Windy

Keep in mind that weather-boosted Necrozma is significantly harder to defeat, so always prepare strong Ice, Dragon, Fairy, or Ground-type counters.

Weather Boost Effects

  • Sunny weather : boosts Fire type attackers.
  • Windy weather : boosts Ghost and Dark attackers.

Revive & Potion Cost

  • Average revives used: 8–14
  • Average potions used: 10–18
  • Dodging significantly reduces resource usage
  • Using Mega Pokémon reduces total relobbies

Common Raid Mistakes

  • Not bringing a Mega Pokémon for team boosts
  • Using weather-boosted Pokémon of the wrong type
  • Relobbying too late and losing damage time
  • Ignoring dodging during hard-hitting moves

Best Team Compositions

Small Group (3–4 Trainers)

With strong Ice, Dragon, or Ground-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Necrozma can be defeated by 3 to 4 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (5–7 Trainers)

A group of 5 to 7 trainers with decent counters should have no trouble defeating Necrozma.

Large Group (8+ Trainers)

Necrozma becomes very easy with a large group, but avoid overfilling the lobby to save raid passes.

Summary

To defeat Necrozma efficiently, bring high-level Dark, Ghost, or Bug Pokémon, and coordinate shields for multi-bar attacks. Small teams must prioritize dodging and timing, while large groups focus on raw DPS and type advantage. Weather-boosted counters can shorten the fight and maximize rewards.
