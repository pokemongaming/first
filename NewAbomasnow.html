My Real Mega Abomasnow Raid Experience

In my recent Mega Abomasnow raid, I went in with a Fire-heavy team including Mega Blaziken and Blacephalon. Initially, I underestimated how often Abomasnow used Avalanche, which caused my first team to faint quickly. After adjusting, I focused more on dodging charged attacks instead of pure damage. This significantly improved survival time and allowed us to finish the raid with around 50 seconds remaining. From this experience, I learned that while Fire-types are the best counters, timing dodges correctly is equally important to avoid sudden knockouts.

Boss Moves

Possible attacks

Mega Abomasnow has access to fast moves like Dragon Breath, Steel Wing, and Dragon Tail, both of which can pressure players differently. Dragon Breath deals extremely high fast-move damage, making it dangerous for fragile attackers, while Dragon Tail is weaker but charges energy faster for frequent charged attacks.

Its charged moves typically include Hyper Beam, Thunder Punch, Dragon Claw, Hurricane, Outrage, and Superpower. Hyper Bomb is particularly threatening due to its high base power and ability to hit Ice-type counters hard. Outrage, on the other hand, charges quickly and can spam damage, forcing players to dodge frequently to maintain survivability.

To counter these moves effectively, players should use Pokémon that either resist Dragon-type damage or can deal heavy damage quickly before being knocked out. Ice-types are especially effective because they resist Dragon moves and hit back with super-effective damage. Rock-types can also perform well, but they must be used carefully due to their vulnerability to Dragon-type attacks.

Moveset Analysis

Abomasnow uses fast Dragon-type moves (Dragon Breath, Dragon Tail) and strong charged moves (Dragon Claw, Outrage). Ice-types resist Dragon attacks, reducing damage taken. Dragon-types also resist Dragon but may lack DPS; consider weather-boosted Ice moves for best efficiency.


Strategy

Mega Abomasnow is considered a moderately difficult Tier 6 raid boss due to its high attack stat and access to fast, high-damage moves. The key to defeating it efficiently lies in exploiting its multiple weaknesses while maintaining strong team coordination. Ice and Rock-type attackers are the most reliable choices, as they not only deal super-effective damage but also offer good survivability against its Dragon-type moves.

For beginners, the safest approach is to join larger groups of 5 or more trainers and focus on using recommended counters without worrying too much about optimization. Intermediate players should aim to build a balanced team with proper movesets and consider dodging charged attacks to extend their Pokémon’s lifespan. Advanced players can attempt smaller group raids or even duo challenges by maximizing DPS, using Mega Evolutions strategically, and coordinating boosts with teammates.

Weather conditions also play an important role in this raid. Windy weather boosts Abomasnow Dragon-type moves, while Rainy weather enhances its Flying-type attacks. In both cases, the boss becomes more dangerous, so players should be prepared with stronger counters or additional trainers. Conversely, neutral weather conditions make the raid significantly easier to manage.

Dodging is another important mechanic to consider. While it reduces your overall DPS slightly, it can significantly increase survivability, especially against Draco Meteor. Players using glass cannon attackers like Shadow Pokémon should prioritize dodging to maintain consistent damage output throughout the battle.

Overall, success in this raid depends on preparation, proper team composition, and understanding the boss’s moveset. With the right strategy and counters, Mega Abomasnow can be defeated efficiently even with a relatively small group of skilled trainers.

Mega Raid Mechanics

Mega Raids work differently from standard raids. Instead of catching the Mega form directly, players earn Mega Energy after defeating the boss. This energy is then used to Mega Evolve Victreebel. The faster the raid is completed, the more Mega Energy you receive. This makes using strong counters and coordinated teams extremely important.

Mega Abomasnow vs Other Dragon Raid Bosses

Compared to other Dragon-type raid bosses like Mega Garchomp, Mega Abomasnow is more attack-focused but less bulky. This makes it easier to defeat but more punishing if you fail to dodge charged moves.

Why Raid Mega Abomasnow?

Raiding Mega Abomasnow allows players to collect Mega Energy, which is essential for Mega Evolution. Mega Evolutions provide powerful attack boosts to your team in raids and are useful for both PvE and events. Additionally, players have the chance to catch a high-IV or shiny Abomasnow, making this raid valuable for collectors and competitive players alike.

Best Team Composition

A strong team should focus on Ice and Rock-type Pokémon, as they deal the most damage to Mega Abomasnow. Mega Evolutions such as Mega Abomasnow can boost the entire team's Ice-type attacks. Avoid using Grass, Water, Electric-type Pokémon, as they deal reduced damage and may faint quickly against the boss.

Counter Strategy Breakdown

Ice-type Pokémon are the most effective due to double weakness. Rock-types provide both offense and survivability, while Dragon-types offer strong burst damage. Using Shadow Pokémon increases damage output significantly, making raids faster and more efficient, especially in smaller groups.

Real Raid Experience

In real battles, Mega Abomasnow often defeats players through consistent damage rather than sudden attacks. Fragile Pokémon tend to faint quickly, reducing overall performance.

Balanced attackers like Mamoswine or Aurorus perform better because they survive longer and deal steady damage throughout the raid.

My Personal Raid Tip

I completed this raid with 8 trainers. We finished with around 60 seconds remaining. Initially, we failed once due to using wrong counters like Water-types. After switching to Ice-types, the raid became significantly easier. From experience, the biggest advantage in this raid comes from using a Mega Evolution early in the battle. Starting with a Mega Ice-type like Mega Abomasnow or Mega Glalie boosts not only your damage but also your teammates’ attacks, making the raid significantly faster. Another key tip is to avoid over-relying on glass cannon Pokémon unless you are confident in dodging. Survivability matters more than raw DPS in smaller groups, as fainting frequently can reduce your overall damage output.

❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using wrong types
    Avoid using fragile Fire or Grass attackers that will faint quickly to Mega Abomasnow’s strong Dragon- and Flying-type moves.
  • Ignoring weather boost
    Windy and Rainy weather both buff the boss, making the raid harder than expected.
  • Not using Mega Evolution
    Missing out on Mega Boost reduces total team DPS significantly.
  • No dodging
    Taking full damage from Outrage can wipe out your team quickly.
  • Underestimating group size
    Trying to duo or trio without optimized counters often leads to failure.
