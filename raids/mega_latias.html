Mega Latias Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Mega Latias is a powerful Dragon and Psychic-type Mega Raid Boss in Pokémon GO. It is known for its extremely high bulk and defensive stats, making it one of the toughest Mega Raids in the game. Because of its typing, Mega Latias is weak to several types including Dragon, Dark, Ghost, Fairy, Ice, and Bug attacks.

This Mega Latias raid guide explains everything trainers need to know to defeat it, including the best counters, weaknesses, catch CP ranges, weather boosts, and effective raid strategies for small and large raid groups.

Introduction

Mega Latias is the Mega Evolution of Latias and one of the most challenging Mega Raid bosses available in Pokémon GO. With its Dragon and Psychic typing combined with extremely high defensive stats, Mega Latias can survive longer than most other Mega Raid bosses.

Because of its bulk, defeating Mega Latias usually requires a coordinated raid group with strong counters. Dragon-type attackers are typically the most effective, although Dark, Ghost, Fairy, and Ice-type Pokémon can also perform very well in this raid battle.

This guide will help trainers prepare the best team to defeat Mega Latias efficiently. You will learn about its weaknesses, the strongest counters, raid strategies, and how to maximize your chances of catching Latias after the raid.

Mega Latias is a Dragon / Psychic-type Pokémon. When Mega Evolved, it becomes one of the bulkiest Mega Pokémon in the game and provides damage bonuses to Dragon-type and Psychic-type Pokémon in raids.

Mega Latias is useful because:

🌀 Mega Latias Moveset

Latias common fast moves are Zen Headbutt (reliable damage) or Dragon Breath (fast energy generation) or Charm. Charged moves include Thunder (possible coverage moves) or Aura Sphere or Psychic (heavy STAB psychic damage) and Outrage (very strong charged move). Elite moves include Mist Ball (possible coverage moves).

Pro tip: Mega Latias can quickly cycle through fast moves, making dodging and shield timing crucial. Use resilient Dragon or Dark counters to absorb Psychic damage while dealing consistent DPS

🎯 Rewards & Catch CP

In Mega Raids, you catch a normal Latias and also earn Mega Energy.

  • Perfect (L20): 2,644-2,743 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 3,305-3,430 CP
  • Boosted by: Windy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

🌦️ Weather Boost

Mega Latias gets a weather boost in:

  • Windy → boosts Dragon and Psychic

Your counters get boosted in:

  • Snow → boosts Ice
  • Windy → boosts Dragon
  • Cloudy → boosts Fairy
  • Fog → boosts Dark and Ghost

Dragon counters like Mega Rayquaza and Eternatus deal massive damage due to STAB and type advantage, while Ghost, Dark, Ice, and Fairy types cover Mega Latias’s diverse weaknesses.

💡 Best Strategy to Defeat Mega Latias

  1. Start with your highest Dragon DPS (like Mega Rayquaza or Eternatus).
  2. Dodge charged moves like Outrage and Psychic when possible to extend your counters’ longevity.
  3. Use Ghost and Dark counters to exploit Psychic resistances.
  4. If your team faints, rejoin quickly to maximize total DPS within the time limit.

Can Mega Latias Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes, trainers can encounter a Shiny Latias after defeating Mega Latias in a raid. While Mega Pokémon themselves cannot be caught, players will encounter the normal Latias after the battle, which has a chance of being shiny.

Shiny Latias has a golden yellow body instead of the usual red color seen in the normal version. This unique appearance makes it highly sought after by Pokémon collectors.

Mega Raids generally have higher shiny rates compared to wild encounters, so participating in multiple Mega Latias raids increases the chances of obtaining a shiny version.

📌 Is Mega Latias Worth Raiding?

Mega Latias is mainly worth raiding for its Mega Pokédex entry and for collectors. It is very bulky but not the best damage-dealing Mega Pokémon compared to others.
