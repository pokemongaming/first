Introduction

Mega Banette is the Mega Evolution of Banette and one of the more challenging Ghost-type Mega Raid bosses in Pokémon GO. It has access to fast and charged Ghost-type moves that can quickly damage unprepared counters. Its Mega Evolution increases its attack significantly, so trainers must plan carefully to defeat it efficiently.

Fortunately, Mega Banette has clear weaknesses against Dark and Ghost-type moves, which can help trainers defeat it faster with a smaller group. This guide will cover everything you need to know to prepare for a Mega Banette raid, including counters, movesets, CP ranges, shiny chances, and recommended strategies.

🧬 About Mega Banette in Pokémon GO

Mega Banette is a Ghost-type Pokémon. When Mega Evolved, it becomes one of the strongest Ghost-type attackers in the game and provides damage bonuses to Ghost-type Pokémon in raids.

Mega Banette is useful because:

  • It boosts Ghost-type attackers
  • It is one of the highest DPS Ghost-type Mega Pokémon
  • It is excellent against Psychic and Ghost-type raid bosses

Weaknesses & resistances

CategoryDetails
❌ Weak To: Ghost • Dark
✅ Resistant / Immune To: Poison • Bug • Normal • Fighting

Notes: Ghost-types hit each other super effectively, this raid often turns into a damage race where both sides faint Pokémon quickly. It also resists Normal and Fighting, so avoid those types entirely.

Common Raid Mistakes

Avoid using fragile Water or Ice attackers—they'll often be outpaced by Banette's high damage. Also be cautious with Ghost types if Banette runs Crunch.

Suggested Team Compositions

Small Group (3-4 Trainers)

With strong Dark-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Mega Banette can be defeated by 3 to 4 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (5-7 Trainers)

A group of 5 to 7 trainers with decent counters should have no trouble defeating Mega Banette.

Large Group (7+ Trainers)

Mega Banette becomes very easy with a large group, but avoid overfilling the lobby to save raid passes.

Can Mega Banette Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes, players can encounter a Shiny Banette after completing a Mega Banette raid. While the Mega form itself cannot be caught, defeating the raid gives a chance to encounter regular Banette, which may be shiny.

Shiny Banette has a slightly darker gray appearance compared to the normal version. Because shiny Pokémon are rare, participating in multiple raids increases your chance of finding one.

FAQ

Can Mega Banette be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Shiny Banette may be available during specific events.

What are best moveset of Banette?

Hex as a fast attack and Shadow Ball as a charged attack are commonly recommended.

Is Mega Banette difficult to defeat?

It has extremely high attack but low defenses. Using type-advantage counters ensures it is defeated efficiently before it can overwhelm your team.

What makes Mega Banette unique?

Its possessed, stitched-together Mega form, incredible attack, and ghostly moves make Mega Banette visually striking and strategically dangerous in Max Raid Battles.

Does Mega Banette keep its Mega form after the battle?

Mega Evolution lasts only during battles where Mega is enabled; it does not persist outside combat.

What is Mega Banette’s exclusive move?

Mega Banette can use moves like Shadow Claw and Shadow Ball, dealing massive damage with high critical-hit potential in Max Raid Battles.
