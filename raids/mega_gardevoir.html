Mega Gardevoir Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Mega Gardevoir is a powerful Fairy and Psychic-type Mega Raid Boss in Pokémon GO. It is known for its extremely high attack stat and strong Fairy-type moves, which can deal heavy damage to many raid teams.

This raid guide explains everything trainers need to defeat Mega Gardevoir, including its weaknesses, best counters, movesets, catch CP ranges, weather boosts, shiny availability, and effective raid strategies for both small and large groups.

Introduction

The Mega Evolution of Gardevoir, Mega Gardevoir is one of the strongest Fairy-type raid bosses in Pokémon GO. Thanks to its Fairy and Psychic typing, it can deal significant damage with powerful moves like Charm and Dazzling Gleam.

However, Mega Gardevoir also has several important weaknesses that trainers can exploit. Steel-type Pokémon are especially effective because they resist Fairy-type moves while dealing strong super-effective damage.

By building a team of powerful Steel, Ghost, or Poison attackers, players can defeat Mega Gardevoir efficiently and earn valuable Mega Energy to evolve their own Gardevoir. This guide will help you prepare the best raid team and understand the strategies needed to win the battle.

🌀 Mega Gardevoir Moveset

Gardevoir common fast moves are Magical Leaf or Charge Beam or Confusion (Fast Psychic moves that accumulate damage quickly) or Charm. Charged moves include Shadow Ball (Optional but extremely dangerous against non-Steel Pokémon) or Psychic or Triple Axel or Dazzling Gleam (Charged moves with high potential to knock out unprepared counters). Elite moves include Synchronoise.

Most Dangerous Moves: Charm and Dazzling Gleam deal huge Fairy-type damage.

Pro tip: Bring Pokémon that resist Psychic attacks to minimize fainting risk and maintain sustained DPS.

🎯 Rewards & Catch CP

In Mega Raids, you catch a normal Gardevoir and also earn Mega Energy.

  • Perfect (L20): 2,480-2,578 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 3,100-3,223 CP

💡 Best Strategy to Defeat Mega Gardevoir

  1. Steel-types are the safest and strongest counters.
  2. Avoid using Dragon, Fighting, or Dark Pokémon.
  3. Dodge Dazzling Gleam if using Ghost-types.
  4. 2–3 strong trainers can defeat Mega Gardevoir easily.

👥 How Many Trainers Are Needed?

  • 3 strong trainers → Possible
  • 4–6 trainers → Comfortable clear
  • Low-level players → 7+ recommended

Can Mega Gardevoir Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes, trainers have a chance to encounter a Shiny Gardevoir after defeating Mega Gardevoir in a raid battle. While Mega Pokémon themselves cannot be caught directly, players will encounter the regular Gardevoir after the raid, which may appear in its shiny form.

Shiny Gardevoir has a noticeable color change compared to the normal version, featuring a lighter body and blue highlights instead of green. Because shiny Pokémon are rare, many trainers participate in multiple Mega raids to increase their chances of finding one.

Once obtained, a shiny Gardevoir can also Mega Evolve, allowing players to use a shiny Mega Gardevoir in battles and raids.

📌 Is Mega Gardevoir Worth Raiding?

Yes, Mega Gardevoir is one of the best Fairy-type attackers in Pokémon GO and is extremely useful for Dragon-type, Dark-type, and Fighting-type raids.

FAQ

Can Mega Gardevoir be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Shiny Gardevoir has been released in raids or events.

What is Mega Gardevoir’s exclusive move?

Mega Gardevoir can use moves like Psychic and Dazzling Gleam, dealing massive Psychic and Fairy-type damage to opponents in Max Raid Battles.

Is Mega Gardevoir difficult to defeat?

It has high special attack but relatively low defenses. Using type-advantage Pokémon ensures efficient and safe battles.

What makes Mega Gardevoir unique?

Its elegant Mega form, combination of Psychic/Fairy typing, and powerful special moves make Mega Gardevoir visually stunning and strategically strong in Max Raid Battles.

Does Mega Gardevoir keep its Mega form after the battle?

Mega Evolution lasts only during battles where Mega is enabled; it does not persist outside combat.
