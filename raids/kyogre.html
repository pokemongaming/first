Kyogre is one of Pokémon GO’s most iconic Legendary raid bosses, known for its exceptional bulk and powerful Water-type moves. While its Water typing gives it resistance to many common attacking types, it also has specific vulnerabilities that trainers can exploit to defeat it quickly and efficiently. Simply using high-CP Pokémon isn’t enough — choosing counters that hit Kyogre’s weaknesses and understanding how weather and battle mechanics influence your team’s performance is key.

Because Kyogre resists Fire, Water, Ice, and Steel moves, counters that exploit its weak points — especially Grass and Electric types — will shine. In this guide, we break down not only the best counters, but also when and how to use them, how weather boosts affect damage output, and practical battle strategies that help you win whether you’re raiding solo or with a full lobby.

⚡ Electric-Type Counters (Top Priority)

Battle Strategy & Tactical Insights

Kyogre’s primary vulnerability is to **Electric and Grass-type attacks**. Pokémon such as Zapdos with Thunder Shock + Thunderbolt, Raikou with Thunder Shock + Wild Charge, and Roserade with Razor Leaf + Energy Ball deal excellent super-effective damage. These counters not only hit Kyogre’s weaknesses hard but also resist its Water-type charged moves, improving their survivability.

Weather plays a big role in this battle. In **Rainy weather**, Water-type moves get a boost — making Kyogre even tougher. Conversely, in **Partly Cloudy** or **Sunny/Clear** weather, Electric and Grass-type moves receive boosts, increasing your counters’ damage output significantly. Trainers should always check in-game weather before assembling their team to make the most of these conditions.

Charged moves like **Hydro Pump** and **Surf** hit hard and can quickly wear down your counters if they aren’t resistant or bulky enough. Learning to dodge these charged attacks — especially in smaller raid lobbies — helps your counters survive longer and deal more total damage. Prioritize dodging when your Pokémon’s health is low or when a charged move would otherwise knock out a key counter early in the fight.

When planning your team, lead with your highest DPS Electric and Grass counters that exploit Kyogre’s weaknesses. If these Pokémon begin to faint early, rotate in secondary attackers that still deal super-effective damage but offer greater bulk or durability. In larger raid groups, coordinating roles — such as focusing Electric counters first and then supporting with Grass types — helps speed up the clear and makes the battle smoother for everyone involved.

If you have access to Mega Evolutions, using a **Mega that supports Electric or Grass counters**, like Mega Ampharos (for Electric support) or Mega Venusaur (for Grass support), can boost your entire team’s effectiveness. Pairing these with strong attackers optimizes both damage and sustainability in the fight.

Raid Strategy

Prioritize Electric Damage

Electric-type teams are generally safer than Grass teams because they avoid Ice-type weaknesses. Stack multiple Electric attackers for consistent damage.

Watch for Blizzard

If Primal Kyogre has Blizzard, Grass-type Pokémon may faint quickly. In this scenario, Electric-type attackers become significantly more reliable.

Coordinate Primal Boosts

Primal Pokémon provide powerful team-wide bonuses. Activating a Primal Electric Pokémon during the raid can significantly increase damage output for the entire group.
