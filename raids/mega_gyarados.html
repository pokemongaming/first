Mega Gyarados Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Mega Gyarados is a powerful Water and Dark-type Mega Raid Boss in Pokémon GO. Originally evolving from Magikarp, this intimidating Pokémon becomes even more dangerous in its Mega Evolution form. Mega Gyarados has strong attack stats and access to powerful Water and Dark-type moves that can challenge unprepared trainers.

Fortunately, Mega Gyarados has several weaknesses that skilled trainers can exploit. Electric and Grass-type Pokémon are especially effective counters in this raid. In this guide, you will learn the best counters, weaknesses, catch CP ranges, shiny availability, and raid strategies needed to defeat Mega Gyarados successfully.

Introduction

Mega Gyarados is one of the most iconic Mega Evolutions in Pokémon GO. When Gyarados Mega Evolves, it gains the Dark typing in addition to its original Water type, making it a powerful and aggressive raid boss.

This dual typing gives Mega Gyarados strong resistances against several attack types, but it also leaves it vulnerable to Electric and Grass-type attacks. Using powerful Electric Pokémon can significantly reduce the raid difficulty.

Because Mega Gyarados has high attack and stamina stats, trainers should prepare strong counters before attempting this raid. With the right strategy and a well-prepared team, even small groups of trainers can defeat Mega Gyarados efficiently.

🧬 About Mega Gyarados in Pokémon GO

Mega Gyarados is a Water / Dark-type Pokémon. When Mega Evolved, it becomes much bulkier and provides damage bonuses to Water-type and Dark-type Pokémon in raids.

Mega Gyarados is useful because:

  • It boosts Water-type and Dark-type attackers
  • It is very bulky compared to many other Mega Pokémon
  • It is excellent against Psychic, Ghost, Fire, and Ground-type Pokémon

🌀 Mega Gyarados Moveset

Gyarados common fast moves are Waterfall or Dragon Breath or Bite. Charged moves include Twister or Crunch or Hydro Pump or Outrage. Elite moves include Dragon Tail or Aqua Tail or Dragon Pulse.

Pro tip: Dodge Hydro Pump if your Pokémon are fragile, and prioritize Electric types to exploit its double Water typing.

🎯 Rewards & Catch CP

In Mega Raids, you catch a normal Gyarados and also earn Mega Energy.

  • Perfect (L20): 2,597-2,695 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 3,247-3,369 CP
  • Boosted by: Rainy & Fog

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

💡 Best Strategy to Defeat Mega Gyarados

  1. Electric and Fighting types give the fastest clear times.
  2. Dodge Hydro Pump whenever possible.
  3. Avoid Psychic types — Dark moves destroy them.
  4. 3–4 strong trainers can defeat Mega Gyarados comfortably.

👥 How Many Trainers Are Needed?

  • 3 strong trainers → Possible
  • 4–6 trainers → Comfortable clear
  • Low-level players → 7+ recommended

Can Mega Gyarados Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes, trainers can encounter a Shiny Gyarados after defeating Mega Gyarados in a raid battle. While the Mega Evolution itself cannot be caught, players will encounter the regular Gyarados afterward, which has a chance to appear as a shiny Pokémon.

Shiny Gyarados is one of the most famous shiny Pokémon in the franchise. Instead of the usual blue color, shiny Gyarados appears in a striking red color that makes it highly recognizable and valuable to collectors.

Completing multiple Mega Gyarados raids increases your chances of encountering this rare shiny variant.

📌 Is Mega Gyarados Worth Raiding?

Yes, Mega Gyarados is a very strong and bulky Mega Pokémon that provides excellent raid boosts and is useful in many different raid scenarios.

FAQ

Can Mega Gyarados be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Shiny Gyarados has been released in raids or events.

What is Mega Gyarados’s exclusive move?

Mega Gyarados can use moves like Hydro Pump, dealing massive Dark and Water-type damage to opponents in Max Raid Battles.

Is Mega Gyarados difficult to defeat?

It has high attack and decent bulk but moderate speed. Using type-advantage Pokémon ensures efficient and safe battles.

What makes Mega Gyarados unique?

Its intimidating Mega form, Water/Dark typing, and high offensive stats make Mega Gyarados visually powerful and strategically dangerous in Max Raid Battles.

Does Mega Gyarados keep its Mega form after the battle?

Mega Evolution lasts only during battles where Mega is enabled; it does not persist outside combat.
