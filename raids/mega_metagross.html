Mega Metagross Raid Guide — Best Counters, Teams & Strategy

Mega Metagross is a bulky Steel/Psychic Mega that punishes unprepared teams with high-defense, heavy-hitting charged moves and strong resistances. This guide gives you the best counters, recommended team lineups, moveset breakdown, weather considerations, and tips to steamroll (or at least survive) Mega Metagross raids — written in a gaming-blog tone so you can jump right in.

Last updated: October 24, 2025

Mega Metagross — Quick Overview

Mega Metagross combines Steel and Psychic typing: it resists many common raid types (Normal, Flying, Psychic, Bug, Rock, Dragon, Steel, Grass, Ice, Fairy) and has notable weaknesses to Fire and Ghost and Ground (depending on moves). In Pokémon GO it tends to use powerful Steel or Psychic charged moves; this makes Fire and Ground counters the most reliable picks. Because of its bulk, fights often favor sustained DPS and survivability over glass-cannon burst.

Pro tip: Mega Metagross may carry a fast Steel- or Psychic-based moveset — always check the quick and charged moves before selecting your team.

Battle Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Check Metagross's charged move before joining — Fire is always strong, but if you see Psychic or heavy Steel moves, adjust accordingly.
  2. Lead with your fastest Fire DPS to force early damage and pressure shields.
  3. Use Ground counters as swap-ins to absorb or punish Steel-based attacks and cover moves that resist Fire.
  4. Save shields for clutch moments in smaller groups — if Metagross uses Meteor Mash or Heavy Slam, shields can prevent wipeouts of your key DPS.
  5. Consider Mega choices — Mega Charizard or other Fire-boosting Megas help shorten fights; bring Mega Tyranitar only if you're adding Rock coverage for a specific moveset.
  6. Communicate shields & revives when possible — stagger revives so your group doesn't all faint at once.

If Metagross is using Psychic-heavy moves, bring a Ghost or strong Fire attacker with fast charge to force energy windows where Psychic moves are less fatal.

IV / Level & Resource Advice

Focus on powering a few high-impact attackers instead of spreading resources thin. A maxed Reshiram or Victini plus a powered Groudon/Garchomp will carry many raid teams. Blast Burn / Overheat and Precipice Blades are priority moves to obtain via Community Day or TMs where applicable.

Weather Effects & How They Help

Weather can significantly change which counters shine:

  • Sunny/Clear — boosts Fire attackers (great for your Fire counters).
  • Partly Cloudy — boosts Rock (less relevant vs Metagross but can help if using Tyranitar alternatives).
  • Windy — boosts Dragon (not relevant here).
  • Rain — favors Water counters (neutral choice if you lack Fire/Ground).

If sunny weather is active in your area, your Fire attackers gain a big edge — prioritize joining in clear weather when possible.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Bringing only Psychic or Steel attackers — Metagross resists both.
  • Ignoring the charged move — Meteor Mash and Heavy Slam change damage windows.
  • Relying solely on glass-cannon Fire attackers without tanks in small groups.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What is Mega Metagross weak to?

A: Mega Metagross is weak to Fire, Ground, and Ghost (depending on moves). Fire is the most consistent counter type in most scenarios.

Q: Can I solo Mega Metagross?

A: Soloing is difficult due to Metagross's high defense. Only very well-geared trainers with multiple maxed Fire/ Ground attackers should attempt it. Groups of 2–3 top players are much more realistic.

Q: Which Mega should I bring?

A: Mega Charizard (if it buffs Fire attackers on your roster) is a solid choice. Use a Mega that boosts your main damage type (Fire) where possible.

Q: Is Overheat or Blast Burn worth TMing for this fight?

A: Yes — Blast Burn / Overheat dramatically improve many Fire attackers' raid performance and are worth obtaining (Community Day or TM) if you plan to tackle Metagross frequently.

Q: Should I bring Tyranitar or Rampardos?

A: Not as primary counters—these Rock attackers are more situational vs Flying/Fire combos. Prioritize Fire and Ground, then add Rock only for specific move sets or backup roles.

Moves & Threats

Metagross common fast moves are Fury Cutter or Bullet Punch or Zen Headbutt. Charged moves include Earthquake or Flash Cannon and Psychic. Elite moves include Meteor Mash.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 2,705-2,806 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 3,382-3,508 CP
  • Boosted by: Snowy & Windy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Sunny weather : boosts Fire type attackers. Windy weather : boosts Ghost and Dark attackers.

Mega Metagross’s Meteor Mash hits very hard. Prioritize bulky Fire- or Ground-types to absorb damage while dishing out super-effective hits.

When to avoid

Avoid using Fairy or Psychic types, as they take heavy damage from Metagross’s Steel and Psychic moves. Fragile Bug or Ice types can also faint too quickly.

Duo & Trio Possibility

A duo is possible with maxed-out counters and coordination. A trio is comfortable for mid-level players. Casual groups should bring 4–5 trainers.

Rewards

  • Mega Energy for Metagross
  • Chance to catch Metagross (with boosted IVs)
  • Rare Candy & XL Candy

Why Raid Mega Metagross?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Steel / Psychic attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

PvP Relevance

While Metagross isn’t top tier in PvP, it’s a fun spice pick. Mega Metagross shines more in raids and PvE content.

Mega Metagross is the strongest Mega, but it’s easy to raid thanks to multiple weaknesses. It’s worth raiding for its shiny form, Mega Energy, and for filling your Mega Pokédex. With Dark, Fire, Ghost and Ground counters, you’ll defeat it with ease.
