Introduction

Virizion is one of the four Swords of Justice Pokémon and a Legendary Grass/Fighting-type raid boss in Pokémon GO. Its dual typing gives it access to powerful moves that can challenge many common raid attackers.

Despite its strengths, Virizion has clear weaknesses that make it manageable with the right counters. Fire, Flying, Psychic, and Fairy-type Pokémon are especially effective. This guide will help trainers choose the best counters, plan strategies, and maximize rewards after the raid.

Whether you are a beginner looking for solo tips or an experienced player preparing a full raid team, this guide has everything you need to take down Virizion efficiently.

Difficulty

Virizion can use moves like Leaf Blade, Sacred Sword, and Stone Edge. While it isn’t extremely bulky, Stone Edge in particular can punish Flying-type Pokémon, so dodging or smart shield usage becomes very important, especially in small groups.

Moves & Threats

Virizion common fast moves are Quick Attack or Double Kick or Zen Headbutt. Charged moves include Close Combat or Stone Edge or Leaf Blade. Elite moves include Sacred Sword.

Dangerous Moves: Close Combat and Sacred Sword hit very hard and should be dodged if possible.

Stone Edge can be dangerous for Flying-type Pokémon, so be prepared to dodge if you are using fragile attackers.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,649-1,727 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,061-2,159 CP
  • Boosted by: Sunny & Cloudy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Best Team Compositions

Solo

Virizion is one of the few Legendary Pokémon that can be reliably soloed using strong Flying-type counters, especially with a Mega boost.

Small Group (2–3 Trainers)

With decent Flying-type teams, 2 to 3 trainers can easily defeat Virizion.

Medium & Large Groups (4+ Trainers)

With 4 or more trainers, Virizion becomes extremely easy and will go down very quickly.

Weather Boost Effects

  • Sunny weather: Boosts Virizion’s Grass-type moves
  • Cloudy weather: Boosts Virizion’s Fighting-type moves
  • Windy weather: Boosts Flying- and Psychic-type counters

Windy weather is the best condition for fighting Virizion because it boosts your Flying-type attackers without boosting Virizion’s own damage.

Battle Tips and Strategy

  • Always prioritize Flying-type attackers because of the double weakness.
  • Dodge Stone Edge if possible to keep your best attackers alive.
  • Use a Mega Flying-type Pokémon to boost your team’s damage.
  • Avoid using Water-, Electric-, and Dark-types because Virizion resists them.
  • Rejoin quickly if your team faints to save time.

Shiny Virizion in Pokémon GO

Yes! Players can encounter a Shiny Virizion after defeating it in a raid. The shiny version has a slightly different hue with greener highlights compared to the regular Virizion.

Shiny Virizion is rare, so repeated raids increase your chance of obtaining it. Participating in legendary raids with a group will improve efficiency and give more opportunities to encounter a shiny.

Rewards

  • Mega Energy for Virizion
  • Chance to catch Virizion (with boosted IVs)
  • Rare Candy & XL Candy

FAQ

Can Virizion be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes, shiny Virizion is available in Pokémon GO raids.

What moves does Virizion use in raids?

Virizion can use moves like Leaf Blade, Sacred Sword, Close Combat, and Stone Edge, dealing strong Grass, Fighting, and Rock-type damage.

Is Virizion difficult to defeat?

Virizion has good defense and balanced stats but a major weakness to Flying-type attacks. Strong Flying Pokémon can defeat it quickly in raids.

What makes Virizion unique?

Virizion is one of the Swords of Justice and is known for its elegant design, Grass/Fighting typing, and strong combat abilities.

Conclusion

Virizion is one of the easiest Legendary raid bosses in Pokémon GO. Prepare strong Flying-type counters, use Mega evolutions, and take advantage of Windy weather to defeat and catch Virizion quickly and efficiently.
