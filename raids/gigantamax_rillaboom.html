Overview

Rillaboom’s Gigantamax form magnifies its Grass potency — expect powerful G-Max Drum turns that hit multiple times or apply amplified status-like effects (in concept). As a Grass-type boss, Rillaboom threatens with strong Grass STABs (e.g., Drum Beating analogues), possible Ground or Fighting coverage in special raids, and sustained physical/special damage. In both GO and SWSH contexts you should prioritize Fire, Flying and Ice counters, but also plan for hybrid coverage by checking the charged move preview before locking in.

Pro tip: Rillaboom’s access to coverage moves (like Low Sweep / Superpower / Drum Beating) can punish Pokémon that are poor against Fighting coverage — keep one flexible slot in your party.

DPS, Energy & Dynamax Notes

Pokémon GO: focus on fast-move EPS (Fire Spin, Wing Attack) and high-value charges (Blast Burn, Overheat). In G-Max raids the boss HP is large—sustained DPS and energy generation matter more than single-hit nukes. Save big charges for shieldless windows.

Sword & Shield: Dynamax parties should time Max Flare / Max Airstream to break or bait shields. Use supports (Leech Seed, Leftovers) to lengthen windows of safe damage and coordinate dynamax so it multiplies your primary damage type.

Pro tip: Protect your highest-DPS Fire attacker (Blast Burn/Overheat) with a bulky swap or a teammate assigned to shield it from predicted Fighting/coverage moves.

Step-by-step Battle Strategy

  1. Preview charged moves: Lock in Fire if Drum Beating / Seed Bomb appears; lock in Flying/Ice if Fighting coverage shows up.
  2. Lead for EPS: Use fast-energy attackers to generate charges early (Fire Spin, Wing Attack, Powder Snow).
  3. Shield economy (GO): Bait shields using medium charges; save at least one shield in small groups for your strongest charge user.
  4. Swap & tank: Rotate in bulky neutralists (Heatran / Mamoswine) to eat predictable heavy charges then swap back to DPS.
  5. Dynamax timing (SWSH): Use Max Flare / Max Airstream to break defensive stances or to secure a multi-turn DPS window.
  6. Revive policy: Stagger revives and rejoin during safe windows so you avoid faint waves and unnecessary item usage.

Assign one teammate to preserve a shield (GO) or hold a Max Move (SWSH) for the final 20–30% HP window — that single saved resource often flips close fights into clears.

FAQ

Q: What's the single best counter to Gigantamax Rillaboom?

A: Reshiram (Fire Fang / Overheat) or a Blast Burn Charizard are the top single best counters due to powerful Fire STAB. Talonflame and Mamoswine are excellent alternatives for Flying and Ice roles respectively.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Rillaboom?

A: Soloing a Gigantamax boss is very unlikely due to the enlarged HP pool. Only extremely optimized players with multiple maxed legendaries and ideal weather/dynamax timing could attempt it. Realistic clears require 2–5 players depending on roster quality.

Q: Which TMs should I prioritize?

A: Blast Burn / Overheat (Fire), Brave Bird (Flying), and Avalanche (Ice) are high-priority TMs to get the most out of your counters. Invest TMs in the few Pokémon you will repeatedly use.

Q: Should I wait for weather?

A: If you and teammates have weather-boosted Fire or Flying attackers, waiting for Sunny/Windy can reduce player counts. Don’t wait if you already have ideal counters ready—correct roster beats small weather boosts.

Moves & Threats

Rillaboom common fast moves are Razor Leaf or Scratch. Charged moves include Earth Power or Grass Knot and Energy Ball.G-Max Drum Solo not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,817-1,899 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,272-2,374 CP
  • Boosted by: Sunny

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Sunny weather boosts Grass and Fire damage. Snow weather boosts Ice attackers.

Battle flow tip: Bring strong Fire and Flying attackers, especially if Sunny or Windy weather is active, for the fastest clears. Use top Fire and Flying attackers for maximum efficiency. Ice Pokémon can also help, particularly in Snowy weather conditions.

Battle Tips

  • Bring a mix of high DPS (Fire/Bug/Flying/Ice) and at least one tank for large lobbies.
  • Lead with your strongest DPS to shrink the boss fast — G-Max bosses have huge HP pools, so early damage matters.
  • Quick-dodge only very heavy G-Max hits; too much dodging reduces total DPS in long fights.
  • Star and favorite raid teams before joining to minimize relobby time.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Rillaboom?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Grass attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Rewards & Catch Tips

  • Rewards typically include Rare Candy, Dynamax resources, and a chance to catch Rillaboom (shiny availability depends on event).
  • Use Golden Razz for high-IV or shiny attempts, Pinap when farming candy en masse.
  • Weather-boosted catches (Sunny, etc.) yield higher CP; plan catch attempts accordingly.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Keep shields for heavy Grass charged moves like Solar Beam. Rotate attackers if your Fire or Flying types faint quickly. Golden Razz Berries give the highest catch success, while Silver Pinaps help farm Rillaboom Candy.

FAQ

What is Gigantamax Rillaboom type and weakness?

Rillaboom is a Grass type. It is weak to Fire, Flying, Ice, Psychic type moves.

What are good counters for G-Max Drum Solo?

Strong Fire, Flying, Ice, Poison, Bug attackers such as Gigantamax Toxtricity, Gigantamax Venusaur, Gigantamax Charizard, Gigantamax Lapras, Dynamax Passimian, Dynamax Machamp.

Can Gigantamax Rillaboom be shiny?

Yes, but in certain events.

Is Gigantamax Rillaboom catchable after the raid?

Yes — standard raid catch mechanics apply.

Does weather change move sets?

Sunny boosts both Rillaboom’s Grass attacks and your Fire counters, making battles riskier but faster.

What does G-Max Drum Solo do?

G-Max Drum Solo is a charged attack that deals immediate damage and creates a short lingering damage field affecting trainers’ Pokémon. It favors teams with at least one bulky support or planned swap windows.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Rillaboom is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Grass is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Fire and Flying Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller. With the counters and tactics above, you’ll clear Venusaur efficiently and maximize your rewards.
