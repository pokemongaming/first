📖 Introduction

Palkia is a legendary Dragon‑ and Water‑type boss that challenges trainers with high damage output and key resistances, requiring a mix of Dragon and Fairy‑type counters to defeat.

Palkia, the Spatial Pokémon, is one of the Legendary Raid Bosses in Pokémon GO. With its dual Water and Dragon typing, it can hit hard with both Dragon and Water moves, making it a challenging opponent for many trainers.

Its weaknesses to Dragon and Fairy-type attacks allow properly prepared teams to take it down efficiently. This guide provides all the information trainers need, from recommended counters to raid strategy, to maximize rewards after defeating Palkia.

🗡️ Best Counters for Palkia

Using powerful Dragon and Fairy attackers will significantly increase your chances of defeating Palkia quickly. The best Pokémon to defeat Palkia are strong Dragon and Fairy attackers.

🐉 Dragon-Type Counters (High DPS Option)

Dragon-type Pokémon are among the strongest counters against Palkia. They deal super-effective damage and typically have high offensive stats. However, since Palkia also uses Dragon-type moves, Dragon attackers may faint quickly in battle.

  • Use fast moves like Dragon Tail
  • Charged moves such as Outrage or Draco Meteor maximize damage
  • Mega Dragon Pokémon boost team-wide Dragon damage
  • Primal Groudon: (Dragon Tail)

    • Versatile and tanky Dragon; can survive longer in raid.

  • Gyarados: (Dragon Tail / Outrage)

    • High damage, but vulnerable to Water moves; best with weather boost.

  • Zekrom: (Dragon Breath / Outrage)

    • Very high DPS Dragon attacker. Watch out for Water moves.

✨ Fairy-Type Counters (Safer Alternative)

Fairy-type Pokémon deal super-effective damage while resisting Palkia’s Dragon-type moves. This makes them a safer and more consistent option, especially in smaller groups.

  • Use Charm as a fast move for steady damage
  • Charged moves like Dazzling Gleam improve burst damage
  • Mega Fairy Pokémon can boost overall team output
  • Gardevoir: (Confusion / Dazzling Gleam)

    • Fairy type; excellent against Dragon type; avoid Water moves.

  • Togekiss: (Charm / Dazzling Gleam)

    • High CP Fairy; very effective against Dragon.

Why these counters work

Dragon-type Pokémon such as Rayquaza and Salamence can deal extremely high damage thanks to their powerful Dragon attacks.

Fairy-type Pokémon like Gardevoir and Togekiss are also effective because they resist Dragon moves and can survive longer during the raid.

🧠 Raid Strategy & Advanced Tips

1. Balance Power and Survival

Dragon-type attackers deal high DPS but may faint quickly. If your team is struggling to survive, mix in Fairy-type Pokémon to increase overall stability.

2. Watch for Hydro Pump

Palkia’s Water-type charged move can deal heavy damage to Fire- or Rock-type Pokémon. Avoid using those types.

3. Coordinate Mega Evolutions

Using a Mega Dragon or Mega Fairy Pokémon provides a team-wide damage boost. Coordinate with your raid group to keep the Mega boost active throughout the battle.

Palkia’s Dragon and Water typing makes it weak only to Dragon and Fairy-type moves. Because it has no double weakness, trainers must rely on high-DPS counters and strong team coordination to defeat it efficiently.

Weather conditions are an important factor in this raid. In **Rainy weather**, Electric and Grass-type moves receive a weather boost, which increases your counters’ damage per second (DPS). In **Partly Cloudy or Cloudy** weather, Dragon and Fairy moves are boosted, further strengthening those counter types. Checking the in-game weather before assembling your team ensures you’re maximizing your attack potential.

Palkia can use powerful charged moves like **Hydro Pump** and **Draco Meteor**, which hit very hard. Dodging the most damaging charged attacks — especially when your Pokémon are low on health — can significantly improve their survivability and allow your counters to deal more total damage over the course of the battle. In smaller raid groups, tactical dodging becomes even more critical because each faint matters more.

When organizing your team, lead with the highest DPS counters that exploit Palkia’s weaknesses. Once these Pokémon start to fall, rotate in secondary attackers that still deal super-effective damage but offer greater durability. In larger raid groups, coordinating roles — such as focusing Electric counters first, followed by Grass, Dragon, and Fairy types — helps maintain high DPS throughout the encounter and speeds up the clear.

If you have access to **Mega Evolutions** that support your primary counter types — such as **Mega Ampharos** for Electric support or **Mega Altaria** for Fairy support — using them early in the battle can provide an additional damage boost. With strategic team composition, weather-aware planning, and careful charged-move dodging, Palkia becomes a more manageable and repeatable raid encounter.
