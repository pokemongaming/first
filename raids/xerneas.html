Xerneas Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Xerneas is a Fairy-type Legendary Raid Boss in Pokémon GO. Trainers face a challenging battle due to its high stats and powerful moves. Understanding its weaknesses and using the right counters is essential to defeating Xerneas efficiently.

In this guide, you’ll learn everything from Xerneas’ best counters and movesets to catch CP ranges, raid strategies, shiny availability, and expert tips for both beginners and experienced trainers.

Introduction

Xerneas is the Fairy-type Legendary Pokémon representing life and vitality in Pokémon GO. As a Legendary raid boss, it has high attack and stamina, making the battle difficult for unprepared trainers.

Its typing gives it only a few weaknesses, mainly Steel and Poison-type attacks, so trainers need to choose counters wisely. Xerneas also has a variety of moves that can challenge even experienced players.

This guide will cover Xerneas’ weaknesses, the best counters, optimal movesets, CP ranges, raid strategies, and tips for shiny hunting. By following this guide, you can defeat Xerneas efficiently and maximize rewards.

Difficulty

Xerneas can use moves such as Moonblast, Thunder, Close Combat, and Giga Impact. While it isn’t extremely fast, Moonblast in particular can seriously hurt neutral targets, making bulky Steel-types the smartest choice for most teams.

Moves & Threats

Xerneas common fast moves are Tackle or Zen Headbutt. Charged moves include Giga Impact or Close Combat and Megahorn or Thunder or Moonblast. Elite moves include Geomancy.

Dangerous Move: Moonblast is very strong and should be dodged if possible.

Moonblast hits extremely hard and can quickly knock out fragile counters.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 2,073-2,160 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,591-2,701 CP
  • Boosted by: Cloudy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Best Team Compositions

Small Group (3–4 Trainers)

With strong Steel-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Xerneas can be defeated by 3 to 4 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (5–7 Trainers)

A group of 5 to 7 trainers with good counters should defeat Xerneas comfortably.

Large Group (8+ Trainers)

With a large group, Xerneas becomes very easy, but avoid overfilling the lobby unnecessarily.

Weather Boost Effects

  • Cloudy weather: Boosts Xerneas’s Fairy-type moves and also boosts your Poison-type counters
  • Snowy weather: Boosts Steel-type counters

Snowy weather is the best condition for fighting Xerneas because it boosts your Steel-type attackers without boosting Xerneas’s own damage.

Battle Tips and Strategy

  • Steel-types are the safest and strongest counters.
  • Poison-types deal high damage but are usually more fragile.
  • Dodge Moonblast whenever possible.
  • Use Mega evolutions to increase team damage.
  • Rejoin quickly if your team faints.

Shiny Xerneas

Yes, trainers can encounter a Shiny Xerneas after completing the raid. Shiny Xerneas has a slightly different color palette, with more vibrant and golden highlights on its antlers compared to the regular version.

Shiny Xerneas is rare, making it highly sought after by collectors. Participating in multiple Legendary raids increases the chance of obtaining the shiny version.

Rewards

  • Chance to catch Xerneas (with boosted IVs)
  • Rare Candy & XL Candy

FAQ

Can Xerneas be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes! Shiny Xerneas is available in Pokémon GO. Its shiny form features light blue antlers and body.

What moves does Xerneas use in raids?

Xerneas can use moves like Moonblast, Dazzling Gleam, and Close Combat, dealing powerful Fairy and Fighting-type damage to challengers.

Is Xerneas difficult to defeat?

Xerneas has high bulk and strong Fairy-type attacks, making it a moderately challenging raid boss. Using strong Steel-type Pokémon helps defeat it efficiently.

What makes Xerneas unique?

Xerneas represents life in the Kalos region and is known for its beautiful antler design and powerful Fairy-type abilities.

What makes Xerneas strong?

Its high attack, strong resistances, and Meteor Mash make it a top-tier raid and PvP option.

Conclusion

Xerneas is a powerful Legendary Fairy-type Pokémon. Prepare strong Steel- and Poison-type counters, use Mega evolutions, and take advantage of weather boosts to defeat and catch Xerneas efficiently.
