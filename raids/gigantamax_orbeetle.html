Gigantamax Orbeetle — Quick Overview

Gigantamax Orbeetle keeps the Bug/Psychic identity but with much higher HP and slightly increased defensive presence. Expect charged Psychic moves (e.g., Psyshock/Psychic) and high-damage Bug options (e.g., Bug Buzz). Because of this dual typing, Orbeetle is commonly vulnerable to Fire, Flying, Ghost and Dark (situational) but resists Fighting and Psychic to some extent. Your goal: pick counters that deal *consistent super-effective damage* while surviving big psychic or bug nukes.

Pro tip: Gigantamax bosses reward sustained DPS and correct typing — bring high-DPS counters with decent bulk and coordinate Megas if you have them.

Battle Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Preview the charged move before the fight and select Ghost counters if Psychic appears; otherwise default to Fire/Flying.
  2. Lead with your highest DPS Fire/Flying to maximize early damage on the Gigantamax HP pool.
  3. Save shields in small groups for heavy Psychic or Bug nukes that would otherwise wipe your main DPS.
  4. Use Megas that directly boost Fire or Flying damage (e.g., Mega Charizard Y styles) to shorten the fight.
  5. Rotate in Ghost counters if Orbeetle uses multiple Psychic attacks — Ghost types can turn the tide quickly.
  6. Communicate revives & swaps — stagger revives so your team maintains pressure and doesn’t faint together on the next charged move.

Against mixed movesets, a balanced team (2 Fire, 1 Flying, 1 Ghost) offers flexibility to handle any preview your raid shows.

IV / Level & Resource Advice

Prioritize powering a small set of top counters (Ho-Oh, Reshiram, Heatran) rather than many mediocre ones. For accessibility, invest in a couple of Talonflame/Charizard Blast Burn users and a Chandelure if you lack legendaries — they perform solidly for stardust cost.

Weather Effects & Their Impact

  • Sunny/Clear — boosts Fire attackers (ideal).
  • Windy — boosts Flying (good if you rely on Talonflame/Ho-Oh).
  • Partly Cloudy — neutral to slight benefit for some counters.
  • Rain — can boost certain moves but usually makes the raid harder for Fire-heavy teams.

Prefer Sunny or Windy boost when possible — both materially reduce the number of players required and shorten clears.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Bringing teams full of Psychic or Bug attackers — Orbeetle resists these and the fight drags.
  • Ignoring charged move preview — switching strategy after the first nuke is a common and costly mistake.
  • Wasting shields early in small groups — save at least one shield for a late heavy hit.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is Gigantamax Orbeetle weak to Fire?

A: Yes — Fire is one of the most consistent and reliable weaknesses. High-DPS Fire attackers will shorten the fight significantly.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Orbeetle?

A: Soloing a Gigantamax boss is very difficult due to the large HP pool. Only top-tier trainers with multiple maxed legendaries and perfect weather might pull it off. Small groups of 2–3 with strong counters are realistic.

Q: Which Mega is best to bring?

A: Bring a Mega that boosts Fire or Flying damage if available (e.g., Mega Charizard Y-style). The right Mega makes a huge difference on Gigantamax HP pools.

Q: What if Orbeetle uses Psyshock/Psychic?

A: Switch in Ghost or Dark counters (Gengar, Mewtwo with Ghost moves, Houndoom) to exploit Psychic weakness and mitigate damage windows.

Q: Should I TM my Pokémon for this raid?

A: If you frequently raid against Bug/Psychic bosses, wording TMs into Overheat, Brave Bird or Shadow Ball (for Ghost counters) is useful long-term for raid efficiency.

Moves & Threats

Orbeetle common fast moves are Confusion or Struggle Bug. Charged moves include Bug Buzz or Psychic. G-Max Gravitas not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,203-1,270 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,504-1,588 CP
  • Boosted by: Rainy & Windy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Sunny weather: boosts Fire counters. Fog weather : boosts Dark and Ghost counters. Rainy weather : boosts Orbeetle Bug moves. Windy weather : boosts Orbeetle Psychic moves.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Orbeetle?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Bug / Psychic attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Use a mix of Dark, Ghost, and Fire attackers for the best chance to take down Gigantamax Orbeetle quickly. Coordinating with friends will make the raid much easier.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Orbeetle is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Bug/Psychic combo is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Fire and Dark Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller.
