Gigantamax Alcremie — Quick Overview

Alcremie’s Gigantamax form magnifies its Fairy typing and gives it a bulkier HP pool and potentially stronger charged moves (Dazzling Gleam, Draining Kiss, and event coverage like Psyshock or Shadow Ball). In raid form, it behaves like a resilient Fairy boss that rewards correct super-effective counters (Poison, Steel, Ghost in some cases) and punishes teams that bring common physical attackers without coverage. Because of the enlarged HP, energy efficiency and repeatable charged-move value become more important than single-hit burst.

Pro tip: If the charged-move preview shows heavy Fairy moves (Dazzling Gleam / Moonblast), prioritize Poison + Steel counters; if Psychic coverage appears, Ghost becomes a high priority.

DPS & Energy Considerations

Gigantamax fights reward repeated valuable charges and high EPS where practical. Use these tactical rules:

  • EPS vs single-hit: Prefer attackers with good fast-move energy gain that pair with high-value charged moves (Shadow Ball, Meteor Mash, Sludge Bomb).
  • Charge timing: Bait shields with a medium charge and use your big charges after shields are gone.
  • Swap economy: Avoid unnecessary swaps — each swap wastes DPS time and risks faint waves in long HP pools.
  • Shield preservation: Save at least one shield in small groups for protecting your top Meteor Mash / Shadow Ball user.

A core like Giratina + Metagross covers Ghost & Steel needs, letting one player anchor Psychic surprises and another clean with Meteor Mash.

Step-by-step Battle Strategy

  1. Preview charged move: Let it dictate whether to prioritize Poison/Steel or Ghost.
  2. Lead with Ghost/Steel core: Early Ghost pressure forces shields; Meteor Mash users then capitalize on windows.
  3. Shield economy: Save one shield to protect your Meteor Mash or Shadow Ball salvo in small groups.
  4. Swap & tank: Rotate in Excadrill to absorb unexpected heavy charges then switch back to DPS.
  5. Charge timing: Bait shields with medium charges and follow with your biggest charge when shields drop.
  6. Revive policy: Stagger revives and rejoin to avoid immediate faint waves — allow a safe window first.

If you see Psychic + Fairy moves in the same spawn, two Ghosts + one Meteor Mash/Poison slot is the most reliable composition.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What's the single best counter to Gigantamax Alcremie?

A: Metagross with Bullet Punch / Meteor Mash is the single best overall due to Meteor Mash's raid-scaling power and Steel advantage. Giratina (Shadow Ball) is the best Ghost option when Psychic coverage appears.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Alcremie?

A: Soloing Gigantamax bosses is impractical due to massive HP pools. Only the most optimized players with multiple maxed legendaries, perfect movesets and ideal weather might attempt it. Realistic clears are teams of 2–5 depending on roster quality.

Q: Should I TM for Meteor Mash or Shadow Ball?

A: Yes — Meteor Mash (for Metagross) and Shadow Ball (for Giratina/Gengar/Mewtwo) are high-value TMs that drastically improve raid efficiency against Fairy and Psychic variants.

Q: Which counters should I power first?

A: Prioritize Metagross (Meteor Mash) and Giratina; then Excadrill and Toxtricity depending on your roster and raid frequency.

Moves & Threats

Alcremie common fast moves are Tackle or Charm. Charged moves include Psyshock or Dazzling Gleam and Energy Ball. G-Max Finale not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,824-1,915 CP

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Rainy weather : boosts Poison moves. Cloudy weather : boosts Fairy attackers. Snow weather : boosts Steel attackers.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Alcremie?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, tallest Fairy attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Bring top-tier Steel and Poison Pokémon to maximize damage output. Metagross with Meteor Mash is the most reliable counter. Avoid bringing Dragon, Dark, or Fighting types, as they are vulnerable to Fairy attacks. A small group of high-level trainers can clear the raid with optimal counters.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Alcremie is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Fairy type is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Poison and Steel Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller.
