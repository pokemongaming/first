Gigantamax Appletun — Quick Overview

Appletun’s Gigantamax form increases its HP significantly and emphasizes its Grass typing while borrowing move coverage from its evolutions. In raid form it behaves like a durable Grass-based boss that can threaten with Dragon- or Fairy-type charged moves depending on the variant. Expect long fights where consistent super-effective damage (Ice, Fire, Dragon, Flying depending on exact moves) and good shield management are keys to success.

Pro tip: Always check the charged-move preview. Appletun often pairs a strong Grass charged move (Frenzy Plant/Leaf Storm) with a Dragon or Fairy charged move — that preview determines whether you prioritize Dragon/Fairy or Ice/Fire counters.

DPS & Energy Considerations

Gigantamax fights reward consistent energy generation and high-value charge moves. Keep these rules in mind:

  • Fast-move EPS: For prolonged fights, fast energy gain (e.g., Dragon Breath or Powder Snow) gives you more charge opportunities.
  • Charge-move value: Save your high-value charges (Blast Burn, Overheat, Outrage) for expected shieldless windows for maximum effect.
  • Shield economy: Bait shields with a medium charge and follow with your big nuke once shields are gone.
  • Swap cost: Minimize unnecessary swaps — each swap costs DPS time and can waste revives if mistimed.

A Kyurem + Reshiram core covers both Dragon and Fire needs for most lobbies and yields excellent EPS and charge-move value in long fights.

Battle Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Preview the charged move: This determines whether you want Ice/Dragon or Fire/Poison/Steel coverage.
  2. Lead with high-EPS DPS: Kyurem or Mamoswine to build pressure early and force shields.
  3. Shield economy: Save at least one shield in small groups for a late heavy charged move.
  4. Swap in tanks when needed: Heatran or bulky Dialga to absorb predicted heavy charges.
  5. Use charge moves wisely: Bait shields and follow with your big nuke once shields are gone to maximize damage-per-Premier-Ball benefit.
  6. Stagger revives: Rejoin in a safe energy window to avoid immediate fainting and wasting revives.

If you’re in a mixed group, aim for a 2:1 ratio of Ice/Dragon to Fire — this covers most preview outcomes and keeps DPS optimized.

Weather & Mega Considerations

  • Snow/Hail: Boosts Ice — excellent for Mamoswine & Kyurem Ice usage.
  • Sunny/Clear: Boosts Fire — ideal for Reshiram & Blast Burn users when Appletun is pure Grass heavy.
  • Windy: Boosts Dragon/Flying — helps Dragon attackers like Dialga and Salamence.
  • Megas: Mega Charizard (Blast Burn) or Mega Gyarados (if using Water/Dragon combos) can improve throughput — coordinate Megas with your group for maximum benefit.

Expert note: waiting briefly for an appropriate weather boost (Snow for Ice, Sunny for Fire) can convert a 5-player clear into a 3-player clear if you and one teammate have the right legendaries.

Gigantamax raids drop rare candies, TMs, Stardust and a high-CP catch. Use Golden Razz and curveballs for best capture odds. Save Premier Balls early; use berries (Razz/Golden) to manage capture chance. Aim for great/curve throws at the end to maximize success.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What's the best overall counter to Gigantamax Appletun?

A: Kyurem (Dragon Breath / Outrage or Ice Beam) and Mamoswine (Powder Snow / Avalanche) are top overall depending on whether the boss leans Dragon or Grass — they cover the widest range of possible moves.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Appletun?

A: Soloing a Gigantamax boss is extremely difficult due to its large HP pool. Only the most optimized players with multiple maxed legendaries, perfect movesets, and weather boosts might attempt it. Realistic clears are 2–5 trainers depending on roster quality.

Q: Which TMs should I prioritize?

A: Priority TMs are Outrage/Draco Meteor for Dragon attackers, Avalanche for Ice attackers that lack it, and Blast Burn/Overheat for Fire attackers when you expect Grass-only variants.

Q: Which weather is most helpful?

A: Snow/Hail (Ice boost) or Sunny/Clear (Fire boost) are most useful depending on your planned counters. Windy helps Dragon users — coordinate with teammates for best results.

Q: Should I power Kyurem or Reshiram first?

A: If you raid often and face many Dragon/Grass threats, Kyurem is more universally useful; Reshiram is best if you frequently need powerful Fire options. Prioritize based on your local raid pool and which boss variants appear most often.

Moves & Threats

Appletun common fast moves are Bullet Seed or Astonish. Charged moves include Dragon Pulse or Energy Ball or Outrage and Seed Bomb. G-Max Sweetness not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,330-1,401 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,662-1,751 CP
  • Boosted by: Windy & Sunny

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Windy weather : boosts Dragon moves. Sunny weather : boosts Grass attackers. Snowy weather : boosts Ice moves. Cloudy weather : boosts Fairy attackers.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Appletun?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Grass / Dragon attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Focus on Ice and Fairy-type Pokémon for maximum effectiveness, as they hit both Grass and Dragon hard. Avoid bringing Grass or Water Pokémon since they are weak to Appletun’s Grass attacks. A small coordinated group can handle the raid with top counters.
