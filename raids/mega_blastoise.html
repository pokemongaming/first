Mega Blastoise Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Mega Blastoise is a Water-type Mega Raid Boss in Pokémon GO. Its massive defense makes it challenging in raids, but knowing the right counters can make the battle much easier. Mega Blastoise has a major weakness to Electric and Grass-type attacks, making these your best choices for quick wins.

This guide covers everything about Mega Blastoise, including its weaknesses, best counters, movesets, catch CP ranges, weather boosts, shiny availability, and expert raid strategies for both beginner and advanced players.

Introduction

The Mega Evolution of Blastoise, Mega Blastoise is a tanky Water-type Mega Raid boss with high defense and strong moves. Its attacks can deal significant damage to unprepared teams, especially those using Fire- or Ice-type Pokémon.

Despite its defensive strength, Mega Blastoise has clear weaknesses to Electric and Grass-type moves. Trainers using Pokémon like Raikou, Zapdos, or Venusaur can exploit these weaknesses for a fast and efficient raid. This guide provides all the details needed to defeat Mega Blastoise, including counters, movesets, CP info, and more.

🧬 About Mega Blastoise in Pokémon GO

Mega Blastoise is a Water-type Pokémon. When Mega Evolved, it becomes one of the strongest Water-type attackers in the game and provides damage bonuses to Water-type Pokémon in raids.

Mega Blastoise is useful because:

  • It boosts Water-type attackers
  • It is one of the best Water-type Mega Pokémon
  • It is excellent against Fire, Rock, and Ground-type raid bosses

Weaknesses & resistances

CategoryDetails
❌ Weak To: Electric • Grass
✅ Resistant To: Water • Ice • Steel • Fire

Notes: Electric-types are usually the safest and most consistent choice, while Grass-types can deal huge damage but may be riskier depending on Blastoise’s moveset. It resists Fire, Water, Ice, and Steel, so those types are very inefficient here.

The best way to defeat Mega Blastoise is to use strong Electric or Grass-type Pokémon.

Best Mega Evolutions

Recommended Megas to use against Mega Blastoise:

  • Venusaur, Sceptile – Grass boost
  • Rayquaza, Sceptile - Dragon boost

Best Moveset

  • Fast: Water Gun, Rollout, Bite
  • Charged: Ice Beam, Hydro Pump, Flash Cannon, Skull Bash
  • Elite: Hydro Cannon

Pro tip: Its Hydro Cannon hits extremely hard even through resistance, and Ice Beam can punish careless Grass-type teams.This is not a one-shot boss — it is a slow grinder that drains potions and revives.

  • Using Mega Pokémon reduces total relobbies

Common Raid Mistakes

Avoid using fragile Water or Ice attackers—they'll often be outpaced by Blastoise's high damage. Also be cautious with Ghost types if Blastoise runs Crunch.

Best Team Compositions

Small Group (2-3 Trainers)

With strong Electric or Grass-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Mega Blastoise can be defeated by 2 to 3 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (4-5 Trainers)

A group of 4 to 5 trainers with decent counters should have no trouble defeating Mega Blastoise.

Large Group (6+ Trainers)

Mega Blastoise becomes very easy with a large group, but avoid overfilling the lobby to save raid passes.

Can Mega Blastoise Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes! After defeating Mega Blastoise in a raid, trainers will encounter a regular Blastoise, which has a chance of being shiny. Shiny Blastoise has a slightly darker shell and is highly sought after by collectors.

Mega Blastoise itself cannot be caught or shiny, but participating in Mega Raids increases your chances of obtaining a shiny Blastoise afterwards.

FAQ

Can Mega Blastoise be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Shiny Blastoise has been released in raids or events.

What is Mega Blastoise’s exclusive move?

Mega Blastoise can use moves like Hydro Pump and Aqua Tail, dealing massive Water-type damage to opponents during Max Raid Battles.

Is Mega Blastoise difficult to defeat?

It has high special attack and good bulk. Using type-advantage counters ensures the battle is efficient and safe.

What makes Mega Blastoise unique?

Its massive water cannons, improved stats, and strong Water-type moves make Mega Blastoise visually impressive and strategically powerful in Max Raid Battles.

Does Mega Blastoise keep its Mega form after the battle?

Mega Evolution lasts only during battles where Mega is enabled; it does not persist outside combat.
