Overview

Urshifu (Rapid-Strike) is a unique Fighting/Water (in style behavior) fast-striking mon that, when Gigantamaxed, becomes a large whirlwind of precise strikes and sweeping waves. Rapid-Strike Urshifu favors multi-hit combos and priority-like moves (e.g., Surging Strikes analogues) and commonly mixes Fighting and Water coverage in raid variants. As a G-Max boss it gains huge HP and G-Max moves that amplify its pressure — expect heavy single-target and raid-wide strikes.

Pro tip: Rapid-Strike Urshifu’s main weakness is Flying and Psychic (Fighting weakness) — but it often carries Water coverage that can threaten Flying resistors. Balance your slots: Flying primary + Psychic/Water-neutral backup works well.

DPS, Energy & Dynamax/Gigantamax Notes

Pokémon GO: Focus on EPS (Air Slash / Wing Attack) and high-value charge windows (Hurricane / Brave Bird). In G-Max fights with large HP pools, sustain and energy generation beat one-shot glass-cannons. Hold big charges until shields are baited or gone. If Urshifu uses Water moves often, avoid putting all DPS into Flying-only fragile picks.

Sword & Shield: Dynamax increases HP and move potency. Use Max Airstream / Max Mindstorm intelligently: Max Airstream raises Speed and sets up repeated big hits for your team; Max Mindstorm / Max Psychic secures heavy single-target damage. Coordinate dynamax with teammates to avoid wasted Max Moves.

Pro tip: Protect your top flyer in GO — assign one teammate to bait heavy coverage or to preserve a shield for the final nuke.

Step-by-step Strategy

  1. Preview charged moves: Lock in Flyers if Cross Chop/Surging Strikes dominates; slot Psychic if Heavy Fighting + Water appears.
  2. Lead for EPS: Use Air Slash / Wing Attack leads to generate early energy and create charge windows.
  3. Shield economy (GO): Bait small/medium charges and save one shield for your highest-DPS flyer.
  4. Swap & tank: Rotate in bulky psychics or neutralists to absorb predicted heavy charges then swap back to DPS.
  5. Dynamax timing (SWSH): Use Max Airstream / Max Mindstorm after the boss uses shields or to break a defensive state.
  6. Revive management: Stagger revives and rejoin during safe windows to avoid faint waves and wasted items.

Calling one teammate to preserve a shield (GO) or hold their Max Move (SWSH) for the final 20–30% HP window often converts near-miss attempts into wins.

Rewards & Catch / Drop Tips

Pokémon GO: Gigantamax raids reward rare candy, TMs, stardust and a high-CP catch. Use Golden Razz + curve + Great/Excellent throws to maximize capture chance. Save Premier Balls early and aim for accurate throws when HP is low.

Sword & Shield: Max Raid drops usually include Watts, TRs, rare candies and sometimes special items — pick up TRs and Watts to maximize long-term progression.

FAQ

Q: What's the single best counter?

A: Rayquaza (Air Slash / Hurricane) is the top universal flyer for GO. In SWSH, powerful flyers (Rayquaza, Talonflame) and Psychic attackers (Mewtwo, Gardevoir) combined make the cleanest core.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Urshifu (Rapid-Strike)?

A: Soloing is highly unlikely due to the massive HP pool. Only the most optimized trainers with multiple maxed legendaries and favorable weather might attempt it. Realistic clears are 2–5 players depending on roster quality.

Q: Which TMs should I prioritize?

A: Hurricane / Brave Bird for Flying attackers; Psystrike for Mewtwo/Gardevoir; and energy-efficient fast moves to maximize EPS (Air Slash / Wing Attack).

Q: Should I wait for weather?

A: Windy weather boosts Flying — it helps small groups significantly. If you have Windy-boosted Rayquaza/Tornadus windows, coordinate for those to reduce player counts. Otherwise, proceed with a correct roster.

Moves & Threats

Urshifu common fast moves are Rock Smash or Counter or Waterfall. Charged moves include Brick Break or Dynamic Punch or Close Combat and Aqua Jet. G-Max Rapid Flow not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Weather Effects

Rainy weather : boosts Urshifu’s Water moves and powers up Electric/Grass counters moves. Cloudy weather : boosts Fighting moves. Windy weather : boosts Flying / Psychic counters.

Battle Tips

  • Front-load your best Electric/Grass DPS to capitalize on early damage windows.
  • Coordinate dodges only on heavy Max Moves — constant dodging reduces overall DPS in long fights.
  • Star and favorite teams to reduce relobby time; players in large groups should communicate role.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Urshifu is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Grass/Poison combo is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Fire and Flying Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller.
