Buzzwole Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Buzzwole is a Bug and Fighting-type Ultra Beast in Pokémon GO. It has a few key weaknesses that trainers can exploit, but it also has extremely high attack stats, making it a dangerous raid boss. This guide provides all the information needed to defeat Buzzwole efficiently, from best counters and weaknesses to movesets, CP ranges, and raid strategy.

Introduction

Buzzwole is one of the more intimidating Ultra Beasts in Pokémon GO. With its combination of Bug and Fighting typing, it packs some very strong charged attacks, making it a tough opponent for unprepared trainers.

However, it has several notable weaknesses, including Flying, Psychic, and Fire-type attacks, which can be leveraged to defeat it quickly. High-level counters, proper raid strategy, and teamwork are key to defeating this powerful Ultra Beast.

This guide covers everything from Buzzwole’s moves, weaknesses, and catch CP to shiny availability and expert raid tips. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced trainer, this guide will help you beat Buzzwole efficiently.

CP Ranges

Understanding CP ranges helps trainers evaluate the strength of Buzzwole when caught from raids.

  • Perfect (L20): 1,894-1,977 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,368-2,472 CP
  • Boosted by: Rainy & Cloudy

Keep in mind that weather-boosted Buzzwole is significantly harder to defeat, so always prepare strong Flying, Fairy, Fire, or Psychic-type counters.

Why Buzzwole Can Be a Dangerous Raid Boss

Unlike Nihilego, Buzzwole is very bulky and can take a lot of punishment. It also hits extremely hard with moves like Superpower, Dynamic Punch, and Lunge. This means trainers need both strong counters and good team coordination to defeat it efficiently.

Weather Boost Effects

  • Windy: Boosts Flying-type moves (BEST weather for this raid)
  • Cloudy: Boosts Buzzwole’s Fighting-type moves
  • Rainy: Boosts Buzzwole’s Bug-type moves

Revive & Potion Cost

  • Average revives used: 8–14
  • Average potions used: 10–18
  • Dodging significantly reduces resource usage
  • Using Mega Pokémon reduces total relobbies

Common Raid Mistakes

  • Using Fighting or Dark Pokémon (they are resisted)
  • Ignoring Flying-type attackers
  • Using only bulky Pokémon instead of high DPS attackers
  • Not dodging strong charged moves

Best Team Compositions

Small Group (3–4 Trainers)

With strong Flying-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Buzzwole can be defeated by 3 to 4 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (5–7 Trainers)

A group of 5 to 7 trainers with decent counters should have no trouble defeating Buzzwole.

Large Group (8+ Trainers)

Buzzwole becomes very easy with a large group, but avoid overfilling the lobby to save raid passes.

Raid Strategy

  • Start the battle with your strongest Flying-type attackers and keep constant pressure on Buzzwole. Because Buzzwole is bulky, the fight will last longer than Nihilego, so consistency and sustained DPS matter more.
  • Dodging charged moves like Superpower or Dynamic Punch can greatly improve your team’s survival time, especially if you are using glass-cannon Flying types.

Shiny Buzzwole in Pokémon GO

Yes! Trainers have the opportunity to encounter a Shiny Buzzwole after defeating it in a raid. While Buzzwole is extremely powerful, obtaining a shiny version is highly rewarding for collectors and competitive players alike.

The shiny Buzzwole has a slightly different, darker red appearance than its normal variant, making it easy to distinguish. Shiny encounters are random, so multiple raid attempts may be required to get one.

Why Raid Buzzwole?

It has a very popular shiny form. It is one of the bulkiest Fighting/Bug-type Ultra Beasts and a valuable raid and collection Pokémon. It’s a top-tier Legendary for raids and collections.

FAQ

Can Buzzwole be shiny?

Yes, Shiny Buzzwole is available during certain special events.

What are best moveset of Buzzwole?

Best moveset of Buzzwole are Counter which does damage of 13 under Fast Attack and Superpower of 85 under Charged Attack.

Is Buzzwole harder than Nihilego?

Yes. Buzzwole is much bulkier and hits harder, making it a significantly more demanding raid.

What is the best Mega Pokémon to use?

Mega Rayquaza or Mega Pidgeot are the best choices because they boost Flying-type damage for the entire raid team.

Is Buzzwole a difficult raid boss?

Buzzwole has high attack and strong Fighting-type moves, but its weakness to Flying-type attacks makes the raid manageable with proper counters.

How many trainers are needed to defeat Buzzwole?

2–4 trainers with strong Flying or Psychic-type counters can defeat Buzzwole efficiently.
