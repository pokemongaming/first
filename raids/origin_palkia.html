Origin Palkia Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Origin Forme Palkia is a powerful Legendary Pokémon in Pokémon GO raids. This Dragon and Water-type Pokémon has extremely high attack power and can deal massive damage with moves like Draco Meteor and Aqua Tail. Because of its typing, it is vulnerable to Dragon and Fairy-type attacks, making strong Dragon counters the best option for defeating it quickly.

This raid guide explains everything you need to know to defeat Origin Palkia, including its weaknesses, best counters, movesets, catch CP ranges, weather boosts, shiny availability, and raid strategies for both small and large groups of trainers.

📖 Introduction

Origin Palkia is a formidable Tier 5 Legendary raid boss combining Water and Dragon typing. Due to its dual weaknesses to Dragon and Fairy attacks, prepared trainers using powerful counters can bring it down efficiently with proper team composition.

Origin Forme Palkia is the alternate form of Palkia introduced in Pokémon GO during special events inspired by Pokémon Legends: Arceus. This form retains its Dragon and Water typing but has improved battle performance, making it one of the most powerful Dragon-type raid bosses in the game.

Due to its high attack stat and powerful charged moves, Origin Palkia can be challenging for unprepared trainers. However, its weakness to Dragon and Fairy-type Pokémon means that well-prepared teams can defeat it efficiently.

With dual weaknesses to Dragon and Fairy attacks, Origin Palkia can be efficiently defeated with the right counters and team strategy. Proper weather conditions and type advantages make the fight significantly easier.

In this guide, you will learn the best Pokémon to use against Origin Palkia, how weather conditions affect the battle, and strategies to maximize your chances of catching this legendary Pokémon after the raid.

🗡️ Best Origin Palkia Counters

The best counters are powerful Dragon and Fairy-type Pokémon.

🐉 Dragon-Type Counters (Highest DPS)

Dragon-type attackers are the strongest damage dealers against Palkia Origin Forme. They output high DPS and can shorten raid times significantly. However, since Palkia can also use Dragon-type moves, Dragon counters may faint faster in battle.

  • Fast moves like Dragon Tail or Dragon Breath
  • Charged moves such as Outrage or Draco Meteor
  • Mega Dragon Pokémon boost team-wide Dragon damage
  • Haxorus: (Dragon Tail / Breaking Swipe)

    • High DPS, fragile, use with weather boost

  • Palkia: (Dragon Tail / Draco Meteor)

    • Very tanky, resists Dragon moves

  • Latios: (Dragon Breath / Dragon Claw)

    • Bulk and consistent DPS

✨ Fairy-Type Counters (Safer Option)

Fairy-type Pokémon deal super-effective damage while resisting Dragon-type attacks. This makes them more reliable in small groups or when survivability is a concern.

  • Charm provides steady damage output
  • Dazzling Gleam offers strong burst damage
  • Mega Fairy Pokémon increase overall team damage
  • Tapu Koko: (Nature's Madness)

    • Highest raw DPS if powered up

  • Tapu Lele: (Nature's Madness)

    • Best against Dragon moves; bulky if powered

  • Xerneas: (Geomancy / Moonblast)

    • Can deal neutral damage but needs to dodge Dragon Tail

🧠 Raid Strategy & Advanced Tips

1. Choose DPS or Stability

If you have high-level Dragon attackers, you can defeat Palkia Origin Forme quickly. If your team lacks bulk, mix in Fairy-types to reduce relobby time.

2. Watch for Aqua Tail or Hydro Pump

Water-type charged moves can heavily damage Fire- and Rock-type Pokémon. Avoid using Pokémon that do not deal super-effective damage.

3. Coordinate Mega Evolutions

Mega Dragon or Mega Fairy Pokémon provide team-wide attack boosts. Coordinate with your group so that Mega boosts remain active throughout the battle.

Origin Palkia’s dual Water and Dragon typing gives it weaknesses to **Dragon**, **Fairy** attacks. Counters that exploit these weaknesses not only deal super-effective damage but also tend to survive its powerful charged moves better. For example, **Rayquaza with Dragon Tail and Outrage** hits hard on the Dragon weakness, while **Raikou with Thunder Shock and Wild Charge** provides excellent Electric-type damage. **Togekiss with Charm and Dazzling Gleam** is a solid Fairy counter, especially in weather that boosts Fairy moves.

Weather conditions significantly impact this battle. During **Rainy weather**, Electric moves get a major boost, making Electric-type counters more effective. In **Partly Cloudy or Cloudy weather**, Dragon and Fairy moves receive boosts — so Dragon and Fairy counters also perform better under those conditions. Checking the weather before building your team ensures you’re optimizing your damage per second (DPS).

Origin Palkia can use charged moves like **Hydro Pump** and **Draco Meteor**, which hit incredibly hard. It’s not always possible to dodge everything in fast paced raids, but learning to dodge the highest-damage charged moves at the right moments — especially when your counters are low on health — can help your team survive longer and increase total damage output. In smaller raid groups, tactical dodging becomes even more critical because fewer Pokémon are available to absorb hits.

When constructing your team, lead with your highest DPS counters that exploit Origin Palkia’s weaknesses. Once these Pokémon begin to faint, rotate in secondary attackers that still hit weak points but offer better bulk or endurance. In larger raid groups, coordinating roles — for example, starting with Electric and Dragon counters followed by Fairy and Grass types — helps maintain pressure and speed up the clear.

Using Mega Evolutions that support your primary counter types — such as **Mega Aerodactyl** for Rock support (when your team includes Flying threats) or **Mega Ampharos** for Electric support — can further enhance your team’s overall damage output and survivability. With thoughtful team composition, weather awareness, and tactical dodging, Origin Palkia becomes a more manageable raid boss that you can defeat consistently.
