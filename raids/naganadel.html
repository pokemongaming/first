Introduction

Naganadel is an Ultra Beast introduced in Pokémon GO as a high-level raid boss with strong Poison and Dragon-type moves. Its unique typing allows it to deal serious damage, so choosing the right counters is crucial for success.

Due to its weaknesses to Ice, Dragon, and Ground-type attacks, trainers can form small to medium-sized groups using these types to take it down quickly. Naganadel also has a chance to drop a shiny version after the raid.

This guide covers everything from its weaknesses, movesets, recommended counters, catch CP, weather boosts, to shiny availability and raid strategy.

Moves & Threats

Naganadel common fast moves are Poison Jab or Air Slash. Charged moves include Dragon Pulse or Dragon Claw or Sludge Bomb or Fell Stinger and Acrobatics.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,732-1,813 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,165-2,266 CP
  • Boosted by: Cloudy & Windy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Is Naganadel Good in Raids?

Yes. Naganadel is one of the best non-Mega Poison-type attackers in Pokémon GO. It is especially powerful against Fairy- and Grass-type raid bosses.

It competes directly with Pokémon like Roserade, Nihilego, and Mega Beedrill (as a Mega option), and in many cases Naganadel’s raw DPS makes it one of the top picks.

Best Uses for Naganadel

  • Against Fairy-type raid bosses
  • Against Grass-type raid bosses
  • As a high-DPS Poison attacker in short-man raids
  • As a Dragon attacker when you lack better Dragon types

Weather Effects

  • Cloudy: Boosts Poison-type moves (BEST weather)
  • Windy: Boosts Dragon-type moves
  • Sunny: Boosts Fire and Grass (helps some enemies it fights)

In Cloudy weather, Naganadel becomes significantly more dangerous as a Poison-type attacker.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Poison / Dragon attacker and XL Candy farming.

How to Get Naganadel

Naganadel is obtained by evolving Poipole. Poipole is usually available through Special Research or limited-time events.

Should You Power Up Naganadel?

Yes, if you need strong Poison-type attackers. Naganadel is a great investment, especially for players who do not have multiple high-level Mega or Shadow Poison-type Pokémon.

However, because it is fragile, it performs best in raids where you want maximum damage rather than long survival.

Naganadel in PvP

Naganadel is generally not recommended for PvP due to its extremely low bulk and poor survivability. It may work as a surprise pick in some limited formats, but it is not meta-relevant.

Shiny Naganadel

Yes, trainers can encounter a Shiny Naganadel after completing the raid. While Naganadel itself cannot be caught directly, its encounter phase may yield the shiny form.

Shiny Naganadel has a darker purple and pink body, making it visually distinct and highly sought-after by collectors.

The shiny rate in Ultra Beast raids is higher than wild encounters, so participating in multiple raids increases your chances.

FAQ

Is Naganadel better than Nihilego?

In pure DPS, Naganadel often competes very closely or even wins, but Nihilego has much better bulk. Both are excellent and serve slightly different roles.

What moves does Naganadel use in raids?

Naganadel can use moves like Draco Meteor, Sludge Bomb, and Dragon Pulse, dealing massive Dragon and Poison-type damage to opponents.

Is Naganadel better than Roserade?

Yes, in most raid situations Naganadel has higher DPS, though Roserade is easier to build.

Is Naganadel a Legendary Pokémon?

No. It is an Ultra Beast.

Is Naganadel difficult to defeat?

With very high special attack and speed but moderate bulk, Naganadel can be challenging. Using type-advantage Pokémon ensures efficient battles.

What makes Naganadel unique?

As an Ultra Beast, its Poison/Dragon typing, high speed, and special attack make Naganadel visually impressive and strategically powerful in raids.
