Gigantamax Pikachu Raid Guide — Best Counters, Teams & Strategy

Gigantamax Pikachu is the electrifying, crowd-pleasing raid boss that turns a normal Pikachu into a massive Electric threat. Despite its cute looks, it hits hard and—when Gigantamaxed—gains a huge HP pool. This guide covers weaknesses, top counters (Ground & Rock focus), sample teams, moveset analysis, weather interactions, and step-by-step tactics so your group clears faster and cleaner.

Last updated: October 27, 2025

Gigantamax Pikachu — Quick Overview

Gigantamax Pikachu keeps its core Electric identity: high offensive Electric damage, typically featuring fast Electric quick moves and 1–2 heavy Electric charge moves (e.g., Wild Charge, Thunderbolt). Gigantamax HP pools are large — fights reward consistent, focused DPS and the correct type advantage. The single most important rule: Electric is weak to Ground. Ground counters dominate; Rock and bulk can also help in certain move variants.

Pro tip: Always check the charged move preview before the fight. If Pikachu has non-Electric coverage (e.g., Surf or Brick Break in some special events), adapt by swapping in neutral counters accordingly.

Battle Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Check the charged move preview — prepare for Wild Charge or Thunderbolt and confirm Ground counters.
  2. Lead with fast Ground attackers to generate energy and apply early pressure.
  3. Use shields sparingly — in small groups save a shield when Pikachu shows a heavy nuke (Wild Charge/Volt Tackle).
  4. Swap to bulky Ground tanks (Rhyperior, Clodsire) if Pikachu uses repeated heavy charges to stabilize the team.
  5. Coordinate Megas if you have any that boost Ground or neutral damage — use them early to maximize the effectiveness over the long HP bar.
  6. Revive smartly — stagger revives to avoid everyone fainting simultaneously on follow-up charged moves.

If your group lacks strong Ground attackers, concentrate on one powered Ground Pokémon and use shields to protect it — preserving one high-DPS attacker often matters more than many mediocre counters.

IV / Level & Resource Advice

Prioritize powering Ground attackers with correct movesets over perfect IVs. Mud Shot + Earthquake/Precipice Blades and Smack Down + Earthquake are high-impact combinations. If resource-limited, power a single Excadrill or Swampert with ideal moves; they are widespread and highly useful.

Weather Effects & Their Impact

  • Sunny/Clear — neutral for Pikachu; doesn't favour Electric but benefits Fire (irrelevant here).
  • Windy — neutral.
  • Rain — boosts Electric moves (harder); avoid joining a Rain-boosted Gigantamax Pikachu unless you have strong Ground counters.
  • Partly Cloudy — neutral advantage — fine to join.

Aim to join raids that are not Rain-boosted; unboosted or neutral weather makes the fight easier for Ground counters.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Bringing non-Ground teams (e.g., pure Water or Grass) — they'll deal poor damage and prolong the fight.
  • All players bringing the same low-tier Ground Pokémon — diversity of high-quality counters wins faster.
  • Using shields too early in small groups — save one for a late heavy charged move.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is Gigantamax Pikachu weak to Ground?

A: Yes — Ground is Pikachu's primary weakness and your best bet to deal heavy, consistent damage.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Pikachu?

A: Soloing a Gigantamax boss is difficult due to the massive HP pool. Only the most well-equipped trainers with multiple maxed Ground attackers and perfect weather would have a reasonable shot. Small groups of 2–3 top players or 3–6 strong players are realistic.

Q: Is Rain weather bad for this raid?

A: Rain boosts Electric moves — it makes Gigantamax Pikachu tougher. Avoid Rain-boosted raids unless your team is very strong in Ground counters.

Q: Which free-to-play Pokémon help most?

A: Powered-up Excadrill, Rhyperior, and Swampert are excellent accessible choices for F2P players. Excadrill is especially efficient if you have several with good IVs.

Q: Should I TM for Earthquake or Precipice Blades?

A: If you frequently raid against Electric bosses, TMing in Earthquake or Precipice Blades for your Ground attackers is a good long-term investment—Precipice Blades (Groudon) and Earthquake (many Ground options) are top picks.

Moves & Threats

Pikachu common fast moves are Quick Attack or Thunder Shock. Charged moves include Discharge or Thunderbolt and Wild Charge. Elite moves include Present or Surf and Thunder. G-Max Volt Crash not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 493-536 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 616-670 CP

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Rainy weather : boosts Pikachu Electric moves. Sunny / Clear weather : boosts Ground attackers.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Pikachu?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, GOAT Electric attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Pikachu is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Electric combo is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Ground Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller. With the counters and tactics above, you’ll clear Pikachu efficiently and maximize your rewards.
