Gigantamax Melmetal — Quick Overview

Melmetal is a Steel-type juggernaut. In Gigantamax form we scale its HP and slightly boost damage to match other G-Max bosses: expect heavyweight Steel STABs (Flash Cannon-like moves, Heavy Slam variants) and occasional Electric or Rock coverage in event variants. Steel typing gives Melmetal many resistances — but also a few clear weaknesses. The crucial objective: bring strong Fire, Ground, and Fighting counters while balancing survivability so your team lasts the long HP bar.

Pro tip: Since Melmetal resists many common types, prioritize counters that are reliably super-effective and can repeatedly land charged moves — avoid glass-cannons that will be repeatedly shielded.

DPS & Energy Considerations — Practical Showdown Logic

With Gigantamax HP, fights reward sustainable DPS and efficient charge windows. Apply these rules:

  • EPS matters: Fast moves that charge quickly combined with high-value charged moves (Blast Burn, Precipice Blades) shorten long HP bars.
  • Charge-move timing: Bait shields with medium charges then use your high-value charge into an unshielded window.
  • Swap cost: Each swap loses time and damages momentum; plan swaps to absorb predictable moves only.
  • Survivability vs DPS: In G-Max fights, a slightly lower DPS bulky counter is often better than a frail glass-cannon that dies repeatedly and costs revives.

Example: a Heatran that survives two charged moves and fires Flamethrower twice may outperform a frail Fire glass-cannon that delivers one Overheat and faints.

Step-by-step Battle Strategy

  1. Preview the charged move: let it guide whether to favor Fire or Ground.
  2. Lead with a high DPS Fire or Ground: early pressure forces shields and opens charge windows.
  3. Save shields: in small groups keep at least one shield for a late heavy hit; in larger groups be flexible.
  4. Use bulky swap-ins: rotate Heatran/Rhyperior to absorb predicted heavy hits then return to DPS.
  5. Charge timing: bait shields with medium charges and use Overheat/Precipice Blades when you expect the opponent not to shield.
  6. Stagger revives: don’t all rejoin at once after a wipe; wait for a safe DPS window.

If the raid lobby lacks Fire counters, one high-level Groudon can anchor the fight; protect it to preserve DPS throughput.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What's the single best counter to Gigantamax Melmetal?

A: Reshiram (Fire Fang / Overheat) is the top overall counter for most move sets due to strong Fire STAB and high sustained DPS. Heatran and Blast Burn Charizard are excellent alternatives.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Melmetal?

A: Soloing a Gigantamax boss is extremely unlikely. Only the most optimized trainers with multiple maxed legendaries, perfect movesets and ideal weather could attempt it. Realistic clears are 2–5 trainers depending on roster quality.

Q: Should I TM into Blast Burn / Overheat?

A: Yes — Blast Burn (Charizard) and Overheat (Reshiram/Arcanine) are high-value TMs for Melmetal and other Steel-type raid bosses. They're priority TM investments for raid-focused players.

Q: Which weather should I wait for?

A: Sunny/Clear is the best weather to wait for if your group has multiple Fire attackers (Reshiram, Charizard, Heatran). Partly Cloudy gives minor Fighting/Rock benefits but is less impactful.

Q: Which Pokémon should I power first?

A: Prioritize Reshiram (if available), Heatran, and a Blast Burn Charizard. For F2P players, Hydro Cannon Swampert isn't optimal here; instead, focus on Excadrill or Rhyperior as sturdy fillers and a powered Arcanine or Charizard for Fire coverage.

Moves & Threats

Melmetal common fast moves is Thunder Shock. Charged moves include Hyper Beam or Superpower or Thunderbolt or Rock Slide and Flash Cannon. Elite moves include Double Iron Bash. G-Max Meltdown not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Melmetal?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Steel attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

When To Avoid

Avoid normal, flying, rock, bug Pokémon — they take heavy damage from Melmetal moves.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Gigantamax Melmetal’s bulk makes battles lengthy. Focus on DPS-heavy Fire and Fighting Pokémon. Avoid bringing Fairy, Ice, or Dragon Pokémon as they deal little damage. In smaller groups, prioritize top Fire attackers; in larger groups, mix in Ground and Fighting counters for coverage.
