Gigantamax Hatterene — Quick Overview

Hatterene’s Gigantamax form amplifies its Psychic/Fairy toolkit and HP pool. Expect charged Psychic and Fairy moves (Psyshock/Psychic, Dazzling Gleam/Moonblast) and possibly coverage such as Shadow Ball or Thunderbolt in special events. Gigantamax HP changes the optimal approach from "burst and swap" to "sustained pressure + efficient shield usage." For raids, your best approach is to maximize super-effective hits (Dark, Ghost) while including solid neutral tanks to soak occasional heavy Psychic nukes.

Pro tip: Psychic/Fairy double-typed threats often punish common Fighting or Poison counters — bring Ghost and Poison-aware Dark attackers for best effectiveness.

DPS & Energy Considerations (Showdown-style thinking)

Gigantamax raids reward sustained DPS and efficient energy usage. Apply these tactical rules when building or evaluating parties:

  • EPS vs Burst: A steady high-EPS attacker that consistently lands charge moves into unshielded windows wins over glass-cannon burst that faints early.
  • Charge value: Use the most value-packed charge moves (Shadow Ball, Foul Play) into predicted shield-less windows. Bait shields with medium charges.
  • Shield economy: In small groups, save at least one shield for a late heavy nuke. In mid/large groups you can be more liberal but avoid wasting both early.
  • Swap economy: Swapping costs time — use bulky swap-ins (Giratina Altered, Tyranitar) to soak known charged moves, then swap back to damage dealers.

A combo like Giratina + Darkrai provides Ghost and Dark coverage with high EPS and complementary bulk — ideal for small expert groups.

Step-by-step Battle Strategy

  1. Preview charged move: Let the preview decide your lead. If Psychic-heavy, Ghost leads; if Fairy-heavy, include Poison.
  2. Lead aggressively: Open with your highest DPS Ghost/Dark to force early shields and set energy windows.
  3. Shield management: Save one shield for late-stage high-value charge moves (Shadow Ball / Moonblast) in small groups.
  4. Swap to tank: Rotate in Giratina Altered or Tyranitar to soak predicted high-damage charges and return to DPS after the window.
  5. Timing charges: Place your big charges in unshielded windows — using a medium charge to bait a shield then following with Shadow Ball works well.
  6. Revive & return: Stagger revives; re-enter when a safe window appears so you don't faint again immediately.

If Hatterene uses both Psychic and Fairy in the same fight, a balanced core of Giratina + Tyranitar + Roserade/Crobat gives the best flexibility without wasting slots.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What's the single best counter to Gigantamax Hatterene?

A: Giratina (Origin or Altered) with Shadow Claw / Shadow Ball is the best overall counter due to high Ghost DPS and bulk.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Hatterene?

A: Soloing a Gigantamax boss is extremely difficult. Only the most optimized players with multiple maxed legendaries, perfect movesets and ideal weather could attempt it. Realistic clears are teams of 2–5 depending on roster.

Q: Should I TM for Shadow Ball?

A: Yes — Shadow Ball on Ghost and some Psychic counters (Giratina, Mewtwo) is extremely useful for raid efficiency. It’s a high-priority TM if you frequently raid Psychic/Fairy bosses.

Q: Which weather is best?

A: Weather has limited influence for Hatterene; prioritize bringing correct counters. If you have Mega Gengar, boost timing on cloudy/windy days when Ghost moves are slightly better supported.

Q: Which Pokémon should I power first?

A: Prioritize Giratina (Origin or Altered), Darkrai, and Tyranitar for general use. TM investments that grant Shadow Ball or strong Dark/Ghost options are often more valuable than wide Candy XL spending.

Moves & Threats

Hatterene common fast moves are Psycho Cut or Confusion or Charm. Charged moves include Psyshock or Dazzling Gleam or Psychic and Power Whip. G-Max Smite not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

  • Perfect (L20): 1,540-1,616 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,925-2,020 CP

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Windy weather : boosts Psychic moves. Cloudy weather : boosts Fairy attackers. Snowy weather : boosts Steel moves. Foggy weather : boosts Ghost attackers.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Hatterene?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Psychic / Fairy attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Steel-types dominate this raid, resisting Fairy and Psychic attacks. Ghost-types are great offensive picks. Poison-types can also help.
