Cresselia Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

← Back to All Raid Guides

Cresselia is a Psychic-type Legendary Raid Boss in Pokémon GO. It has strong defensive stats, making it a challenging opponent for solo or small-group raids. This guide covers the best counters, weaknesses, movesets, CP ranges, weather boosts, shiny availability, and raid strategies to help trainers defeat Cresselia efficiently.

Introduction

Cresselia is a Legendary Psychic-type Pokémon with exceptional defense and stamina. It’s commonly used in raids to challenge trainers because it can survive high-level counters and inflict significant damage with charged Psychic moves.

Trainers can take advantage of Cresselia’s weaknesses to Bug, Ghost, and Dark-type moves. With the right counters, even smaller groups of 2–3 high-level trainers can defeat this raid boss efficiently. This guide provides all the information needed to maximize your success against Cresselia.

🧬 About Cresselia in Pokémon GO

Cresselia is a pure Psychic-type Legendary Pokémon from the Sinnoh region. While it is not a top-tier attacker in raids, it is extremely annoying to fight because of its high HP and Defense.

Cresselia is especially popular in:

  • GO Battle League (PvP)
  • Pokédex completion
  • Legendary collection

Weaknesses & resistances

CategoryDetails
❌ Weak To: Bug • Ghost • Dark
✅ Resistant To: Fighting • Psychic

Notes: Trainers can exploit these weaknesses to reduce battle time significantly. Its Psychic typing also resists Fighting and Psychic attacks, meaning that using the wrong counters can make the raid drag on. Planning your team around its vulnerabilities is key to success.

The best way to defeat Cresselia is to use Dark, Ghost, or Bug-type Pokémon.

Best Mega Evolutions

Recommended Megas to use against Cresselia:

  • Gengar, Banette – Ghost boost

Difficulty

Unlike glass-cannon raid bosses, Cresselia survives longer and hits steadily. The challenge isn’t raw damage but managing sustained DPS over multiple charged attacks. Groups of mid-level trainers may need shields and coordination, while large groups can brute-force it—but efficiency comes from picking the right counters.

Best Moveset

  • Fast: Confusion, Psycho Cut
  • Charged: Future Sight, Aurora Beam, Moonblast
  • Elite: Grass Knot

Most dangerous moveset: Confusion + Moonblast or Future Sight

CP Ranges

Understanding CP ranges helps trainers evaluate the strength of Cresselia when caught from raids.

  • Perfect (L20): 1,555-1,633 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,944-2,041 CP
  • Boosted by: Windy

Keep in mind that weather-boosted Cresselia is significantly harder to defeat, so always prepare strong Dark, Ghost, or Bug-type counters.

🌦️ Weather Boost

Cresselia gets a weather boost in:

  • Windy weather (boosts Psychic)

Your counters get boosted in:

  • Foggy → Dark and Ghost-types
  • Rainy → Bug-types

Revive & Potion Cost

  • Average revives used: 8–14
  • Average potions used: 10–18
  • Dodging significantly reduces resource usage
  • Using Mega Pokémon reduces total relobbies

Common Raid Mistakes

  • Not bringing a Mega Pokémon for team boosts
  • Using weather-boosted Pokémon of the wrong type
  • Relobbying too late and losing damage time
  • Ignoring dodging during hard-hitting moves

Best Team Compositions

Small Group (3–4 Trainers)

With strong Dark, Ghost and Bug-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Cresselia can be defeated by 3 to 4 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (5–7 Trainers)

A group of 5 to 7 trainers with decent counters should have no trouble defeating Cresselia.

Large Group (8+ Trainers)

Cresselia becomes very easy with a large group, but avoid overfilling the lobby to save raid passes.

Raid Strategy

  • Use Dark or Ghost-type Pokémon for best damage
  • Avoid Fighting and Psychic types
  • Dodge Moonblast if possible (it hits very hard)
  • Use Mega Pokémon to boost team damage
  • Raid with friends for faster and safer clears

Can Cresselia Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes, players have a chance to encounter Shiny Cresselia after completing a Cresselia raid. While not guaranteed, shiny Pokémon appear at a slightly higher rate in Legendary raids compared to wild encounters.

Shiny Cresselia has a slightly different coloration than the normal version, making it a prized Pokémon for collectors.

Participating in multiple Cresselia raids increases your chances of obtaining the shiny variant.

Buddy Distance

20 km (per Pokémon GO buddy distance)

Rewards

📌 Is Cresselia Worth Raiding?

Cresselia is a collector and utility raid. Trainers raid for shiny hunting, XP farming, and to gain XL Candy for powering up top-tier Dark, Ghost, and Bug Pokémon. Its high HP and sustained damage also make it a fun and strategic raid for advanced trainers who enjoy planning and coordination.

PvP Relevance

While Cresselia isn’t top tier in PvP, it’s a fun spice pick. Cresselia shines more in raids and PvE content.

FAQ

Can Cresselia be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Shiny Cresselia has been released in raids or events.

What are best moveset of Cresselia?

Best moveset of Cresselia are Confusion which does damage of 19 under Fast Attack and Moonblast of 130 under Charged Attack.

Is Cresselia a difficult raid boss?

Cresselia is known for its extremely high bulk, making it one of the more defensive raid bosses that can take longer to defeat.

How many trainers are needed to defeat Cresselia?

Cresselia has very high defense, so 4–6 trainers with strong counters are usually recommended.

🏁 Conclusion

Cresselia is a longer, endurance-focused raid. Pick counters that exploit its Psychic weaknesses, consider weather boosts, and plan around shields to survive charged moves. Even casual trainers can succeed with proper preparation, but the true challenge is maximizing efficiency while keeping your squad alive.
