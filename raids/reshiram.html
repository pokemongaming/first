Reshiram Raid Guide — Counters, Weakness & Strategy

Your complete resource for defeating FIERY DRAGON in Pokémon GO raids.

Dragon Fire

Reshiram is a Legendary Dragon and Fire-type raid boss in Pokémon GO known for its powerful moveset and tough defensive stats. Its dual typing gives it specific resistances, but also clear exploitable weaknesses that trainers can take advantage of. Simply using high-CP Pokémon isn’t enough — successful raids depend on picking counters that hit Reshiram’s weaknesses while balancing survivability and damage output.

Reshiram’s unique combination of Dragon and Fire means it resists many common attacking types while being weak to Rock, Dragon, Ground moves. Weather conditions and move effectiveness also influence how battles play out, so adjusting your team based on weather boosts can make a big difference. This guide breaks down the best counters and explains not only *which* Pokémon to use, but *why* and *how* to use them effectively.

Below, you’ll find Reshiram’s weaknesses, recommended counters, weather impact analysis, and tactical battle strategies to help you clear this raid reliably — whether you’re raiding solo or with a group.

Best Reshiram Counters

🐉 Dragon-Type Counters (Highest DPS Option)

Dragon-type Pokémon are among the strongest attackers against Reshiram. They deal consistent super-effective damage and can shorten raid times significantly. However, since Reshiram can also use Dragon-type charged moves, Dragon attackers may faint quickly.

  • Fast moves like Dragon Tail or Dragon Breath
  • Charged moves such as Outrage or Draco Meteor
  • Mega Dragon Pokémon boost team-wide Dragon damage
  • Tyrantrum : (Dragon Tail)
  • Haxorus : (Dragon Tail / Breaking Swipe)
  • Latios: (Dragon Breath / Dragon Claw)

🪨 Rock-Type Counters (Safe and Effective)

Rock-type Pokémon are excellent alternatives. They deal super-effective damage and resist Fire-type attacks, making them more durable during the battle.

  • Use Smack Down or Rock Throw
  • Charged moves like Rock Slide or Stone Edge
  • Mega Rock Pokémon provide additional team boosts
  • Tyrantrum: (Rock Throw)
  • Aerodactyl: (Rock Throw / Rock Slide)
  • Rampardos: (Smack Down / Rock Slide)

🌍 Ground-Type Counters (Strong Backup Option)

Ground-type attackers also deal super-effective damage. They perform particularly well in Sunny weather when Ground-type moves are boosted.

  • Rhyperior: (Rock Wrecker)
  • Groudon: (Mud Shot / Precipice Blades)

Battle Strategy & Tactical Insights

Reshiram’s Dragon and Fire dual typing gives it several key weaknesses — particularly to **Rock**, **Dragon**, **Ground** moves. Counters that exploit these weaknesses not only deal super-effective damage but are also more likely to survive Reshiram’s charged attacks. For example, **Mamoswine with Powder Snow and Avalanche** deals heavy Ice-type damage, while **Rayquaza with Dragon Tail and Outrage** provides strong Dragon-type pressure. **Gardevoir with Charm and Dazzling Gleam** is a reliable counter that resists many of Reshiram’s charged hits.

Weather conditions significantly influence this fight. In **Partly Cloudy** or **Cloudy weather**, Dragon moves receive boosts, which strengthens Dragon counters. Checking the in-game weather before assembling your team helps you take advantage of these boosted moves and maximize your overall damage per second (DPS).

Reshiram can use powerful charged moves like **Overheat** and **Dragon Claw**, which can quickly knock out unprepared counters. Learning to dodge the most damaging charged moves — especially when your Pokémon are low on health — can greatly improve survivability and total damage output. This is particularly important in smaller raid groups where every hit matters more.

When planning your raid team, lead with your highest DPS counters that exploit Reshiram’s weaknesses. Once those Pokémon begin to faint, rotate in secondary attackers that still deal super-effective damage but offer greater bulk or durability. In larger raid lobbies, coordinating roles — such as sending Ice and Dragon counters first followed by Ground types — helps keep constant pressure on Reshiram and makes the battle smoother overall.

Using Mega Evolutions that support your counter types — such as **Mega Ampharos** for Electric or **Mega Gardevoir** for support — can further boost your team’s effectiveness. With thoughtful team composition, weather-aware planning, and tactical dodging, you’ll be in a strong position to defeat Reshiram efficiently and maximize your catch chances.

Difficulty

Solo or duo attempts are extremely difficult, requiring careful dodging and strategic switch-ins. Larger groups can brute-force the raid, but coordination and type synergy are always beneficial.

Best Moveset

  • Fast: Fire Fang, Dragon Breath
  • Charged: Stone Edge, Draco Meteor, Overheat, Crunch
  • Elite: Fusion Flare

Danger: Draco Meteor and Overheat can hit hard, so dodge charged moves when possible to preserve your counters’ health.

Overheat is extremely dangerous as it deals heavy damage and lowers Reshiram’s attack afterward, so dodging is recommended if possible. Fusion Flare also hits very hard, particularly against Grass and Steel types.
