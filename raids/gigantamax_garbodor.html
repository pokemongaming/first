Gigantamax Garbodor Raid Guide | Weakness, Counters & Moveset

Conquer the toxic mountain of trash — Gigantamax Garbodor.

Gigantamax Garbodor is one of the most unique and surprisingly strong Pokémon in both Pokémon GO and Pokémon Sword & Shield. Its massive heap of junk hides dangerous toxins and discarded objects, turning its trash pile into a weaponized fortress. With its exclusive G-Max Malodor move, this Garbage Pokémon can spread poison across the battlefield while dealing solid damage.

This guide will cover everything from its weaknesses and counters to movesets and strategies for defeating Gigantamax Garbodor efficiently.

Overview

Gigantamax Garbodor is a pure Poison-type Pokémon with impressive bulk and a powerful signature move. It debuted in Pokémon Sword & Shield as a Gigantamax form, where its garbage body becomes a toxic mountain containing old toys and even a miniature submarine!

In Pokémon GO, Garbodor’s Gigantamax form is expected to appear in future events or raids, mirroring its strength from the console titles.

Moves & Threats

Garbodor common fast moves are Take Down or Infestation. Charged moves include Body Slam or Acid Spray or Gunk Shot and Seed Bomb. G-Max Malodar inflicts damage and poisons all opposing Pokémon upon impact. One of the few G-Max moves that apply a persistent poison effect to every enemy on the field.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,271-1,339 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,589-1,675 CP
  • Boosted by: Cloudy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects & Battle Flow

Sunny weather : buffs Fire damage. Cloudy weather : buffs Fighting attackers.

Avoid bringing Grass or Fairy Pokémon—they take extra damage from Garbodor’s Poison STABs.

Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Poison attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

G-Max Malodor has both aesthetic and competitive appeal, spreading poison across the field. It synergizes perfectly with bulky partners such as Gigantamax Lapras and Gigantamax Copperajah.

Avoid

Avoid using Fairy, Grass, or Fighting Pokémon—they are resisted and will faint quickly to Poison-type attacks.

FAQ

What is Gigantamax Garbodor type and weakness?

Garbodor is a pure Poison type. It is weak to Ground and Psychic type moves.

What does G-Max Malodar do?

It deals damage and also poisons all opposing pokemon.

What are good counters for G-Max Malodar?

Strong ground and psychic attackers such as Gigantamax Charizard, Gigantamax Kingler, Gigantamax Toxtricity, Dynamax Latios, Dynamax Latias.

Can Gigantamax Garbodor be shiny?

Yes, but in certain events.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Garbodor is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Poison type is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Ground and Psychic Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller. With the counters and tactics above, you’ll clear Garbodor efficiently and maximize your rewards.
