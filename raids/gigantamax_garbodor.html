Gigantamax Garbodor Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness & Strategy

Gigantamax Garbodor is a Poison-type Mega Raid Boss in Pokémon GO with enormous HP and strong attacks. Its Poison typing makes it weak to Ground and Psychic moves, which are key to defeating it efficiently.

This guide covers everything trainers need to know: best counters, weaknesses, movesets, CP ranges, weather boosts, shiny availability, and raid strategies for both beginners and expert players.

Introduction

Gigantamax Garbodor is a formidable Poison-type Pokémon in Pokémon GO raids. With its massive HP and high attack power, it can be a challenge even for experienced trainers.

However, knowing its typing and move set makes it much easier to defeat. Ground and Psychic-type Pokémon are especially effective, dealing super effective damage while resisting many of Garbodor's attacks.

This guide will help you understand Gigantamax Garbodor’s weaknesses, the best counters to bring, strategies for solo or group raids, and details on shiny encounters. Whether you are new to raids or an expert player, this guide ensures you’re prepared to take down Gigantamax Garbodor efficiently.

🧬 About Gigantamax Garbodor in Pokémon GO

Garbodor is a pure Poison-type Pokémon, and in its Gigantamax form it becomes much larger and more powerful in Max Battles. Gigantamax Garbodor is known for its high bulk and annoying Poison-type damage.

While it is not the strongest attacker in the game, it is a very interesting and unique Pokémon to collect, especially for players who enjoy Max Battles and special forms.

🌀 Gigantamax Garbodor Moveset

Garbodor common fast moves are Take Down or Infestation. Charged moves include Body Slam or Acid Spray or Gunk Shot and Seed Bomb. G-Max Malodar inflicts damage and poisons all opposing Pokémon upon impact. One of the few G-Max moves that apply a persistent poison effect to every enemy on the field.

Threats: Gunk Shot – heavy Poison damage, Body Slam – high neutral damage, hard to dodge

Tip: Dodge Gunk Shot when possible and lead with strong Ground or Psychic Pokémon to deal maximum damage.

🎯 Catch CP (100% IV)

After defeating Gigantamax Garbodor, you will catch a normal Garbodor.

  • Perfect (L20): 1,271-1,339 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,589-1,675 CP
  • Boosted by: Cloudy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Max Battle Strategy

  • Lead with bulky neutral Pokémon to build Max energy.
  • Switch into Ground-type attackers during Max Phase.
  • Coordinate Max activations for burst chaining.
  • Avoid stacking Grass-types (resisted damage).
  • Ground-types provide safest and most efficient clears.

🌦️ Weather Boost

Gigantamax Garbodor gets a weather boost in:

  • Cloudy weather (boosts Poison)

Your counters get boosted in:

  • Sunny → Ground-types
  • Windy → Psychic-types

Avoid bringing Grass or Fairy Pokémon—they take extra damage from Garbodor’s Poison STABs.

💡 Best Strategy to Defeat Gigantamax Garbodor

  1. Use Ground or Psychic-type Pokémon only
  2. Dodge Gunk Shot if possible
  3. Use Mega or Primal Pokémon for team boost
  4. Raid with friends for faster and safer clear

How the Battle Usually Plays Out?

Opening Phase

Start with your bulkiest Ground type pokemon and focus on consistent damage.

Mid Battle

Garbodor will start using stronger charged moves like Gunk Shot, Sludge Bomb. Fragile psychic types may start fainting here.

Max Phase

When Dynamax or Gigantamax activates, use your strongest Ground type Max Move. This phase removes a huge chunk of the boss's HP.

End Phase

Maintain pressure and avoid unnecessary fainting loops.

Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Poison attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

G-Max Malodor has both aesthetic and competitive appeal, spreading poison across the field. It synergizes perfectly with bulky partners such as Gigantamax Lapras and Gigantamax Copperajah.

📌 Is Gigantamax Garbodor Worth Raiding?

Gigantamax Garbodor is mainly worth raiding for its unique Gigantamax form, Pokédex completion, and collection value. It is not a top-tier attacker, but it is a fun and rare Pokémon to own.

Common Mistakes To Avoid:

  • Using Fairy, Grass, or Fighting types
  • Bringing low-CP Pokémon just because they can Dynamax
  • Ignoring weather boosts
  • Using only Psychic types and no Ground types

Shiny Gigantamax Garbodor

Yes! Trainers can encounter a Shiny Garbodor after defeating Gigantamax Garbodor in a raid. The shiny form features a darker, more vibrant green color compared to the regular version, making it highly collectible.

While the Gigantamax form itself cannot be caught, completing the raid allows you to encounter a normal Garbodor, with a chance of it being shiny. Shiny Pokémon are rare, so multiple raids may be needed to increase your chances.

FAQ

What is Gigantamax Garbodor type and weakness?

Garbodor is a pure Poison type. It is weak to Ground and Psychic type moves.

What does G-Max Malodar do?

It deals damage and also poisons all opposing pokemon.

Is Gigantamax Garbodor difficult to defeat?

It has moderate defensive stats, but teams using Ground or Psychic-type counters can defeat it efficiently.

Can Gigantamax Garbodor be shiny?

Yes, but in certain events.

What makes Gigantamax Garbodor unique?

Gigantamax Garbodor becomes a massive pile of toxic waste and debris, covering the battlefield in filth and dealing poison damage to all opponents.

What is Gigantamax Garbodor’s exclusive move?

Gigantamax Garbodor uses the special move G-Max Malodor. This Poison-type attack deals damage and continuously damages opponents over time.
