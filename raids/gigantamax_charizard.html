Gigantamax Charizard Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Gigantamax Charizard is a powerful Fire and Flying-type raid boss in Pokémon GO. With its massive CP and strong moves, it can be a challenge for most trainers. Its main vulnerability is Rock-type attacks, which deal double damage.

This raid guide covers everything you need to know to defeat Gigantamax Charizard, including best counters, weaknesses, movesets, catch CP ranges, weather boosts, shiny availability, and expert raid strategies for both beginner and experienced trainers.

Introduction

Gigantamax Charizard is one of the most iconic Pokémon in Pokémon GO. Its Fire and Flying typing allows it to deal massive damage with powerful moves like Flamethrower and Blast Burn. Trainers need to plan carefully to avoid taking heavy damage during the raid.

The main strategy to defeat Gigantamax Charizard is using strong Rock-type Pokémon and counters. Fire-type attacks are very dangerous, so avoid using Grass or Bug Pokémon. With proper counters and coordination, even smaller groups of high-level trainers can succeed.

This guide provides detailed information about counters, movesets, CP ranges, weather effects, shiny availability, and raid strategy to help trainers defeat Gigantamax Charizard efficiently.

Max Battle Strategy

  • Open with durable Pokémon to safely build Max Meter.
  • Switch to Rock-type attackers during Max Phase.
  • Avoid Grass, Bug, and Steel attackers due to resistance.
  • Watch for strong Fire-type charged attacks.
  • Coordinate Max activation in small groups for burst chaining.

Moves & Threats

Charizard common fast moves are Air Slash or Fire Spin. Charged moves include Air Cutter or Fire Blast or Overheat and Dragon Claw. Elite moves include Wing Attack or Ember or Dragon Breath or Blast Burn or Flamethrower. G-Max Wildfire not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Threats: Fire Blast and Blast Burn deal high damage quickly, Air Slash adds consistency to damage output.

Tip: Dodge charged moves to preserve your counters.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,574-1,651 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,968-2,064 CP
  • Boosted by: Windy & Sunny

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects & Battle Flow

Sunny weather : boosts Fire damage. Windy weather : boosts Flying attackers. Rainy weather : boosts Water attackers.

Blast Burn is devastating — preserve shields for your highest DPS Rock or Water attackers when you see it.

Battle Tips

  • Avoid Grass-, Bug-, and Fire-types—they take extra damage.
  • Use Rock-type moves for double effectiveness against Flying.

Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Fire / Flying attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

When To Avoid

Avoid using Grass, Bug, and Fairy counters — they deal low damage and risk fainting quickly. Be cautious using fragile Rock types without bulk.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

If Charizard uses Air Slash or Blast Burn, protect your strongest Rock attackers with shields. After the raid, use Golden Razz and curve + Excellent throws to maximize catch chances.

Can Gigantamax Charizard Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes! After defeating Gigantamax Charizard in a raid, players can encounter a Shiny Charizard. While the Gigantamax form itself cannot be caught, the regular Charizard that appears post-raid can be shiny.

Shiny Charizard has a darker orange and red color scheme compared to the normal version, making it a prized Pokémon for collectors and raid enthusiasts.

The shiny rate for raids is higher than regular encounters, so participating in multiple raids increases your chance of obtaining a shiny Charizard.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Charizard is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Fire/Flying combo is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Water and Electric Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller. With the counters and tactics above, you’ll clear Charizard efficiently and maximize your rewards.
