  • Requires coordinated raid teams
  • Has boosted HP and damage output
  • Uses exclusive, high-pressure raid mechanics

Weather Boost – Gigantamax Charizard

Charizard becomes significantly more dangerous or easier in raids depending on weather conditions, because weather directly boosts its Fire or Flying-type moves.

Weather Effects

Sunny Weather (Worst for Raiders)

  • Boosts Fire-type moves
  • Charizard’s damage increases significantly
  • Moves like Fire Spin, Overheat, Blast Burn hit much harder
  • Makes raid much more difficult

Rainy Weather (Best for Raiders)

  • Boosts Water-type counters
  • Makes Gigantamax Blastoise, Gigantamax Inteleon, Gigantamax Kingler much stronger
  • Reduces overall raid difficulty significantly

Partly Cloudy (Very Good for Raiders)

  • Boosts Rock-type counters
  • Strengthens Dynamax Omastar, Dynamax Gigalith, Dynamax Kabutops
  • Rock is already super effective

Windy Weather:

  • Boosts Flying-type moves
  • Charizard’s Air Slash becomes stronger
  • Also boosts Dragon-type counters

Weather Impact Summary

Weather Effect Difficulty
Sunny Boosts Charizard Fire damage Very Hard
Rainy Boosts Water counters Easy
Partly Cloudy Boosts Rock counters Easiest
Windy Boosts Flying damage Medium

Raid Strategy Guide – Gigantamax Charizard

Charizard Gigantamax raids are built around high HP, strong Fire pressure, and group coordination, so your strategy matters more than individual strength.

Pre-Raid Preparation

Build the right team

Focus on:

  • Rock-type attackers (highest priority)
    • Dynamax Omastar
    • Dynamax Gigalith
  • Water-type backup
    • Gigantamax Inteleon
    • Gigantamax Blastoise
  • Electric secondary options
    • Gigantamax Toxtricity
    • Dynamax Zapdos

Check weather first

  • Rainy = best (boosts Water counters)
  • Partly Cloudy = best (boosts Rock)
  • Sunny = worst (boosts Charizard)

Battle Phase Strategy

Opening phase

  • Start with highest DPS Rock attackers
  • Avoid defensive Pokémon early

Mid-fight control

  • Expect heavy pressure from:
    • Fire Spin (fast chip damage)
    • Overheat / Blast Burn
  • Don’t over-dodge—focus on DPS uptime

Faint & rejoin cycle

  • Pre-set second team
  • Rejoin instantly after fainting
  • Maintain constant damage pressure

Group Coordination

  • Ideal team size: 8–12+ players
  • Sync relobbies if group wipes
  • Focus fire
  • Assign “tank DPS” vs “burst DPS” roles if possible

Key Combat Tips

  • Don’t bring random mixed teams
  • Avoid over-defensive Pokémon
  • Save shields only if mechanics allow
  • Keep attacking even during animation delays

Winning Strategy Summary

Core plan:

  • Rock attackers first
  • Weather advantage if possible
  • Fast re-entry after fainting
  • Continuous DPS pressure

Strategy Priority List

Priority Action
Highest Use Rock-type DPS
Highest Maintain constant damage uptime
High Coordinate group timing
High Use favorable weather
Medium Backup Water/Electric counters

Gigantamax Mechanics

Charizard Gigantamax form follows special battle transformation mechanics that are different from normal raids, Mega Evolution, or standard Dynamax systems.

What Gigantamax actually is

  • A temporary transformation during battle events
  • Pokémon becomes:
    • Much larger in size
    • Boosted in HP and damage output
    • Capable of using a special G-Max move

G-Max Move System

For Charizard:

  • G-Max Wildfire
    • Deals Fire damage over time
    • Applies extra pressure to all opponents
    • Designed for raid-wide impact, not single target burst

Stat Scaling Mechanics

When Gigantamax is active:

  • HP is massively increased
  • Attack pressure is amplified
  • Defense is boosted to extend battle duration

Raid Interaction System

  • Designed for multi-player battles only
  • Damage is distributed across multiple attackers
  • Boss is intended to survive long enough for:
    • Multiple attack cycles
    • Team rotations
    • Repeated faint/revive loops

Battle Flow Mechanics

Typical Gigantamax raid flow:

  • Team enters raid
  • Boss enters Gigantamax state
  • High HP pool activates
  • Fast + charged moves apply heavy pressure
  • Players rotate Pokémon repeatedly
  • Boss is defeated via sustained DPS

Environmental Influence

  • Weather boosts can significantly affect difficulty:
    • Sunny → boosts Fire (harder)
    • Rainy → boosts Water counters (easier)
    • Partly Cloudy → boosts Rock counters (easiest vs Charizard)

Key Differences vs Other Systems

System Type
Evolution Permanent upgrade

Common Raid Mistakes – Gigantamax Charizard

Charizard Gigantamax raids are often failed not because the boss is impossible, but because players make avoidable mistakes in preparation and execution.

Using Wrong Counters

  • Bringing Grass, Bug, or Fighting-heavy teams
  • Ignoring the 4× Rock weakness
  • Using low DPS Pokémon just because they are “tanky”

Fix:

  • Prioritize Rock-type attackers like Dynamax Omastar and Dynamax Gigalith.

Ignoring Weather Advantage

  • Fighting in Sunny weather (boosts Charizard) without adjusting team
  • Not taking advantage of Rainy or Partly Cloudy boosts

Fix:

  • Raid during favorable weather or adjust counters accordingly.

Low DPS Teams

  • Overloading teams with defensive Pokémon
  • Not enough high damage output per second (DPS)

Fix:

  • Use glass cannon Rock/Water attackers for faster clears.

Poor Group Coordination

  • Too many players attacking randomly
  • Not rejoining quickly after fainting
  • No synchronization during raid damage windows

Fix:

  • Coordinate relobbies and maintain continuous pressure.

Not Reviving Efficiently

  • Taking too long to rejoin battle
  • Leaving gaps where damage output drops

Fix:

  • Pre-select teams and revive quickly to maintain DPS uptime.

Ignoring Charged Move Threats

  • Not reacting to Blast Burn or Overheat
  • Sending weak counters into charged move cycles

Fix:

  • Expect burst damage and rotate fainted Pokémon smartly.

Underestimating Group Requirement

  • Attempting raids with too few players (3–5 low-level trainers)
  • Assuming normal raid rules apply

Fix:

  • Aim for 8–12+ players for stable clears

Is it better than Mega Charizard?

How many players are needed?

Is it beginner-friendly?

Not really:

  • Requires strong counters (Rock/Water types)

Pokédex Entry

Gigantamax Charizard is described as a Pokémon whose flames become so intense that they can melt nearly anything it touches. Its wings and body are engulfed in blazing energy, making it appear larger, more aggressive, and far more destructive than its standard form.

`

Gigantamax Characteristics

  • Flames intensify into massive, unstable fire energy
  • Body size increases dramatically in raid battles
  • Gains access to exclusive G-Max Fire-based attacks
  • Designed to represent extreme offensive power

Biological Traits

  • Breathes fire hot enough to melt rock and metal
  • Wings generate heat currents during flight
  • Becomes more aggressive in Gigantamax state
  • Fire intensity increases with battle pressure

Battle Role in Pokédex

  • Normal Charizard: Balanced attacker
  • Mega Charizard: Specialized offensive evolution (X/Y forms)
  • Gigantamax Charizard: Temporary raid powerhouse with exclusive mechanics

Events & Availability – Gigantamax Charizard

Charizard Gigantamax form appears only during special limited-time events, not in regular raids. It is one of the most event-locked and rare raid bosses in Pokémon GO–style content.

Conclusion – Gigantamax Charizard

Charizard Gigantamax form is a high-impact raid boss that leans more toward prestige, fun, and collection value than pure competitive necessity. It delivers a challenging raid experience, especially for coordinated groups, and rewards players with rare encounters, shiny potential, and strong Fire-type utility in the right situations. While it is not the absolute top-tier Fire attacker in every scenario, its event exclusivity and iconic status make it one of the most desirable Gigantamax raids whenever it appears.

Overall Verdict

  • Worth raiding for: collectors, event players, and group raiders
  • Optional for: meta-focused DPS players
  • Best part: rarity + raid experience + shiny hunt value
