Gigantamax Inteleon Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

← Back to All Raid Guides

Gigantamax Inteleon is a Water-type Dynamax Pokémon that appears in special Max Raid Battles. Its high Speed and Water-type attacks make it challenging for many trainers, but understanding its weaknesses and best counters allows you to defeat it efficiently.

This guide covers everything about Gigantamax Inteleon, including its best counters, weaknesses, movesets, CP ranges, shiny availability, and expert raid strategies for both beginners and advanced trainers.

Introduction

Gigantamax Inteleon is the Galar region’s evolved Water-type starter, and its Gigantamax form brings impressive Water-type attacks in Max Raid Battles. It can hit hard with moves like Hydro Pump and Snipe Shot, making preparation essential.

Due to its Water typing, Gigantamax Inteleon is highly vulnerable to Electric and Grass attacks. Trainers who prepare the right counters can quickly defeat it, even in smaller groups.

This guide will help you choose the best counters, understand its moves and weaknesses, and ensure you are ready for a successful Gigantamax Inteleon raid.

Overview

Gigantamax Inteleon is the Galar region’s Water-type sharpshooter, standing tall on a massive water tower with incredible precision attacks. Known for its signature move G-Max Hydrosnipe, this form of Inteleon delivers devastating single-target Water-type damage, making it a formidable opponent in Pokémon GO raids.

To secure victory, trainers must prepare teams with strong Electric and Grass-type Pokémon and understand its moveset dynamics.

Moves & Threats

Inteleon common fast moves are Pound or Water Gun. Charged moves include Shadow Ball or Surf and Water Pulse. G-Max Hydrosnipe not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Threats: Shadow Ball – unexpected Ghost coverage.

Tip: Dodge Hydro Pump and Ice Beam for maximum survival, and anticipate Shadow Ball if using Grass-types.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,614-1,693 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,018-2,117 CP
  • Boosted by: Rainy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Rainy weather: boosts Water damage. Sunny weather : boosts Grass attackers.

Battle Tips

  • Lead with Electric Pokémon for high damage output.
  • Grass-types help exploit its Water typing.
  • Beware of Ice Beam if relying on Grass-types; consider dodging.

Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Water attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

When To Avoid

Avoid using fragile Water or Ice attackers that will faint quickly to Inteleon's charged moves. Also be cautious with bulk-reliant Pokémon that do low DPS despite resisting Water.

Max Battle Strategy

  • Lead with Electric-types to safely build Max energy.
  • Activate Max Phase together for burst damage windows.
  • Avoid Fire, Ice, and Steel attackers due to resistance.
  • Be cautious using Grass-types if Ice coverage is present.
  • Electric-type teams provide safest and fastest clears.

Shiny Gigantamax Inteleon

Players have the chance to encounter a Shiny Inteleon after completing a Gigantamax Inteleon raid. The shiny form features a darker blue color compared to the normal version, making it visually distinct and highly desirable for collectors.

While the Gigantamax form itself cannot be caught, the regular Inteleon you receive after the raid can be shiny. Participating in multiple raids increases the chance of obtaining a shiny version.

FAQ

What is Gigantamax Inteleon type and weakness?

Inteleon is a Water type. It is weak to Electric, Grass type moves.

Is Gigantamax Inteleon difficult to defeat?

It has moderate defenses but very high special attack. Using Electric or Grass-type counters allows players to defeat it efficiently.

Can Gigantamax Inteleon be shiny?

Yes, but in certain events.

Is Gigantamax Inteleon catchable after the raid?

Yes — standard raid catch mechanics apply.

What is Gigantamax Inteleon’s exclusive move?

Gigantamax Inteleon uses the special move G-Max Hydrosnipe. This Water-type attack deals massive damage and can target multiple opponents in Max Raid Battles.

What makes Gigantamax Inteleon unique?

Gigantamax Inteleon stretches into a massive sniper-like form, allowing it to attack from long range with incredible precision, making it visually striking and dangerous in battle.
