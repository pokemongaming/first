Introduction

Gigantamax Rillaboom is the Gigantamax form of Rillaboom, the Grass-type evolution of Grookey. With its G-Max Drum Solo moveset, it can deal heavy Grass-type damage to your raid team. Trainers must prepare the right counters to take it down efficiently.

Because Rillaboom is Grass-type, it has several weaknesses, including Fire, Flying, Bug, Poison, Ice, and Fairy-type Pokémon. Using these counters, trainers can maximize damage and reduce the number of high-level Pokémon needed in the raid. This guide provides step-by-step strategies for beginners and advanced players to defeat Gigantamax Rillaboom.

Overview

Rillaboom’s Gigantamax form magnifies its Grass potency — expect powerful G-Max Drum turns that hit multiple times or apply amplified status-like effects (in concept). As a Grass-type boss, Rillaboom threatens with strong Grass STABs (e.g., Drum Beating analogues), possible Ground or Fighting coverage in special raids, and sustained physical/special damage. In both GO and SWSH contexts you should prioritize Fire, Flying and Ice counters, but also plan for hybrid coverage by checking the charged move preview before locking in.

Pro tip: Rillaboom’s access to coverage moves (like Low Sweep / Superpower / Drum Beating) can punish Pokémon that are poor against Fighting coverage — keep one flexible slot in your party.

Moves & Threats

Rillaboom common fast moves are Razor Leaf or Scratch. Charged moves include Earth Power or Grass Knot and Energy Ball.G-Max Drum Solo not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Threat: Grass Knot – moderate but consistent damage.

Tip: Focus on dodging charged moves when possible, especially Drum Solo, to reduce damage.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,817-1,899 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,272-2,374 CP
  • Boosted by: Sunny

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Sunny weather boosts Grass and Fire damage. Snow weather boosts Ice attackers.

Battle flow tip: Bring strong Fire and Flying attackers, especially if Sunny or Windy weather is active, for the fastest clears. Use top Fire and Flying attackers for maximum efficiency. Ice Pokémon can also help, particularly in Snowy weather conditions.

Battle Tips

  • Lead with Fire or Flying Pokémon for maximum effectiveness.
  • Avoid Grass and Water Pokémon—they take heavy damage.
  • eam coordination and dodging charged moves speed up the takedown.

Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Grass attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Rewards & Catch Tips

  • Rewards typically include Rare Candy, Dynamax resources, and a chance to catch Rillaboom (shiny availability depends on event).
  • Use Golden Razz for high-IV or shiny attempts, Pinap when farming candy en masse.
  • Weather-boosted catches (Sunny, etc.) yield higher CP; plan catch attempts accordingly.

Max Battle Strategy

  • Lead with bulky Pokémon to safely charge Max energy.
  • Switch into Fire-types during Max Phase.
  • Avoid Water and Electric attackers due to resistance.
  • Coordinate Max activations for efficient shield breaking.
  • Prioritize high-DPS Fire attackers for fastest clears.

Can Gigantamax Rillaboom Be Shiny?

Yes, trainers can encounter a Shiny Rillaboom after defeating Gigantamax Rillaboom in a raid battle. While the Gigantamax form itself cannot be caught, the regular Rillaboom spawned after the raid has a chance of being shiny.

The shiny Rillaboom has a noticeably darker green coloration compared to the standard version. Participating in multiple Gigantamax Rillaboom raids increases the chance to obtain a shiny Pokémon.

FAQ

What is Gigantamax Rillaboom type and weakness?

Rillaboom is a Grass type. It is weak to Fire, Flying, Ice, Psychic type moves.

What is Gigantamax Rillaboom’s exclusive move?

Gigantamax Rillaboom uses the special move G-Max Drum Solo. This Grass-type attack deals massive damage and continues to strike multiple opponents over several turns.

Can Gigantamax Rillaboom be shiny?

Yes, but in certain events.

Is Gigantamax Rillaboom catchable after the raid?

Yes — standard raid catch mechanics apply.

Is Gigantamax Rillaboom difficult to defeat?

It has high attack and decent defense. Using Fire, Ice, or Flying-type counters allows players to defeat it efficiently.

What does G-Max Drum Solo do?

G-Max Drum Solo is a charged attack that deals immediate damage and creates a short lingering damage field affecting trainers’ Pokémon. It favors teams with at least one bulky support or planned swap windows.

What makes Gigantamax Rillaboom unique?

Gigantamax Rillaboom’s massive drums and musical attacks dominate the battlefield, creating a visually impressive spectacle while dealing massive damage to opponents.
