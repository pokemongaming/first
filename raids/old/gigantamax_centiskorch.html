Introduction

Gigantamax Centiskorch is a Fire-type Pokémon that becomes even more dangerous in its Gigantamax form. Its high attack stats and access to Fire-type moves can deal heavy damage to unprepared teams.

Trainers can take advantage of its elemental weaknesses. Water-, Rock-, and Ground-type Pokémon deal the most damage, making these types essential for an efficient raid strategy. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned trainer, understanding Gigantamax Centiskorch’s moves, CP, and raid mechanics will help you defeat it quickly.

This guide covers all aspects of the Gigantamax Centiskorch raid, from recommended counters and movesets to shiny availability and related Pokémon raids.

Overview

Centiskorch in Gigantamax form is a Fire/Bug raid boss that amplifies damage and HP. Expect high-power Fire STABs (e.g., Fire Blast / Overheat analogues), large-area flamethrowers, and possible Bug-type charged moves (Bug Buzz) or coverage like Earthquake or Iron Head in special spawns. Because of its Fire typing, Centiskorch is especially vulnerable to Water, Rock, and Ground depending on moves. Prioritize counters that can land repeatedly into long HP bars and survive charged coverage.

Max Battle Strategy

  • Build Max Meter using bulky Pokémon early.
  • Switch to Rock-type attackers during Max Phase.
  • Coordinate Max usage in smaller lobbies.
  • Avoid Grass and Steel attackers due to resistance.
  • Prioritize survivability before burst damage in 4–5 player groups.

Moves & Threats

Centiskorch common fast moves are Bug Bite or Ember. Charged moves include Bug Buzz or Lunge or Heat Wave and Crunch. G-Max Centiferno not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Threat: Fire Blast and Flamethrower deal high damage over time.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,630-1,708 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,038-2,136 CP
  • Boosted by: Rainy & Sunny

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Rainy weather : boosts Water counters.Partly Cloudy weather : boosts Rock counters. Sunny weather : boosts Centiskorch Fire moves.

Battle Tips

  • Avoid Fire- and Bug-types—they’ll take extra damage.
  • Dodge charged moves to preserve your Pokémon.

Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, pretty cool Fire / Bug attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Use Rock and Water attackers to exploit Centiskorch’s weaknesses. Avoid Grass or Steel Pokémon as they take heavy damage from Centiskorch’s Fire-type attacks.

Can Gigantamax Centiskorch Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes! After completing the Gigantamax Centiskorch raid, players can encounter a Shiny Centiskorch. While the Gigantamax form itself cannot be caught, defeating it gives the chance to find a shiny regular Centiskorch.

Shiny Centiskorch has a darker, richer red hue compared to the normal version, making it a rare and desirable Pokémon for collectors.

Like all raid shinies, the chance is higher than wild encounters, so participating in multiple raids increases your odds of obtaining it.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Centiskorch is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Fire / Bug combo is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Rock and Flying Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller. With the counters and tactics above, you’ll clear Centiskorch efficiently and maximize your rewards.
