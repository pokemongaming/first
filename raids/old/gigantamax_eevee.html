Introduction

Gigantamax Eevee is a special raid boss in Pokémon GO that appears during certain events. While its typing is Normal, its Gigantamax form allows it to use powerful G-Max moves that can disrupt your raid team.

Trainers should prepare strong Fighting-type counters to exploit its major weakness. This guide provides detailed strategies, recommended counters, and tips for both solo and group raids.

Whether you’re an experienced trainer or just starting, following this guide will help you maximize your chances of capturing Eevee and potentially encountering its shiny form.

Moves & Threats

Eevee common fast moves are Quick Attack or Tackle. Charged moves include Dig or Swift. Elite moves include Body Slam or Last Resort. G-Max Cuddle not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Threats: Hyper Beam can severely damage single counters. Last Resort hits hard if unprepared

Tip: Dodge charged moves and focus on consistent DPS to shorten the raid.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 565-612 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 707-765 CP
  • Boosted by: Partly Cloudy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Partly Cloudy weather : boosts Eevee Normal moves.Cloudy weather : boosts Fighting attackers.

Battle Tips

  • Avoid Normal-type Pokémon—they deal less damage.
  • Use fast and strong Fighting moves.

Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, cute Normal attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Max Battle Strategy

  • Use bulky neutral Pokémon to build Max energy.
  • Switch to Fighting-type attackers during Max Phase.
  • Avoid Ghost-type attackers due to immunity.
  • Stack multiple Fighting attackers in small lobbies.
  • Coordinate Max activations for burst chaining.

Shiny Gigantamax Eevee

Yes! Trainers have the chance to encounter a Shiny Eevee after completing a Gigantamax Eevee raid. Shiny Eevee has a slightly darker brown coat compared to the normal version, making it highly sought after by collectors.

The chance of encountering a shiny Pokémon in special raids is higher than normal wild encounters. Participating in multiple raids increases your odds of obtaining this shiny variant.

FAQ

What is Gigantamax Eevee type and weakness?

Eevee is a Normal type. It is weak to Fighting.

What is Gigantamax Eevee’s exclusive move?

Gigantamax Eevee uses the special move G-Max Cuddle. This Normal-type attack deals damage and heals allies during battle.

Can Gigantamax Eevee be shiny?

Yes, but in certain events.

Is Gigantamax Eevee catchable after the raid?

Yes — standard raid catch mechanics apply.

What makes Gigantamax Eevee unique?

In its Gigantamax form, Eevee grows enormous and looks incredibly fluffy, with a charming appearance that makes it stand out among Gigantamax Pokémon.

Is Gigantamax Eevee difficult to defeat?

It is relatively easy to defeat due to its low defensive stats, especially when using Fighting-type counters.
