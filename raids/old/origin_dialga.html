📖 Introduction

Origin Forme Dialga is a powerful 5★ raid boss with Steel and Dragon typing. Its strong resistances and limited weaknesses make it challenging without proper Fighting- and Ground-type counters.

In this guide you will learn about Origin Dialga’s weaknesses, the best Pokémon to use as counters, optimal weather conditions, catch CP ranges, and tips to help you win the raid efficiently.

Tip:Origin Dialga is immune to Poison-type attacks and highly resistant to most other types, so random selections won’t work effectively.

⚔️ Raid Difficulty

Dialga Origin Forme is bulky and can deal significant damage, but it is manageable with proper counters.

  • 2–3 Trainers: Possible with optimized Fighting teams
  • 4 Trainers: Comfortable clear
  • 5+ Trainers: Very safe win

🧠 Origin Dialga Raid Strategy

Origin Forme Dialga is a Steel and Dragon-type Legendary Pokémon known for its high defense and stamina, making it a tanky raid boss.

Unlike some raid bosses, Dialga has limited weaknesses and can survive for a long time in battle.

  • ⚔️ Focus on high DPS Pokémon
  • ⏱️ Expect longer raid duration
  • 🛡️ Avoid weak counters — Dialga is very bulky

🗡️ Best Origin Dialga Counters

The best counters are strong Ground-type and Fighting-type Pokémon.

🥊 Fighting-Type Counters (Top Priority)

Fighting-type Pokémon are typically the most effective option. They deal strong super-effective damage and often have high DPS potential. Fast moves like Counter combined with powerful charged moves can significantly reduce raid time.

  • Use Counter as the primary fast move
  • Charged moves like Aura Sphere or Dynamic Punch maximize output
  • Mega Fighting Pokémon provide team-wide boosts
  • Conkeldurr: (Counter / Dynamic Punch)
  • Hariyama: (Force Palm / Dynamic Punch)
  • Blaziken: (Counter / Aura Sphere)

🌍 Ground-Type Counters (Strong Alternative)

Ground-type Pokémon also deal super-effective damage. High-DPS Ground attackers using Mud Shot and Earthquake perform very well, especially in Sunny weather.

  • Mud Shot for fast energy gain
  • Earthquake for high damage
  • Mega Ground Pokémon boost overall team damage
  • Garchomp: (Mud Shot / Earth Power)
  • Excadrill: (Mud Slap / Scorching Sands)
  • Swampert: (Mud Shot / Earthquake)

⚔️ Why Fighting & Ground Counters Are Best

Origin Dialga is weak to Fighting and Ground-type moves, which deal super effective damage.

  • 🥊 Fighting types provide high DPS (fast damage)
  • 🌍 Ground types are more durable and consistent
  • ⚖️ Combining both gives the best balance

Using other types is not recommended due to Dialga’s strong resistances.

🧠 Raid Strategy & Advanced Tips

1. Avoid Dragon-Type Attackers

Even though Dialga is part Dragon-type, its Steel typing resists Dragon moves. Using Dragon attackers will significantly reduce your damage output.

2. Watch for Draco Meteor

Dialga Origin Forme can deal heavy damage with Dragon-type charged moves. Glass-cannon Fighting Pokémon may faint quickly, so be prepared to relobby if necessary.

3. Coordinate Mega Evolutions

Using a Mega Fighting or Mega Ground Pokémon boosts super-effective damage for your entire team. Coordinating Mega activation helps maintain consistent high DPS.

Origin Dialga’s Steel and Dragon typing gives it particular vulnerabilities to **Fighting** and **Ground** type attacks. Pokémon such as Machamp with Counter and Dynamic Punch, Conkeldurr with Counter and Drain Punch, and Rhyperior with Mud Slap and Earthquake deal strong super-effective damage and can help melt Dialga’s health quickly. These counters not only exploit Origin Dialga’s weaknesses but also hold up well against its charged moves.

Weather conditions can significantly affect how this raid plays out. In Cloudy weather, Fighting attacks receive a weather boost — increasing overall damage from your primary counters. In Rainy weather, Ground moves gain a boost, making counters like Rhyperior and Groudon even more effective. Always check in-game weather before assembling your team to take full advantage of weather boosts.

Origin Dialga’s powerful charged moves such as Draco Meteor and Iron Head hit hard, and if not properly managed, they can quickly knock out your counters. Learning to dodge these heavy hits — especially when your Pokémon are low on health — extends their survivability and allows your team to deal more total damage. In smaller raid lobbies, this tactical dodging becomes even more important.

When planning your team, lead with the highest DPS counters that hit Origin Dialga’s weaknesses. Once these Pokémon begin to faint, rotate in secondary attackers that still deal super-effective damage but have better bulk. In larger raid groups, assigning roles — such as focusing on Fighting counters first, then following with Ground types — can help maintain pressure while keeping your team healthy.

If you have access to Mega Evolutions that support your primary counter types — such as Mega Lucario for Fighting support — using them early in the battle can significantly increase your team’s damage output. With thoughtful team composition, weather-aware strategy, and tactical dodging, you’ll be in a much better position to secure a clean and efficient raid victory.
