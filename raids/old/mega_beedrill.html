Mega Beedrill Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

← Back to All Raid Guides

Mega Beedrill is a fast and dangerous Bug / Poison-type Mega Raid Boss in Pokémon GO. Its high speed and strong moves make it a threat in raids, but careful preparation with the right counters can make it manageable. This guide covers Mega Beedrill’s weaknesses, best counters, movesets, CP ranges, weather boosts, and raid strategies for trainers of all levels.

Introduction

The Mega Evolution of Beedrill makes it a highly aggressive Mega Raid Boss. Its dual Bug and Poison typing allows it to strike fast with moves like Poison Jab and X-Scissor. While it has significant offensive potential, it also carries predictable weaknesses that trainers can exploit.

Fire-, Rock-, and Psychic-type Pokémon deal the most damage against Mega Beedrill. This guide will help you understand which Pokémon to bring, how to build an efficient team, and how weather affects the raid battle.

Whether you are a solo trainer looking for a challenge or part of a small group, this Mega Beedrill raid guide gives everything you need to win efficiently.

🧬 About Mega Beedrill in Pokémon GO

Mega Beedrill is a Bug / Poison-type Pokémon. When Mega Evolved, it becomes one of the highest DPS Bug-type attackers in the game and provides damage bonuses to Bug-type and Poison-type Pokémon in raids.

Mega Beedrill is useful because:

  • It boosts Bug-type and Poison-type attackers
  • It is one of the strongest Bug-type Mega Pokémon
  • It is excellent against Psychic, Dark, and Grass-type raid bosses

Weaknesses & resistances

CategoryDetails
❌ Weak To: Flying • Rock • Fire • Psychic
✅ Resistant To: Poison • Bug • Grass • Fighting • Fairy

Notes: Among these, Flying and Fire types are usually the safest choices because they deal strong damage and resist some of Beedrill’s Bug moves. It also resists Grass, Fighting, Fairy, Bug, and Poison, so avoid those types completely.

🗡️ Best Counters for Mega Beedrill

The best way to defeat Mega Beedrill is to use strong Fire, Flying, Rock, or Psychic-type Pokémon.

Best Mega Evolutions

Recommended Megas to use against Mega Beedrill:

  • Rayquaza, Salamence – Flying boost
  • Charizard Y, Blaziken, Salamence, Charizard X – Fire boost
  • Alakazam, Gallade, Latios, Gardevoir, Metagross - Psychic boost
  • Diancie - Rock boost

Best Moveset

  • Fast: Poison Jab, Poison Sting, Infestation
  • Charged: Aerial Ace, Sludge Bomb, X-Scissor, Fell Stinger
  • Elite: Bug Bite, Drill Run

Pro tip: Its Sludge Bomb in particular can delete Fairy and Grass attackers instantly, and even neutral hits hurt because of its massive attack stat.

CP Ranges

After defeating Mega Beedrill, you will catch a normal Beedrill.

  • Perfect (L20): 1,846-1,933 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,308-2,416 CP
  • Boosted by: Rainy & Cloudy

🌦️ Weather Boost

Mega Beedrill gets a weather boost in:

  • Rainy → boosts Bug
  • Cloudy → boosts Poison

Your counters get boosted in:

  • Sunny → boosts Fire
  • Windy → boosts Flying & Psychic
  • Partly Cloudy → boosts Rock

Revive & Potion Cost

  • Average revives used: 8–14
  • Average potions used: 10–18
  • Dodging significantly reduces resource usage
  • Using Mega Pokémon reduces total relobbies

Common Raid Mistakes

Avoid using fragile Water or Ice attackers—they'll often be outpaced by Beedrill's high damage. Also be cautious with Ghost types if Beedrill runs Crunch.

Best Team Compositions

Small Group (3-4 Trainers)

With strong Rock, Fire-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Mega Beedrill can be defeated by 3 to 4 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (5-7 Trainers)

A group of 5 to 7 trainers with decent counters should have no trouble defeating Mega Beedrill.

Large Group (7+ Trainers)

Mega Beedrill becomes very easy with a large group, but avoid overfilling the lobby to save raid passes.

Raid Strategy

  • Because of its low defense, Mega Beedrill faints quickly.
  • Use Flying, Rock, Fire, or Psychic attackers.
  • Dodging is usually not necessary.
  • You can easily duo or even solo with top counters.

Can Mega Beedrill Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes, trainers can encounter a Shiny Beedrill after defeating Mega Beedrill in a raid. While Mega Beedrill itself cannot be caught, the regular Beedrill you encounter after the raid may be shiny.

Shiny Beedrill features a brighter yellow color compared to the regular version, making it highly desirable for collectors and competitive trainers alike.

Participating in multiple raids increases the chances of obtaining a shiny Beedrill, as Mega Raids generally have higher shiny rates than wild encounters.

FAQ

Can Mega Beedrill be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Shiny Beedrill has been released in raids or events.

Is Mega Beedrill difficult to defeat?

It has extremely high attack and speed but very low defenses. Using type-advantage counters ensures it is defeated quickly before it can strike repeatedly.

What makes Mega Beedrill unique?

Its needle-like arms, incredible speed, and dual Bug/Poison typing make Mega Beedrill visually striking and strategically dangerous in Max Raid Battles.

Does Mega Beedrill keep its Mega form after the battle?

Mega Evolution lasts only during battles where Mega is enabled; it does not persist outside combat.

What is Mega Beedrill’s exclusive move?

Mega Beedrill can use moves like Poison Jab and X-Scissor, dealing massive damage with very high speed in Max Raid Battles.

🏁 Conclusion

Mega Beedrill is a glass cannon Mega raid boss. With the right Flying, Fire, Rock, or Psychic attackers, it will go down extremely fast.
