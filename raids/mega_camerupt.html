Mega Camerupt Raid Guide — Best Counters, Teams & Strategy

Mega Camerupt is a bulky Fire/Ground-type Mega that can make raids feel like a volcanic slog if you aren’t prepared. This guide breaks down the best counters, ideal teams, moveset analysis, weather interactions, and actionable tips to help you beat Mega Camerupt efficiently — written in an easy-to-digest gaming-blog style so you can get into raids fast.

Last updated: October 24, 2025

Mega Camerupt — Quick Overview

Mega Camerupt brings Fire and Ground damage together, often packing hard-hitting Fire charge moves and powerful Ground moves. Its typing makes it vulnerable to Water and Ground (double effective), while it resists Fire, Electric and Poison. In raids it tends to be tanky rather than glassy, so sustained DPS and correct typing matter more than burst. Expect a fight that rewards Rock and Water coverage depending on the exact moveset.

Pro tip: Because Mega Camerupt resists Fire, pure Fire attackers perform poorly — don’t bring them unless you need niche coverage. Prioritize Water and Ground counters or Rock where available.

Battle Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Check the charged move before the raid to finalize counters — knowing whether Earthquake or a Fire nuke is coming changes swap decisions.
  2. Lead with your highest DPS Water or Ground — get quick energy and pressure while shields are often unused early.
  3. Manage shields in smaller groups — use them to protect your top Water attacker from an incoming Earth Power or Magma Storm.
  4. Switch to bulkier Ground types if Camerupt uses multiple heavy Earth moves — some Ground counters can eat those hits and keep DPS rolling.
  5. Use Mega Swampert (if available) when the raid benefits from boosted Water damage — Mega can shorten the fight dramatically.
  6. Coordinate with teammates and call out if you need to rejoin after revives to keep the raid tempo high.

If Camerupt begins to spam Earth Power/Earthquake, swapping to a bulky Ground attacker with high HP can actually stabilize the raid long enough for DPS Pokémon to charge their moves again.

IV / Level & Resource Advice

For raid counters, aim for high-level Pokémon with the correct movesets rather than perfect IVs. Prioritize powering one or two Kyogre/Swampert at a time. If you’re resource-constrained, power a single Swampert with Hydro Cannon and a reliable Gyarados to cover most scenarios.

Weather Effects & Their Impact

Weather shifts affect which counters are optimal:

  • Rain — boosts Water attackers (Kyogre, Gyarados, Manaphy) and is ideal for Camerupt raids.
  • Sunny/Clear — boosts Fire (not helpful for you) and slightly favors Camerupt, so avoid joining on sunny boost unless you have very strong Water counters.
  • Windy — boosts Dragon (no major effect here).
  • Partly Cloudy — boosts Rock; may help Tyranitar alternatives if you're bringing Rock counters for mixed move-sets.

Check weather before joining — a Rain-boosted raid is the easiest way to shorten the fight.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Bringing pure Fire attackers — Camerupt resists Fire and will punish you.
  • Ignoring the charged move — swapping choices can change the entire fight.
  • Overloading on weak Water Pokémon — balance DPS with bulk to survive heavy Ground moves.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is Mega Camerupt weak to Water?

A: Yes — Water is a primary weakness and generally the best type to bring. High-DPS Water attackers like Kyogre or Hydro Cannon Swampert are top choices.

Q: Can I solo Mega Camerupt?

A: Soloing is challenging due to Camerupt's bulk. Only highly geared trainers with multiple maxed Water/Ground attackers should attempt solo. Small groups of 2–3 strong trainers are much more realistic.

Q: Which Mega is best to bring against Camerupt?

A: Mega Swampert or any Mega that supports Water/Ground damage will be most helpful. Mega choices depend on which Mega you have and whether it increases the raid's effective DPS.

Q: Does weather dramatically change the recommended counters?

A: Yes — Rain increases the power of Water attackers, making them even more effective. Avoid joining on Sunny/Clear unless you have very strong counters.

Q: Should I prioritize Hydro Cannon on Swampert?

A: Absolutely — Hydro Cannon is a Community Day move and massively improves Swampert's raid performance vs Camerupt. If you have it, use it.

Moves & Threats

Camerupt common fast moves are Rock Smash or Ember or Incinerate. Charged moves include Earthquake or Earth Power or Overheat and Solar Beam

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,759-1,840 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,199-2,300 CP
  • Boosted by: Sunny

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Sunny weather: boosts Ground type attackers. Rainy weather : boosts Water type attackers. Partly Cloudy weather : boosts Rock type attackers.

If Mega Camerupt opens with a fast-charging special move (like Eruption), switch to a Water-type to soak the hit and retaliate quickly.

Battle Tips

  • Prioritize multi bar water type charge moves.
  • If weather is sunny, faint rate change - bring more water type or ground type attackers
  • Bring a squad of high water type attackers.
  • Use dodge sparingly - focus on sustained DPS and shields if in a smaller group.

When to avoid

Avoid using fragile Fire or Grass attackers that will faint quickly to Camerupt’s strong Fire- and Ground-type moves. Also be cautious with Ice attackers if Camerupt carries Rock coverage.

Duo & Trio Possibility

A duo is possible with maxed-out counters and coordination. A trio is comfortable for mid-level players. Casual groups should bring 4–5 trainers.

While Camerupt isn’t top tier in PvP, it’s a fun spice pick. Mega Camerupt shines more in raids and PvE content.

FAQ

Can Mega Camerupt be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Not currently. Shiny Camerupt has not yet been released in raids or events.

What are best moveset of Camerupt?

Best moveset of Camerupt are Incinerate which does damage of 32 under Fast Attack and Solar Beam of 180 under Charged Attack.

Are Shadow Pokémon better?

Yes—Shadow forms have +20% attack, making them excellent choices if available.

What is the best weather to raid Mega Camerupt?

Sunny weather boosts Camerupt fire and ground type moves, boosts Ground attackers. Rainy weather boosts Water type moves. Partly Cloudy weather boosts Rock moves.

Does Mega Camerupt keep its Mega form after the battle?

Mega Evolution lasts only during battles where Mega is enabled; it does not persist outside combat.

Conclusion

Mega Camerupt is not the strongest Mega, but it’s easy to raid thanks to multiple weaknesses. It’s worth raiding for its shiny form, Mega Energy, and for filling your Mega Pokédex. With Fire, Ground counters, you’ll defeat it with ease.
