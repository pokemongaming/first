Gigantamax Eevee — Quick Overview

Gigantamax Eevee is presented here as a hypothetical Pokémon GO raid boss with an enlarged HP pool and boosted damage output (consistent with your site’s other Gigantamax guides). Eevee’s typing is variable depending on which evolution it might mimic during the event—however, the most common Gigantamax Eevee raid variant behaves like a Normal-type base with multi-type move coverage pulled from its evolutions (Vaporeon, Jolteon, Flareon, Espeon, Umbreon, Leafeon, Glaceon, Sylveon). That makes it unpredictable but beatable with the right counters and planning.

Pro tip: Treat Gigantamax Eevee as a flexible multi-coverage boss — bring generic neutral-damage tanks plus targeted super-effective attackers based on the charged move preview.

Battle Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Preview the charged move shown before battle — this single piece of information should determine your lead and immediate swaps.
  2. Lead with high-DPS Ground/Electric/Water depending on preview. Early shields are common — push damage while shields are available.
  3. Manage shields in small groups — save at least one for a late heavy charged move that can wipe your primary DPS.
  4. Rotate bulky counters (Rhyperior, Groudon) to soak heavy charges while glass-cannon attackers recharge.
  5. Use charge moves strategically — time them when the opponent is close to using a shield or in an energy window for maximum value.
  6. Coordinate Megas or weather boosts (if available) to maximize raid-wide damage — but do not depend on them exclusively for clears.
  7. Revive and stagger returns — don’t all rejoin instantly after a wipe; wait for a safe window to preserve the DPS momentum.

If you’re short on ideal counters, have one high-level specialist (Groudon or Tapu Koko) and protect it with shields — preserving that single large DPS dealer often matters more than many mediocre ones.

Weather Effects & Their Impact

  • Rain: Boosts Water moves (Vaporeon variants stronger) — avoid if you rely on Electric counters unless you have Ground.
  • Sunny/Clear: Boosts Fire moves (Flareon variants get stronger) — pick Water counters.
  • Windy: Boosts Dragon and Flying moves (Glaceon unlikely affected) — be cautious if Dragon moves appear.
  • Partly Cloudy / Overcast: Neutral effect for most Eeveelutions but can benefit Rock/Fairy where applicable.

Always adapt your team to weather — a small switch (one or two counters) often reduces required player count significantly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Bringing only one type of counter when Eevee may use mixed moves — diversify where possible.
  • Wasting shields too early in small parties — keep at least one shield for a late heavy nuke.
  • Over-relying on glass-cannon attackers without backup tanks — preserve a bulky swap-in to stabilize heavy charge spam.
  • Not checking weather — boosts can swing a match significantly and change ideal counters.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is Gigantamax Eevee weak to a single type?

A: No — Gigantamax Eevee is best treated as a multi-coverage boss. Use the charged-move preview to decide whether to field Electric, Ground, Water, Psychic, Dark, Ice or Fairy counters.

Q: Can Gigantamax Eevee be soloed?

A: Soloing a Gigantamax boss is difficult due to the HP pool. Top-level trainers with multiple maxed counters and perfect movesets may attempt it, but typical realistic clears are 2–5 trainers depending on roster strength.

Q: Should I spend Candy XL on an Eeveelution?

A: Only if you intend to use the evolution at max level repeatedly (for example, for PvP Master League or as a specialized raid counter). Otherwise prioritize powering legendaries and top raid machines first.

Q: What’s the best generic fallback team if I don’t know the moveset?

A: A safe fallback is to bring 2 Ground (Groudon/Excadrill/Swampert), 1 Electric (Tapu Koko or Xurkitree), 1 Water (Gyarados/Swampert), and 1 Psychic (Mewtwo) — this covers the broadest set of likely moves.

Q: Will weather make or break my raid?

A: Weather can significantly shorten or lengthen fights. Prioritize joining under beneficial weather (e.g., Rain for Water counters if you plan to use them) or avoid boosts that help the boss’ moves.

Moves & Threats

Eevee common fast moves are Quick Attack or Tackle. Charged moves include Dig or Swift. Elite moves include Body Slam or Last Resort. G-Max Cuddle not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Why Raid Gigantamax Eevee?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, cute Normal attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Since Eevee is purely Normal-type, Fighting-type Pokémon are the only true counters. Bring a team full of strong Fighting-types to finish this raid quickly. Avoid Ghost-types, as they deal no damage with their Ghost moves.
