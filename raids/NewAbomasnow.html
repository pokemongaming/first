Mega Abomasnow has access to fast moves like Razor Leaf, Powder Snow and Leafage, both of which can pressure players differently. Strategy

The most important factor in defeating Mega Abomasnow is maximizing Fire-type damage. Because of its double weakness, strong Fire-type attackers such as Mega Charizard, Mega Blaziken, or Reshiram can deal extremely high damage per second (DPS). Prioritize Pokémon with powerful Fire-type fast and charged moves rather than mixed-type sets. If Sunny weather is active, Fire-type attacks receive a weather boost, significantly reducing the time required to defeat the raid boss. In contrast, Snowy weather will boost Abomasnow’s Ice-type attacks, increasing incoming damage. During Snowy conditions, make sure your team has strong resistances and avoid fragile glass-cannon attackers unless you can dodge consistently. For lower-level trainers, coordinating with at least 3–5 players is recommended. High-level trainers with optimized Fire-type teams may complete the raid with fewer players, but group play ensures faster clears and more Mega Energy rewards. Dodging charged Ice-type moves such as Blizzard can greatly extend your team’s survival time. If using Mega Evolutions, prioritize one Mega Fire-type to boost all Fire-type allies on the battlefield for maximum efficiency. Overall, Mega Abomasnow is considered one of the more manageable Mega raids due to its double weakness. With proper Fire-type preparation and awareness of weather conditions, this raid can be completed smoothly even without a full lobby.

Mega Abomasnow is extremely vulnerable to Ice and Rock-type attackers due to its Dragon/Flying typing. This makes high-DPS Ice-type Pokémon the best overall choice, especially those with strong fast moves like Draco Meteor paired with Outrage. Top-tier counters include Mega Abomasnow and Mega Glalie for their Mega Boost effects, which increase damage output for all players in the raid. Shadow Articuno stands out due to its incredible raw damage, while Aurorus offers a strong balance between bulk and attack. Glaceon and Mewtwo are excellent alternatives when built with Ice movesets respectively.

When building your team, prioritize Pokémon with both type advantage and strong movesets. Avoid using Water, Electric, or Grass-type Pokémon, as they deal reduced damage and can struggle to survive against Abomasnow charged attacks. A well-optimized team will significantly reduce raid time and increase your chances of success, especially in smaller groups.

Raid Difficulty & Minimum Players

Mega Abomasnow is considered a moderately easy Mega Raid due to its double weakness to Fire-type attacks. However, its access to powerful moves like Blizzard can make the battle challenging if not properly prepared.

Difficulty Breakdown

  • Solo: Not possible due to raid timer and high HP.
  • Duo: Possible but very challenging. Requires Level 40+ trainers using top-tier Fire-type counters like Reshiram or Mega Blaziken with optimal movesets.
  • Trio: Comfortable for experienced players. With strong Fire-type teams, the raid can be completed with time to spare.
  • 4–6 Players: Easy and consistent. Recommended for most players to ensure a safe win.
  • 6+ Players: Very easy. Even mixed teams can complete the raid without difficulty.

What Affects Difficulty?

  • Weather: Sunny weather boosts Fire-type attacks (easier), while Snowy weather boosts the boss (harder).
  • Moveset: Blizzard is the most dangerous move and can quickly knock out fragile attackers.
  • Team Composition: Using Mega Evolutions significantly increases total team damage.
  • Trainer Level: Higher-level players with powered-up Pokémon will perform much better.

Time-to-Win Insight: A strong duo can finish with little margin, while a trio or larger group ensures a comfortable victory without time pressure.

Mega Raid Mechanics

Mega Raids work differently from standard raids. Instead of catching the Mega form directly, players earn Mega Energy after defeating the boss. This energy is then used to Mega Evolve Abomasnow. The faster the raid is completed, the more Mega Energy you receive. This makes using strong counters and coordinated teams extremely important.

Mega Abomasnow vs Other Grass/Ice Raid Bosses

Compared to other Grass/Ice-type raid bosses like Mega Glalie, Mega Abomasnow is more attack-focused but less bulky. This makes it easier to defeat but more punishing if you fail to dodge charged moves.

Why Raid Mega Abomasnow?

Raiding Mega Abomasnow allows players to collect Mega Energy, which is essential for Mega Evolution. Mega Evolutions provide powerful attack boosts to your team in raids and are useful for both PvE and events. Additionally, players have the chance to catch a high-IV or shiny Abomasnow, making this raid valuable for collectors and competitive players alike.

Best Team Composition

A strong team should focus on Fire-type Pokémon, as they deal the most damage to Mega Abomasnow. Mega Evolutions such as Mega Blaziken can boost the entire team's Fire-type attacks. Avoid using Grass, Water, Electric-type Pokémon, as they deal reduced damage and may faint quickly against the boss. If you mission is to complete the raid as soon as possible then best team would be Mega Blaziken or Mega Charizard Y (which will boost entire team), Reshiram, Blacephalon, Heatran, Delphox. If you are new players then budget counter will be Heatran, Charizard Y, Chandelure, Flareon, Blaziken.

The key to defeating Mega Abomasnow efficiently lies in maximizing Fire-type damage output. High DPS attackers should be prioritized over bulky Pokémon, as the goal is to finish the raid quickly rather than sustain damage over time. Using Mega Evolution strategically provides a team-wide boost, increasing overall raid efficiency. Additionally, players should relobby instead of reviving fainted Pokémon to maintain consistent damage output.

Real Raid Experience

In real battles, Mega Abomasnow often defeats players through consistent damage rather than sudden attacks. Fragile Pokémon tend to faint quickly, reducing overall performance.

Balanced attackers like Blacephalon or Delphox perform better because they survive longer and deal steady damage throughout the raid.
