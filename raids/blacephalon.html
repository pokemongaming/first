Blacephalon Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

← Back to All Raid Guides

Blacephalon is a Fire / Ghost-type Ultra Beast Raid Boss in Pokémon GO. With its unique typing, it has several key vulnerabilities, particularly to Dark, Ghost, and Water-type moves. This makes planning your counters crucial for an efficient raid.

In this Blacephalon raid guide, you will find everything you need to defeat this Ultra Beast, including weaknesses, best counters, movesets, catch CP ranges, shiny information, and expert raid strategies for both beginners and experienced trainers.

Introduction

Blacephalon is an Ultra Beast that brings unique challenges to Pokémon GO raids. Its Fire / Ghost typing gives it strong offensive moves and unusual resistances, making it a dangerous opponent for unprepared trainers.

However, by understanding Blacephalon’s weaknesses and selecting the right counters, trainers can defeat it efficiently. This guide covers recommended Pokémon, optimal movesets, raid strategies, shiny availability, and tips for solo or group raids.

Whether you are a new trainer or an experienced player, this guide will help you maximize your rewards and capture Blacephalon safely.

Difficulty

While Blacephalon’s HP is moderate, its damage is deadly. A single poorly-timed Mind Blown or Shadow Ball can faint multiple attackers at once. For small groups, shields and dodge timing are essential. For larger groups, the challenge is less about survival and more about optimizing DPS and avoiding wasted moves.

Best Moveset

  • Fast: Astonish, Incinerate
  • Charged: Shadow Ball, Mystical Fire, Overheat
  • Elite: Mind Blown

Dangerous Moves: Mind Blown and Overheat can KO attackers very quickly.

Blacephalon is more dangerous when it uses Electric-type charged moves like Wild Charge or Fusion Bolt, especially against Water- or Flying-type counters.

CP Ranges

Understanding CP ranges helps trainers evaluate the strength of Blacephalon when caught from raids.

  • Perfect (L20): 1,797-1,884 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,246-2,355 CP
  • Boosted by: Sunny & Foggy

Keep in mind that weather-boosted Blacephalon is significantly harder to defeat, so always prepare strong Ice, Dragon, Fairy, or Ground-type counters.

Weather Boost Effects

  • Foggy weather: boosts Ghost and Dark type attackers.
  • Rainy weather : boosts Water type attackers.
  • Partly Cloudy weather : boosts Rock type attackers.

Revive & Potion Cost

  • Average revives used: 8–14
  • Average potions used: 10–18
  • Dodging significantly reduces resource usage
  • Using Mega Pokémon reduces total relobbies

Common Raid Mistakes

  • Not bringing a Mega Pokémon for team boosts
  • Using weather-boosted Pokémon of the wrong type
  • Relobbying too late and losing damage time
  • Ignoring dodging during hard-hitting moves

Best Team Compositions

Small Group (3–4 Trainers)

With strong Ghost, Dark, or Water-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Blacephalon can be defeated by 3 to 4 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (5–7 Trainers)

A group of 5 to 7 trainers with decent counters should have no trouble defeating Blacephalon.

Large Group (8+ Trainers)

Blacephalon becomes very easy with a large group, but avoid overfilling the lobby to save raid passes.

Raid Strategy

  • Blacephalon is extremely fragile — high DPS teams destroy it fast.
  • Rock and Water types are the safest and strongest choices.
  • Dodge Overheat and Shadow Ball if possible.
  • Relobby quickly — it goes down fast but also hits hard.
  • 2–3 strong players can easily defeat it.

Can Blacephalon Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes! After defeating Blacephalon in a raid, trainers have a chance to encounter a Shiny Blacephalon. Shiny Blacephalon has a slightly different color palette than its regular form, making it a highly sought-after Pokémon for collectors.

While the shiny chance is generally low, participating in multiple raids increases your odds of obtaining this rare variant.

Why Raid Blacephalon?

Blacephalon is primarily a collector’s raid. Trainers focus on catching its shiny form and earning Ultra Beast candy for powering up favorite counters. Its extreme power in raids also makes it a fun challenge for high-level trainers who want a short but intense battle.

PvP Relevance

While Blacephalon isn’t top tier in PvP, it’s a fun spice pick. Blacephalon shines more in raids and PvE content.

FAQ

Can Blacephalon be shiny in Pokémon GO?

Shiny Blacephalon availability depends on special events or future raid rotations in Pokémon GO.

What are best moveset of Blacephalon?

Best moveset of Blacephalon are Incinerate under Fast Attack and Overheat under Charged Attack.

How many trainers are needed to defeat Blacephalon?

2–4 trainers with strong Dark, Ghost, or Rock-type counters can defeat Blacephalon efficiently.

Is Blacephalon a difficult raid boss?

Blacephalon has extremely high attack but very low defense. With proper counters, experienced trainers can defeat it quickly.

Conclusion

The battle against Blacephalon is all about precision and type advantage. Use Rock-type DPS, dodge or shield the big hits, and the fight is over in minutes. Even casual players can clear this raid efficiently if they respect its glass-cannon nature.
