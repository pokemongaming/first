Gigantamax Copperajah Raid Guide – Best Counters, Weakness, CP & Strategy

Gigantamax Copperajah is a massive Steel-type raid boss in Pokémon GO. With its high defense and heavy-hitting moves, it can be challenging for small groups of trainers. However, careful planning and the right counters make this raid manageable.

This guide covers everything you need to defeat Gigantamax Copperajah efficiently, including the best counters, weaknesses, movesets, CP ranges, weather boosts, shiny availability, and raid strategies suitable for both beginners and experienced trainers.

Introduction

Gigantamax Copperajah is the Dynamax form of Copperajah introduced in special raids. Its enormous size and powerful Steel-type moves make it one of the toughest Tier 5 raid bosses. Players must plan their team carefully to avoid wasting time or raid passes.

Despite its tanky defenses, Copperajah’s Steel typing makes it vulnerable to Fire, Ground, and Fighting moves. Using strong counters of these types allows trainers to defeat the raid efficiently, even with fewer players. Moves & Threats

Copperajah common fast moves are Tackle or Rock Smash. Charged moves include Heavy Slam or Play Rough or Earthquake and Stone Edge. G-Max Steelsurge not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Threats: Heavy Slam deals big damage to low-defense Pokémon, while Play Rough can hit hard against certain counters.

Tip: Dodge charged moves whenever possible to keep your team alive longer.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,622-1,702 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,028-2,128 CP
  • Boosted by: Snow

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects

Cloudy weather : boosts Fighting moves. Sunny/Clear weather: boosts Ground and Fire attackers.

Battle Tips

  • Avoid Steel-types—they’re ineffective.
  • Use fast Fire or Fighting attacks to exploit weaknesses

Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Rewards

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Steel attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

When To Avoid

Avoid normal, flying, rock, bug Pokémon — they take heavy damage from Copperajah moves.

Max Battle Strategy

  • Build Max energy using bulky neutral attackers.
  • Switch to Fire-type attackers during Max Phase.
  • Avoid Fairy, Ice, and Grass attackers due to resistance.
  • Coordinate Max activation in small groups for burst chaining.
  • Ground-type attackers are excellent secondary options.

Can Gigantamax Copperajah Be Shiny in Pokémon GO?

Yes! After defeating Gigantamax Copperajah in a raid, trainers can encounter a Shiny Copperajah. Shiny Copperajah has a darker greenish-gray body compared to the normal version, making it highly sought after by collectors.

While the Gigantamax form itself cannot be caught, regular Copperajah caught after the raid has a chance of being shiny. Participating in multiple raids increases the chance to obtain the shiny variant.

What is Gigantamax Copperajah type and weakness?

Copperajah is a Steel type. It is weak to Fighting, Ground, Fire.

What is Gigantamax Copperajah’s exclusive move?

Gigantamax Copperajah uses the special move G-Max Steelsurge. This Steel-type attack deals damage and scatters sharp steel spikes on the battlefield.
