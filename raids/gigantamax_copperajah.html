Overview

Copperajah is a pure Steel-typed Pokémon with a Ground variant in some contexts (regional forms / event moves). Its Gigantamax form significantly increases its HP and often grants heavy physical STABs — think high-damage Steel/Ground moves with wide coverage. In GO, Copperajah’s Steel typing makes it resistant to many common types but vulnerable to Fire and Ground depending on boss moves. In SWSH Max Raids, its moveset variety and dynamax scaling make it a tank that rewards correct typing and sustained DPS.

Pro tip: Heavy Physical attackers with super-effective Ground or Fire moves are your best bet — but prioritize survival and repeatable damage in G-Max fights over one-shot glass-cannons.

DPS, Energy & Dynamax / Gigantamax Notes

Pokémon GO: G-Max fights have very large HP pools — fast-move EPS (Mud Shot, Fire Spin) and high-value charges (Overheat, Precipice Blades) are decisive. Hydro/Fire combos can reduce required players. Hold your best charges for windows after shields are down.

Sword & Shield: Dynamax/Gigantamax changes move power and HP. Use Max Moves strategically: Max Flare / Max Quake can break or bait protective mechanics. Supports that stall (Leech Seed / Recover) help your DPS get more turns of damage in extended fights.

Pro tip: In GO, a high-level Reshiram or Heatran often reduces the required player count more than several mediocre attackers because Fire is a core super-effective type vs Steel.

Step-by-step Strategy

  1. Preview charged moves: Lock in Fire if Steel moves dominate; lock in Ground if Earthquake/Bulldoze appear often.
  2. Lead with energy generators: Use Mud Shot / Fire Spin leads to build energy for high-value charges.
  3. Shield economy (GO): Bait shields with medium charges; save at least one shield in small groups for a final nuke.
  4. Swap & tank: Use Rhyperior or Groudon to absorb predictable heavy charges and rotate back to DPS.
  5. Dynamax timing (SWSH): Use Max Moves to break defensive stances or to set up huge turns once enemy shields/mechanics are baited.
  6. Revive policy: Stagger revives and rejoin in safe windows to avoid faint waves.

In small groups coordinate one player to preserve a shield (GO) or hold their Max Move (SWSH) for the crucial late-phase damage — that single saved resource often flips the result.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What's the single best counter to Gigantamax Copperajah?

A: Reshiram (Fire Fang / Overheat) is the single best universal counter for GO due to Fire’s advantage vs Steel. For Ground coverage, Groudon (Precipice Blades) is critical when Earthquake or Bulldoze appears.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Copperajah?

A: Soloing Gigantamax bosses is extremely difficult because of their enlarged HP pools. Only the most optimized players with multiple maxed legendaries and proper weather/dynamax timing might attempt it. Realistic clears are teams of 2–5 depending on roster and weather.

Q: Which TMs should I prioritize?

A: Overheat / Flamethrower (for Reshiram/Heatran), Precipice Blades (Groudon if available), Head Smash / Stone Edge for Rock users (if you need backup) and Drill Run / Earthquake for Ground picks.

Q: Should I wait for weather?

A: In GO, Sunny/Clear (boosting Fire) is not helpful here; instead, neutral or slightly beneficial weather depends on your counters (Rain for Water if used, Sunny boosts Fire but also increases boss Fire damage). Don’t wait unnecessarily unless you and teammates have ideal weather-boosted counters.

Moves & Threats

Copperajah common fast moves are Tackle or Rock Smash. Charged moves include Heavy Slam or Play Rough or Earthquake and Stone Edge. G-Max Steelsurge not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Copperajah?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Steel attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

When To Avoid

Avoid normal, flying, rock, bug Pokémon — they take heavy damage from Copperajah moves.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Gigantamax Copperajah can hit hard with coverage moves like Earthquake and Play Rough, so avoid bringing Pokémon weak to Ground or Fairy when possible. Use Fire and Fighting types as your main DPS. In smaller groups, prioritize high-level counters; in larger groups, mix types for balance.
