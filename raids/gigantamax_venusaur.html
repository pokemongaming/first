What is Gigantamax Venusaur?

Gigantamax Venusaur is a special transformation of Venusaur introduced in Pokémon Sword and Shield. Unlike standard Dynamax forms, Gigantamax Pokémon change both appearance and signature moves. When Venusaur Gigantamaxes, it gains a massive flower canopy that releases toxic pollen across the battlefield.

Signature Move: G-Max Vine Lash

Gigantamax Venusaur replaces its Grass-type moves with:

  • G-Max Vine Lash:
    • Deals Grass-type damage
    • Applies residual damage for 4 turns to all non-Grass opponents
    • Extremely powerful in raids and PvE scenarios

Typing & Stats Overview

  • Type: Grass / Poison
  • Weaknesses: Fire, Flying, Ice, Psychic
  • Resistances: Water, Electric, Grass, Fighting, Fairy

Role in Pokémon GO

As a Raid Boss

  • Difficulty: Medium–Hard depending on counters and weather

Strengths

  • Bulky and hard to take down quickly

Weaknesses

  • Double weakness to none
  • Easily countered by strong Fire- and Psychic-type attackers

Weather Boost

Shiny Comparison

  • Shiny Venusaur: Slightly darker green body with a more yellowish flower

Gigantamax Mechanics

Here’s a clear, practical explanation of how Gigantamax mechanics work in raids, using Gigantamax Venusaur as the reference.

What is Gigantamax in Raids?

Gigantamax is a special raid mechanic where certain Pokémon:

  • Become massive in size
  • Gain a unique G-Max move
  • Have enhanced battle effects beyond normal raids

G-Max Moves

G-Max Vine Lash

  • Replaces all Grass-type attacks
  • Deals:
    • Initial damage
    • Damage-over-time (DoT) for several seconds/turns

Damage Over Time (DoT) System

Unlike regular raids:

  • You take continuous passive damage
  • It affects all players simultaneously
  • Cannot be fully avoided

Extended Battle Duration

Gigantamax bosses have:

  • Huge HP pools
  • Strong survivability

Large Group Requirement

  • Designed for many players at once
  • Typical requirement:
    • Minimum: ~10 players
    • Ideal: 15–30+ players

Dynamax / Gigantamax Synergy

When you use Dynamax or Gigantamax Pokémon:

  • Their moves become Max Moves
  • These moves deal:
    • Higher damage
    • Sometimes additional effects

Survivability Matters More

Because of DoT:

  • Glass cannon teams fail quickly
  • Bulky Pokémon become valuable

Relobby Pressure

Due to constant damage:

  • Teams faint frequently
  • You must:
    • Rejoin quickly
    • Use pre-built teams

Gigantamax Venusaur – FAQ

Here are the most common questions about Gigantamax Venusaur raids, with clear and practical answers.

What is Gigantamax Venusaur?

Gigantamax Venusaur is a special raid form where Venusaur becomes extremely large and gains a unique G-Max Vine Lash attack that deals damage over time.

Is Gigantamax Venusaur rare?

Yes. Gigantamax Pokémon are usually:

  • Event-based
  • Limited-time raid bosses
  • Not always available in rotation

How hard is the raid?

  • Difficulty: High
  • Requires: 10–30+ players

Harder than normal and Mega raids due to:

  • Huge HP
  • Constant damage pressure

Can I solo Gigantamax Venusaur?

No, Even strong players cannot solo it due to:

  • Massive HP pool
  • DoT damage (G-Max Vine Lash)
  • Multi-player scaling design

What are the best counters?

Best types:

  • Fire
  • Flying
  • Psychic
  • Ice

What weather is best for raids?

  • Windy → Best for counters
  • Snowy → Good for Ice attackers
  • Sunny → Makes Venusaur stronger (avoid)

What CP should I look for?

  • Normal: 1480 – 1554
  • Weather boosted: 1851 – 1943
  • Hundo: 1554 / 1943

Can Gigantamax Venusaur be shiny?

Yes:

  • Shiny rate: ~1 in 128
  • Guaranteed catch if shiny appears

What is G-Max Vine Lash?

A special Gigantamax move that:

  • Deals immediate damage
  • Applies damage over time (DoT)
  • Affects all players in the raid

Why is the raid so difficult?

Because of:

  • Very high HP
  • Constant DoT damage
  • Strong charged moves like Frenzy Plant
  • Large group requirement

How many players do I need?

  • Minimum: ~10
  • Recommended: 15–20
  • Ideal: 20–30+

What Pokémon should I avoid?

  • Grass-types (double resisted)
  • Weak Water/Electric attackers
  • Low-level glass cannons
