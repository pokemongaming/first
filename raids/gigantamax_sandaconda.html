Gigantamax Sandaconda — Quick Overview

Sandaconda in Gigantamax form is presented here as a beefy Ground-type raid boss with boosted HP and slightly augmented damage output to mirror the rest of your Gigantamax series. Expect it to use high-power Ground-type charged moves (e.g., Earthquake, Sand Tomb variants) and occasional Rock or Poison coverage depending on event variants. Its big HP pool favors sustained, energy-efficient attackers; fights often reward consistent fast-move energy generation and high-value charged moves rather than glass-cannon bursts that get shielded away.

Pro tip: Treat Gigantamax Sandaconda like an endurance contest: energy-per-second (EPS) and charge-move value are more important than single-hit DPS in long fights.

DPS & Energy Considerations (Showdown-style logic)

For Gigantamax raids, treat fights like long simulated battles: EPS (energy per second) and charge-move value matter. Here’s a quick checklist to apply competitive reasoning:

  • Fast-move EPS: High EPS (e.g., Mud Shot on Ground attackers — but don't bring Ground here) is valuable only if your charged moves align with super-effective types. For Water attackers, Waterfall (or higher EPS moves) combined with high-value charge moves (Origin Pulse, Hydro Cannon) is ideal.
  • Charge-move value: A single Origin Pulse or Hydro Cannon does more sustained damage vs a Gigantamax HP pool than several small niche charges — treat those as your windows to close HP quickly.
  • Shield efficiency: Bait shields with medium charges so your big charges land into unshielded windows. Don't waste an Origin Pulse into a shield — use it when you can reasonably expect it to connect.
  • Swap economy: Minimize swap losses. Each faint or wasted swap costs DPS and increases RaidTime; use bulky swap-ins to soak predictable moves, then swap back to high DPS for windows.

In two-player clears, a Kyogre + Hydro Cannon Swampert core outperforms many other combos due to high EPS and efficient charge-move economy.

Battle Strategy — Step by Step (Showdown-minded)

  1. Pre-raid scouting: Check the charged-move preview and assign roles: lead (fast DPS), tank (bulky swap), closer (big charge user).
  2. Lead strategy: If the preview shows Earthquake or Sand Tomb, lead with Kyogre or Swampert to build early energy and force shields.
  3. Shield economy: Bait with a medium charge and preserve at least one shield in small groups for the late stage when your primary DPS needs protection.
  4. Swap timing: If the boss charges a heavy move, rotate in a bulky swap (Tapu Fini, Rhyperior if brought as filler) to absorb then switch back to DPS.
  5. Charge windows: Use your highest-value charge moves when boss energy is low or when you’ve baited the last shield — Origin Pulse and Hydro Cannon are your finishers.
  6. Revive coordination: Stagger revives and returns to avoid simultaneous faint waves — let a safe window open first.

When in doubt, communicate: ask teammates to hold one shield or to swap to a specific counter. A single coordinated swap often saves the raid.

Weather & Mega Notes

  • Rain: Boosts Water — ideal for Kyogre/Swampert teams; reduces required players significantly.
  • Sunny/Clear: Boosts Fire — avoid as it buffs Coalossal-like counters; less relevant for Sandaconda.
  • Snow/Hail: Boosts Ice — great if you plan to use Mamoswine/Glaceon.
  • Megas: Mega Swampert (if available) massively increases Water throughput; coordinate Megas only if they support your primary damage type.

Expert note: waiting a short time for Rain can convert a 4–6 player raid into a 2–3 player clear if you and at least one teammate have Kyogre.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What's the single best counter to Gigantamax Sandaconda?

A: Kyogre (Waterfall / Origin Pulse or Hydro Pump) is the top overall counter due to high sustained Water DPS and bulk. Hydro Cannon Swampert is the best budget option.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Sandaconda?

A: Soloing a Gigantamax boss is extremely difficult due to the enlarged HP pool. Only the most optimized players with multiple maxed legendaries, perfect movesets and the right weather boost might attempt it. Realistic clears are teams of 2–5 depending on roster.

Q: Should I TM in Hydro Cannon?

A: Yes — Hydro Cannon on Swampert is a high-value TM investment that turns many budget players into raid assets. Hydro Cannon is often the best single TM for mid-tier raiders.

Q: Which weather should I wait for?

A: Rain is ideal for Water teams. Snow is useful if you plan to rely on Ice counters like Mamoswine.

Q: Which Pokémon should I power first?

A: Prioritize Kyogre, then Swampert (with Hydro Cannon), then a secondary Ice or Grass attacker depending on your local raid pool and personal roster.

Moves & Threats

Sandaconda common fast moves are Mud Shot or Mud Slap. Charged moves include Wrap or Dig or Bulldoze and Earth Power. G-Max Sandblast not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

Snowy weather : boosts Ice moves. Windy weather : boosts Dragon Tail. Rainy weather : boosts Water moves. Sunny / Clear weather : boosts Sandaconda Ground moves.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Sandaconda?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Ground attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

Water-types dominate this raid thanks to their heavy Water DPS. Grass-types also perform strongly, especially in Sunny weather. Ice-types deal consistent damage and counter Dragon Tail sets.
