Gigantamax Gengar Overview

Gengar is already one of the most iconic Ghost/Poison-type Pokémon, but its Gigantamax form takes it to a completely different level of threat and design in Max Raid battles. Gigantamax Gengar is known for its terrifying appearance: a massive, shadowy body emerging from a purple vortex-like mouth on the ground, while Gengar itself hides beneath. It is one of the most aggressive and high-damage Dynamax raid bosses in Pokémon content.

Why Gigantamax Gengar Matters

Gigantamax Gengar is important because:

  • It is one of the strongest Ghost-type raid attackers
  • It can deal huge burst damage in Max Raid battles
  • It is often used in high-difficulty Gigantamax raids
  • It has strong relevance in PvE (raids/gyms) due to Ghost-type power

Typing

  • Type: Ghost / Poison
  • Weaknesses: Ghost, Dark
  • Resistances: Poison, Bug, Grass, Normal, Fairy, Fighting

Difficulty Level

  • Raid Tier: High (Gigantamax Tier)
  • Recommended Players:
    • 6–10 experienced trainers
    • 10–20 for safe clear in public groups
  • Difficulty Reason:

    • Dangerous AoE Ghost attacks

Dangerous Moves

Gigantamax Gengar commonly uses:

Best Counters (Dynamax / Gigantamax Focus)

Weather Boost — Gigantamax Gengar

Gengar Gigantamax raids become significantly more dangerous or easier depending on weather conditions because weather affects move damage, raid difficulty perception, and catch CP.

Best & Worst Weather for Gengar Raids

Fog Weather

  • Boosts Ghost + Dark-type moves

Impact:

  • Gengar’s Ghost moves (Shadow Ball) hit harder
  • Raid boss becomes more dangerous
  • Your Dark-type counters also deal more damage

Result:

  • Higher damage on BOTH sides → faster, riskier raids

Cloudy Weather

  • Boosts Fairy-type moves

Impact:

  • Slightly helps Fairy counters
  • Does not directly counter Gengar strongly

Result:

  • Mild advantage only

Sunny / Clear Weather

  • No direct boost to Ghost/Dark moves

Result:

  • More neutral and easier raid conditions overall

Rainy Weather

  • Boosts Water/Electric moves

Result:

  • Not very relevant for Gengar raids

Catch & Reward Effects

Weather also affects post-raid rewards:

  • Fog weather:
    • Higher CP catches
    • Stronger IV potential feel
  • Non-boosted weather:
    • Lower CP range
    • Easier catch consistency

Strategy Impact

If Fog Weather is active:

  • Bring stronger Dark-type teams
  • Expect higher incoming damage
  • Save shields for Shadow Ball cycles

If neutral weather:

  • Raids are more stable and predictable
  • Safer for mid-level teams

    Raid Strategy Guide — Gigantamax Gengar

    Gengar Gigantamax raids are not just about strong Pokémon — they are about timing, coordination, and survival management. If you play it correctly, even high-difficulty raids become very consistent.

    Team Composition Strategy

    Core Rule: Mix Damage + Survivability

    • 2–3 high DPS attackers
      • Gigantamax Grimmsnarl / Dynamax Arcanine-style counters
    • 1–2 tanky supporters
      • High bulk Pokémon to absorb hits
    • 1 flexible slot
      • Healing / backup attacker / shield support role

    Counter Selection Strategy

    Best focus type:

    • Dark-type Pokémon (priority)
    • Ghost-types only if strong and high level

    Avoid:

    • Fighting-types
    • Low CP glass cannons

    Move Awareness Strategy

    Key threat cycle:

    • Lick → fast energy gain
    • Shadow Ball → main KO move
    • Sludge Bomb → secondary pressure

    What to do:

    • Save shields for Shadow Ball moments
    • Heal immediately after heavy burst damage
    • Rotate fainted Pokémon smartly

    Shield & Survival Management

  • Don’t waste shields early
  • Use shields ONLY for:
    • Shadow Ball hits
    • Emergency survival situations

    Damage Phase Strategy

    • Attack aggressively when Gengar is not charging energy
    • Use burst damage windows after it uses charged moves
    • Sync team attacks to avoid staggered damage loss

    Weather Strategy

    • Fog: increases Ghost damage → dangerous
    • Cloudy: can help Fairy counters but less useful here

    Revive & Rotation Strategy

    • Don’t instantly re-enter with weak Pokémon
    • Rotate teams in waves:
      • Active team → backup team → revived team cycle
    • Avoid downtime DPS loss

    Group Coordination Tips

    • Assign roles:
      • DPS players
      • Support players
      • Shield callers

    Result:

    • Pokémon faint too quickly

    Poor Dark-type Usage

    • Or using underpowered versions of them

    Result:

    FAQ — Gigantamax Gengar

    Here are the most common questions about Gengar Gigantamax form and raid usage.

    Is Gigantamax Gengar hard to defeat?

    Yes. It is considered a high-difficulty Gigantamax raid boss. It deals heavy Ghost-type damage and requires coordinated counters, especially Dark-type attackers.

    What is the best counter for Gigantamax Gengar?

    The best counters are Dark-type Pokémon such as Gigantamax Grimmnsarl, Dynamax Arcanine. Ghost-types can also work but are riskier due to mirror damage.

    Can Gigantamax Gengar be shiny?

    Yes, but only during specific events. Its shiny form is white/pale lavender, making it highly desirable for collectors.

    Is Gigantamax Gengar good for raids?

    Yes. It is a top-tier Ghost-type attacker in PvE raids due to its high burst damage, but it is not very durable.

    Is Gigantamax Gengar useful in PvP?

    Not really. It is mainly built for raid (PvE) performance, not PvP meta dominance.

    When is Gigantamax Gengar available?

    It appears only during special limited-time events, such as Halloween events or Gigantamax re-run raid events. It is not permanently available.

    How many players are needed to beat it?

    • Minimum: 6–10 strong trainers
    • Safe clear: 10–20 players

    What makes Gigantamax Gengar different from normal Gengar?

    Gigantamax Gengar has:

    • Massive visual transformation
    • Special Max Battle effects
    • Higher raid threat level
    • Event-exclusive availability

    Is it worth powering up after catching?

    Yes, if you lack strong Ghost-type attackers. It is one of the best budget-friendly raid attackers in its type category.
