Overview

Gigantamax Kingler turns the classic crustacean into a towering pincered titan — G-Max Crustacean slams create huge water shockwaves and often add hazard-style effects (piercing armor or knockback) in Sword & Shield Max Raids. In Pokémon GO, expect massive HP and repeated heavy Water/STAB moves; Kingler can also carry surprising coverage like Rock Slide, X-Scissor or even Earthquake in special event variants.

Pro tip: Kingler's sheer physical strength makes counters that both exploit its Water typing and survive its heavy physical hits the best choices — think Electric and Grass attackers, but avoid pure Water attackers unless neutralizing coverage.

DPS, Energy & Dynamax / G-Max Notes

Pokémon GO: In G-Max raids, high EPS fast moves (Spark, Thunder Shock) combined with powerful Wild Charge / Thunderbolt windows beat single-shot glass cannons. Save charges for shieldless windows; coordinate one player to bait shields in small groups.

Sword & Shield: Gigantamax Kingler's G-Max move often adds battlefield control. Use Max Lightning / Max Geyser to capitalize on defensive openings. Supports that stall (Leech Seed, Leftovers) increase the time your DPS can output damage.

Pro tip: A single maxed Zekrom or high-CP Raikou will reduce required players far more than several mediocre attackers. Protect that asset during the raid.

Step-by-step Strategy

  1. Preview charged moves: Lock Electric if Crabhammer/Liquidation appears; lock Grass if you see Hydro Pump + Rock Slide mix.
  2. Lead for EPS: Use fast Electric leads (Spark / Thunder Shock) to generate charges early.
  3. Shield economy (GO): Bait medium charges and save one shield for your highest DPS Electric attacker.
  4. Swap & tank: Rotate bulky neutrals to soak Rock Slide then swap back to Electric DPS.
  5. Dynamax timing (SWSH): Use Max Lightning / Max Geyser to break explosive defensive windows and set up huge team damage.
  6. Revive policy: Stagger revives and rejoin during safe windows to avoid faint waves and wasted revives.

Assign one teammate to preserve a shield (GO) or to hold a Max Move (SWSH) for the final 20–30% HP window — that saved resource commonly turns close attempts into wins.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What's the single best counter to Gigantamax Kingler?

A: Zekrom (Charge Beam + Wild Charge) or Raikou (Thunder Shock + Wild Charge) are the best universal Electric counters in GO. In SWSH, Zekrom / Thundurus + Rillaboom/Ferrothorn form the cleanest core.

Q: Can I solo Gigantamax Kingler?

A: Soloing is highly unlikely due to the huge HP pool of Gigantamax bosses. Only extremely optimized trainers with multiple maxed legendaries and perfect weather/dynamax windows might attempt it. Realistically, plan for 2–5 players depending on roster quality.

Q: Which TMs should I prioritize?

A: Wild Charge / Thunderbolt for Electric attackers, Frenzy Plant for Grass attackers (if available), and Earthquake / Rock Slide for backup coverage against special raid variants.

Q: Should I wait for weather?

A: Sunny (for Grass) helps but is not essential; roster quality beats weather. If you can wait for Sunny to buff Venusaur/Frenzy Plant then small-group clears become easier, otherwise proceed with your Electric core.

Moves & Threats

Kingler common fast moves are Metal Claw or Bubble. Charged moves include Vise Grip or X-Scissor or Water Pulse or Crabhammer and Razor Shell. Elite moves include Mud Shot. G-Max Foam Burst not only delivers heavy damage but also leaves a damaging area for a short time — timing your swaps and using at least one bulky support reduces wipe risk.

CP & IV (Catch)

  • Perfect (L20): 1,540-1,616 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 1,925-2,020 CP
  • Boosted by: Rainy

Use Pinap on poor IVs; switch to Golden Razz on 96%+ or Shiny.

Weather Effects & Battle Flow

Rainy wether : boosts Water damage. Sunny weather : boosts Grass attackers.

Gigantamax Kingler's Crabhammer hits very hard — keep at least one high-survivability Electric or Grass attacker to finish the raid if your main DPS faint.

Battle Tips

  • Use Electric or Grass teams (depending on availability) and stack DPS early — front-loading shortens the timer pressure.
  • Keep a Blissey/Lapras or other high-TDO slot to soak Max Moves in large lobbies.
  • Avoid using many Water/Fire/Steel attackers — they’re resisted and lower effective DPS.
  • Star/favorite teams to reduce relobby time and coordinate Mega leads if your group agrees to use them.

Team Size

Recommended: 15–20 trainers for comfortable clears with mixed player levels. With many Gigantamax / Dynamax counters, 10–14 can work. Under 8 trainers should be attempted only if all participants are high-level and coordinated with ideal counters and Dynamax/G-Max boosts.

Why Raid Gigantamax Kingler?

Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Water attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

When To Avoid

Avoid using Water or Ground attackers — they deal little damage and faint quickly to Kingler’s charged moves.

Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

If you lack top attackers, budget Electric Pokémon also perform well. If Kingler uses Crabhammer or Hydro Pump, conserve shields for your highest-DPS Electric attackers. After winning, use Golden Razz for your best chance to catch a high-IV Gigantamax Kingler. Silver Pinap is useful when farming Candy.

Conclusion

Gigantamax Kingler is a mechanically interesting raid boss: its Water type is predictable, but the G-Max residual damage forces teams to plan for survivability, not just pure DPS. Prioritize Electric and Grass Gigantamax picks, add Dynamax support for sustained windows, and include at least one high-TDO tank if your lobby is smaller. With the counters and tactics above, you’ll clear Kingler efficiently and maximize your rewards.
