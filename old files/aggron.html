Mega Abomasnow Raid Guide – Pokémon GO

Counters, Weaknesses, Best Teams & Strategy

Introduction

Mega Abomasnow is one of the most intimidating Mega Pokémon in Pokémon GO, featuring a sleek design and fierce Grass / Ice typing. Known as the “Frost Tree Pokémon,” Abomasnow already has a reputation as a fast but fragile attacker. With Mega Evolution, its power skyrockets, making it a glass cannon that can shred opponents quickly but also falls just as fast.

This guide will cover everything you need to know about defeating Mega Abomasnow in raids: weaknesses, best counters, ideal team setups, weather boosts, duo/trio possibilities, and even tips for maximizing rewards.

Moves & Threats

Cobalion’s common fast moves are Metal Claw and Double Kick. Charged moves include Iron Head, Close Combat, and Stone Edge.

  • Iron Head hits Fairy/Rock hard but is resisted by Fire and Steel.
  • Close Combat punishes Rock/Dark counters—favour Fire/Ghost here.
  • Stone Edge threatens Fire/Flying—avoid Charizard if it’s present.

Weather Effects

  • Rain: Buffs Water—watch for big Hydro Pump; also boosts Electric damage.
  • Cloudy: Buffs your Fighting attackers.
  • Wind: Boosts the caught Gyarados (Water/Flying) and Dragon moves.

    • The Pokémon you catch is regular Gyarados; weather boost depends on its Water/Flying typing.

    Practical battle tips

    Battle Tips

    • Prioritize Electric teams; swap to Grass when Rain powers up both sides.
    • Run a matching Mega for teamwide damage bonuses and extra candies.
    • Charm/Fairy options are safe when Crunch is common and Outrage is absent.
    • Dodge one nuke (Hydro Pump/Outrage) to keep shadows alive and sustain DPS.

    Duo & Trio Possibility

    A duo is possible with maxed-out counters and coordination. A trio is comfortable for mid-level players. Casual groups should bring 4–5 trainers.

    Rewards

    • Mega Energy for Abomasnow

    Why Raid Mega Abomasnow?

    Reasons include Pokédex entry, strong Grass / Ice attacker, shiny chance, and XL Candy farming.

    PvP Relevance

    While Abomasnow isn’t top tier in PvP, it’s a fun spice pick. Mega Abomasnow shines more in raids and PvE content.

    FAQ

    Can Mega Aggron be shiny in Pokémon GO?

    Yes, shiny Mega Aggron is available. You need a shiny Aggron first, then Mega Evolve it

    What are best moveset of Aggron?

    Best movset of Aggron are Iron Tail or Dragon Tail which does damage of 14 under Fast Attack and Meteor Beam of 140 under Charged Attack.

    How do I get Mega Energy for Aggron?

    You can earn Mega Energy by defeating Mega Aggron in raids or walking with Aggron if already Mega Evolved once.

    What is the best weather to raid Mega Aggron?

    Sunny weather boosts Fighting and Ground counters, Rainy weather boosts Water counters, Ice weather boosts Psychic counters.

    Are Shadow Pokémon better?

    Yes—Shadow forms have +20% attack, making them excellent choices if available.

    Conclusion

    Mega Abomasnow is not the strongest Mega, but it’s easy to raid thanks to multiple weaknesses. It’s worth raiding for its shiny form, Mega Energy, and for filling your Mega Pokédex. With Electric, Fighting, and Grass counters, you’ll defeat it with ease.
