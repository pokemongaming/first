About Gigantamax Centiskorch

Gigantamax Centiskorch is a Fire/Bug-type raid boss introduced in Pokémon GO during limited-time Gigantamax events. Its massive fiery form makes it an exciting encounter. As a Fire/Bug Pokémon, it is weak to Flying, Rock, and Water moves, giving trainers several effective options to counter it.

Moves & Threats

Centiskorch common fast moves are Bug Bite or Ember. Charged moves include Bug Buzz or Lunge or Heat Wave and Crunch.

Weather Effects

  • Rainy: Boosts Water counters.
  • Partly Cloudy: Boosts Rock counters.
  • Sunny: Boosts Centiskorch Fire moves.

    • Team Size

    15-20 trainers are required.

    Recommended items

    • Max Revives
    • Max Potions
    • Razz / Golden Razz for catch

    Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

    Use Rock and Water attackers to exploit Centiskorch’s weaknesses. Avoid Grass or Steel Pokémon as they take heavy damage from Centiskorch’s Fire-type attacks.

    FAQ

    What is Gigantamax Centiskorch type and weakness?

    Centiskorch is a Fire / Bug type. It is weak to Rock, Water, Flying.

    What are good counters for G-Max Centiferno?

    Strong rock, flying, water attackers such as Gigantamax Charizard, Gigantamax Blastoise, Gigantamax Inteleon, Gigantamax Cinderace, Dynamax Suicune, Eternatus.

    Can Gigantamax Centiskorch be shiny?

    Yes, but in certain events.

    Is Gigantamax Centiskorch catchable after the raid?

    Yes — standard raid catch mechanics apply.

    Does weather change move sets?

    Sunny weather boosts Centiskorch’s Fire moves, while Rainy weather boosts Water counters. Partly Cloudy weather supports Rock-type counters.

    Does Blastoise keep G-Max Centiferno?

    No — it’s exclusive to Gigantamax raids.

    Is Gigantamax Centiskorch good in Pokémon GO?

    Gigantamax Centiskorch is primarily a collector’s raid boss. While it has fun visuals and decent typing, it doesn’t outperform top Fire or Bug attackers in PvE battles.
