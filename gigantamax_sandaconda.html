About Gigantamax Sandaconda

Gigantamax Sandaconda is a Ground-type raid boss in Pokémon GO, available during Gigantamax events. Its coiled tornado-like body is unique, and its G-Max Sandblast move makes it dangerous in battle. Sandaconda is weak to Water, Ice, and Grass-type Pokémon, offering several powerful counter options.

Moves & Threats

Sandaconda common fast moves are Mud Shot or Mud Slap. Charged moves include Wrap or Dig or Bulldoze and Earth Power.

Weather Effects

  • Snowy: Boosts Ice moves.
  • Windy: Boosts Dragon Tail.
  • Rainy: Boosts Water moves.
  • Sunny / Clear: Boosts Sandaconda Ground moves.

    • Team Size

    15-20 trainers are required.

    Recommended items

    • Max Revives
    • Max Potions
    • Razz / Golden Razz for catch

    Raid Strategy & Catch Tips

    Water-types dominate this raid thanks to their heavy Water DPS. Grass-types also perform strongly, especially in Sunny weather. Ice-types deal consistent damage and counter Dragon Tail sets.

    FAQ

    What is Gigantamax Sandaconda type and weakness?

    Sandaconda is a Ground type. It is weak to Water, Ice, Grass.

    What are good counters for G-Max Sandblast?

    Strong ice and grass attackers such as Gigantamax Rillaboom, Gigantamax Lapras, Gigantamax Charizard, Gigantamax Venusaur, Dynamax Articuno, Dynamax Rillaboom.

    Can Gigantamax Sandaconda be shiny?

    Yes, but in certain events.

    Is Gigantamax Sandaconda catchable after the raid?

    Yes — standard raid catch mechanics apply.

    Does weather change move sets?

    Sunny/Clear weather boosts Sandaconda’s Ground-type moves, while Windy boosts Dragon Tail. Rainy weather strengthens Water counters, and Snowy weather benefits Ice-types.

    Does Sandaconda keep G-Max Sandblast?

    No — it’s exclusive to Gigantamax raids.
