Nihilego Raid Guide — Counters, Weakness & Strategy

Your complete resource for defeating Nihilego in Pokémon GO raids.

Rock Poison

Nihilego is an Ultra Beast Rock/Poison-type raid boss in Pokémon GO, known for its extremely high Special Attack but very low Defense and Stamina. While it can deal massive damage if ignored, it is also one of the frailest Tier 5 raid bosses, meaning prepared trainers can defeat it very quickly with the right counters. This guide explains the best Nihilego counters, battle strategy, weather boosts, and example teams for every type of player.

CP Ranges

Understanding CP ranges helps trainers evaluate the strength of Nihilego when caught from raids.

  • Perfect (L20): 2,167-2,256 CP
  • Weather Boost (L25): 2,709-2,821 CP
  • Boosted by: Partly Cloudy & Cloudy

Keep in mind that weather-boosted Nihilego is significantly harder to defeat, so always prepare strong Ice, Dragon, Fairy, or Ground-type counters.

Why Nihilego Is Easier Than Most Tier 5 Raids

Although Nihilego can hit hard with moves like Sludge Bomb, Thunderbolt, or Rock Slide, its Defense and HP are very low for a legendary-tier boss. This makes Nihilego a “glass cannon” raid: it deals damage, but it also goes down extremely fast when attacked with proper counters.

With top Ground-type counters, even 2–3 high-level players can defeat Nihilego comfortably.

Weather Effects

  • Sunny / Clear: Boosts Ground-type moves (BEST weather for this raid)
  • Partly Cloudy: Boosts Nihilego’s Rock-type moves
  • Cloudy: Boosts Nihilego’s Poison-type moves

In Sunny weather, Ground attackers become even more powerful and Nihilego will melt extremely fast. However, in Cloudy or Partly Cloudy weather, Nihilego becomes more dangerous and can knock out your team faster.

Revive & Potion Cost

  • Average revives used: 8–14
  • Average potions used: 10–18
  • Dodging significantly reduces resource usage
  • Using Mega Pokémon reduces total relobbies

Common Raid Mistakes

  • Using Rock or Fairy Pokémon (they are resisted)
  • Ignoring Ground-type attackers
  • Over-focusing on tanky Pokémon instead of high DPS
  • Not reviving and rejoining quickly

Best Team Compositions

Small Group (3–4 Trainers)

With strong Ice, Dragon, or Ground-type counters and at least one Mega Pokémon, Nihilego can be defeated by 3 to 4 high-level trainers.

Medium Group (5–7 Trainers)

A group of 5 to 7 trainers with decent counters should have no trouble defeating Nihilego.

Large Group (8+ Trainers)

Nihilego becomes very easy with a large group, but avoid overfilling the lobby to save raid passes.

Raid Strategy

    Start the battle with your strongest Ground-type attackers and maintain constant pressure. Because Nihilego is very fragile, the key is to maximize DPS rather than focus too much on survival.

    If Nihilego has Sludge Bomb or Thunderbolt, some Pokémon may faint quickly. Don’t worry — just rejoin and continue attacking. In most cases, the raid will still end very fast.

Yes, Shiny Nihilego is available during certain events.

What are best moveset of Nihilego?

Conclusion

Nihilego is a glass-cannon raid boss that can be defeated very quickly with the right Ground-type counters. If you build your team properly and take advantage of weather boosts, this raid is one of the easiest and fastest Tier 5 raids in Pokémon GO.
